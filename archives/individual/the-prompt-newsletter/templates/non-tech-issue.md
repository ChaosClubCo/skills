# Non-Tech Edition Template — Weekly

Use this exact section structure for every Non-Tech weekly edition. Do not skip sections.
Every hidden gem feature must appear in its designated position.

---

## Header Block

```
> THE PROMPT
by INT · Your weekly AI intelligence brief
Week [N] — [Full Date, e.g., March 10, 2026]
What happened in AI this week — and what it means for INT Inc.
⏱ Reading time: ~2 minutes
```

In HTML email: render with Eclipse (`#00405F`) background, orange `>` symbol, white title,
blinking cursor (static in email — use a solid orange block). Tier badge: ALL STAFF.
In DOCX: "THE PROMPT" as H1, orange rule below, subtitle in Deep Space blue.
In Teams: `# > THE PROMPT — Week [N] | All Staff`

---

## 📊 Stat of the Week

Single compelling statistic in a callout box with Meteor orange left border. Plain English
framing with a real-world comparison. Source URL required.

**Example:**
> **Stat of the Week:** 900 tech workers across 6 companies signed open letters supporting
> Anthropic's Pentagon refusal — the largest cross-company solidarity action in AI history.
> *Source: [Publication](URL)*

## 🔁 Last Week We Said...

2–3 sentence callback to a specific claim or prediction from last week. Connect to this
week's developments. This is the trust-building feature. Source from the continuity log.

**Example:**
> **Last Week We Said...** Claude was the #1 app in the App Store after the Pentagon dispute.
> This week: downloads have stabilized, but Anthropic reports paid subscribers are still
> growing at 3x their pre-dispute rate.

## Section 1: The Big Picture (~50 words)

1–2 sentences. The entire newsletter in miniature. A leader who reads only this walks away
informed. "This week, [major thing] happened. For INT, it means [specific implication]."

## Section 2: What Changed (~250 words)

2–3 developments in plain English. For each:
- **What happened** (1–2 sentences, zero jargon)
- **Why it matters to INT** (1 sentence)
- **What you need to do** (usually "Nothing" — but say it explicitly)

**Writing rules:**
- No acronyms without definition at first use
- Every statistic translated to plain English with comparison
- No benchmark names — translate to outcomes ("performs better at coding tasks")
- Analogies welcome ("Think of it as...")
- Every claim has a source URL

## 💬 Shareable One-Liner

Place after Section 2. Boxed, slightly larger font. Standalone — works without context.

**Example:**
> **Quote of the week:** "The AI company that said no to the Pentagon became the #1 app
> in America. Safety isn't a cost — it's a growth strategy."

## Section 3: Client Talking Points (~100 words)

2–3 Q&A pairs for client conversations.

> **If a client asks: "[likely question]"**
> Here's what to say: "[confident, defensible response]"

Rules: Never speculate. Every claim sourced. If uncertain, say "We're monitoring this
closely and will update you if anything changes." Check continuity log for consistency.

## 💡 Did You Know?

1–2 sentence surprising AI fact. Light tone. Palate cleanser between sections.

**Example:**
> **Did You Know?** Claude can now process the equivalent of about 2,000 pages of text in
> a single conversation — roughly the complete works of Shakespeare, twice.

## Section 4: What INT Is Doing (~50 words)

1–2 bullets on actions Kyle/team are taking. Makes INT feel proactive, not reactive.

## Section 5: Coming Up (~50 words)

1 sentence preview of next week. Creates anticipation and a reason to open the next edition.

## 🎓 I Wish a Veteran Told Me (NON-TECH TIER ONLY)

Practical beginner tip for using AI tools. Friendly advice from a senior colleague.
Voice: "When [situation], try [specific action]. It saves [time/effort] because [reason]."

**Example:**
> **I Wish a Veteran Told Me:** When Claude gives you a long answer, you can say
> "Summarize that in 3 bullet points" and it will. You don't have to read everything
> it generates — you're the editor, not the reader.

---

## 🎁 Free This Week  ← MANDATORY

A copy-paste AI prompt for a real work task tied to this week's top story. One per issue.
Never skip this section. It is the highest-retention feature in The Prompt.

**Format:** Green "FREE" badge + the deliverable in a copyable box.

**Example:**
> 🎁 **Free This Week:** This week's news is about [topic]. Paste this into Claude to
> [specific outcome]:
>
> *"You are an expert in [domain]. I'm going to describe a situation from [context].
> Your job is to [task]. Here's the situation: [placeholder]. Give me [output format]."*
>
> *(Swap in your own details — works in Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini)*

**Rules:**
- Must be tied to this week's lead story
- Must be immediately usable by a non-technical person
- Include a note on which AI tools it works with
- Log in continuity file to maintain variety week to week

## Word Count Target

## Voice Checklist (self-audit before output)

- [ ] Could my grandmother follow this? (If not, simplify)
- [ ] Are there any undefined acronyms?
- [ ] Is every stat in plain English with a comparison?
- [ ] Do the client talking points sound natural spoken aloud?
- [ ] Is there anything that would make a non-technical reader feel stupid? (Remove it)
- [ ] Does "What INT Is Doing" make the company sound proactive?
- [ ] Is the reading time estimate accurate?
- [ ] Is the Free This Week prompt genuinely usable, not generic?
- [ ] Does the header say "The Prompt by INT" — not "LLM Weekly" or "State of LLMs"?

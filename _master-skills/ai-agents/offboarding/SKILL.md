# Employee Offboarding Management

## Overview

The Offboarding skill provides comprehensive guidance for managing employee separations professionally, legally, and efficiently. Effective offboarding protects organizational knowledge, ensures compliance with employment laws, maintains positive relationships with departing employees, and secures company assets and information.

Offboarding encompasses all separation types and serves multiple organizational objectives:
- **Knowledge Preservation**: Capturing critical information before departure
- **Legal Compliance**: Meeting final pay, benefits, and documentation requirements
- **Security Protection**: Revoking access and recovering company assets
- **Relationship Maintenance**: Creating potential brand ambassadors and boomerang employees
- **Operational Continuity**: Ensuring smooth transitions and minimal disruption

### Separation Types

### Offboarding Phases

**Phase 1: Notification**
- Resignation acceptance
- Termination communication
- Documentation initiation
- Timeline establishment

**Phase 2: Transition Planning**
- Knowledge transfer scheduling
- Project handoff planning
- Client/stakeholder notification
- Temporary coverage arrangements

**Phase 3: Administrative Processing**
- Final pay calculation
- Benefits continuation setup
- Access revocation planning
- Asset return coordination

**Phase 4: Exit Activities**
- Exit interview
- Knowledge documentation
- Equipment collection
- Badge and access deactivation

**Phase 5: Post-Separation**
- Final pay distribution
- COBRA notification
- Reference request handling
- Alumni program enrollment

## When to Use

### Primary Use Cases

**Voluntary Separations**
- Employee resignation processing
- Retirement transitions
- Internal transfer offboarding
- Contract completion

**Involuntary Separations**
- Performance-based terminations
- Policy violation terminations
- Position eliminations
- Reduction in force events

**Special Circumstances**
- Executive departures
- Key person transitions
- Merger/acquisition integrations
- Remote employee offboarding

### Trigger Scenarios

| Scenario | Immediate Actions |
|----------|-------------------|
| Resignation received | Accept, confirm last day, initiate checklist |
| Termination decision | Legal review, documentation, timeline |
| Retirement notice | Transition planning, phased departure options |
| RIF announced | Notification scheduling, severance preparation |
| Abandonment suspected | Contact attempts, documentation, policy review |
| Death of employee | Sensitive handling, beneficiary notification |

### Timing Considerations

**Standard Resignation**
- Two weeks notice typical
- Extended notice for senior roles (30-90 days)
- Garden leave considerations
- Immediate departure scenarios

**Involuntary Termination**
- Same-day processing common
- WARN Act compliance (60 days for mass layoffs)
- Final pay timing (varies by state)
- Benefits continuation deadlines

**Retirement**
- 30-90 day notice encouraged
- Phased retirement options
- Knowledge transfer priority
- Celebration planning

## Core Processes

### Process 1: Voluntary Resignation Handling

**Day 1: Resignation Receipt**

```
Step 1: Resignation Documentation
        - Receive written resignation
        - Confirm last day of employment
        - Document reason for leaving
        - Assess retention possibility

Step 2: Initial Notifications
        - Notify HR operations
        - Alert IT for access planning
        - Inform payroll
        - Schedule manager meeting

Step 3: Departure Planning
        - Determine notice period
        - Plan knowledge transfer
        - Identify transition needs
        - Schedule exit interview
```

**Notice Period Activities**

```
Week 1:
- Project inventory and handoff planning
- Documentation of key processes
- Client/stakeholder notification plan
- Replacement hiring initiated

Week 2 (Final Week):
- Knowledge transfer completion
- Equipment return
- Access revocation timing
- Exit interview conducted
- Farewell activities
- Final administrative processing
```

### Process 2: Involuntary Termination Processing

**Pre-Termination Preparation**

```
1. Documentation Review
   └── Performance records
   └── Policy violation evidence
   └── Prior warnings/PIPs
   └── Investigation results

2. Legal Review
   └── Employment attorney consultation
   └── Risk assessment
   └── Discrimination analysis
   └── Contractual obligations

3. Logistics Planning
   └── Meeting scheduling
   └── Security notification
   └── IT access preparation
   └── Final pay calculation

4. Communication Preparation
   └── Termination script
   └── Q&A preparation
   └── Severance details (if applicable)
   └── Benefits information
```

**Termination Meeting Protocol**

```
TERMINATION MEETING CHECKLIST
-----------------------------
Attendees:
[ ] Direct manager
[ ] HR representative
[ ] Security (if needed)

Environment:
[ ] Private meeting room
[ ] End of day timing preferred
[ ] Tissues available
[ ] Exit pathway planned

Documents Ready:
[ ] Termination letter
[ ] Final pay information
[ ] Benefits continuation details
[ ] COBRA notice
[ ] Severance agreement (if applicable)
[ ] Non-disclosure reminders
[ ] Equipment return list

Meeting Flow:
1. Direct statement of termination (30 seconds)
2. Brief explanation of reason
3. Review of final pay and benefits
4. Provide documentation package
5. Explain next steps
6. Collect company property
7. Escort from building

Do NOT:
- Debate the decision
- Make promises
- Apologize excessively
- Engage in extended discussion
- Leave employee unattended
```

### Process 5: Final Pay Processing

**Final Pay Components**

```
FINAL PAYCHECK CALCULATION
==========================

EARNINGS:
+ Regular wages through last day
+ Accrued PTO payout (per policy/law)
+ Prorated bonus (if applicable)
+ Commission (earned, not speculative)
+ Expense reimbursements
+ Severance (if applicable)

DEDUCTIONS:
- Standard tax withholdings
- Benefits premiums (final)
- 401(k) contributions
- Outstanding advances
- Unreturned equipment value
- Signed authorization required

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- State final pay timing laws
- Direct deposit vs. check
- Multi-state employees
- Commission reconciliation
- Equity vesting implications
```

**State Final Pay Requirements**

```
FINAL PAY TIMING BY STATE (Sample)
==================================

Resignation (Voluntary):
- California: 72 hours (or immediately with notice)
- Illinois: Next regular payday
- New York: Next regular payday
- Texas: Next regular payday

Note: Always verify current state requirements
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Delayed Access Revocation

**Problem**: Access remains active after employee departs, creating security risk.

**Consequences**:
- Data breach potential
- Intellectual property theft
- Compliance violations
- System manipulation

**Prevention**:
- Automated termination workflows
- Real-time IT notification
- Audit of access post-departure
- Integration with HRIS termination

### Pitfall 2: Final Pay Violations

**Problem**: Final paycheck not delivered within state-required timeframe.

**Consequences**:
- Statutory penalties
- Waiting time penalties
- Legal claims
- Employee relations damage

**Prevention**:
- State requirement reference guide
- Automated final pay processing
- Compliance calendar alerts
- Legal review of practices

### Pitfall 3: Inadequate Knowledge Transfer

**Problem**: Critical knowledge leaves with employee.

**Consequences**:
- Operational disruption
- Lost client relationships
- Repeated mistakes
- Extended learning curves

**Prevention**:
- Structured knowledge transfer process
- Documentation requirements
- Overlap periods for key roles
- Knowledge management systems

### Pitfall 4: COBRA Notice Failures

**Problem**: COBRA notices not sent or sent late.

**Consequences**:
- Excise taxes ($110/day)
- Extended election periods
- Potential lawsuits
- Regulatory penalties

**Prevention**:
- Automated notice generation
- Tracking system for deadlines
- Vendor management
- Regular compliance audits

### Pitfall 5: Poorly Conducted Exit Interviews

**Problem**: Exit interviews yield no actionable insights.

**Consequences**:
- Missed improvement opportunities
- Continued turnover patterns
- Employee disengagement
- Wasted administrative time

**Prevention**:
- Trained interviewers
- Standardized questions
- Confidentiality assurance
- Regular analysis of trends

### Pitfall 6: Equipment Recovery Gaps

**Problem**: Company equipment not returned.

**Consequences**:
- Financial loss
- Data on devices
- License compliance issues
- Inventory inaccuracy

**Prevention**:
- Clear equipment tracking
- Return shipping for remote workers
- Final pay hold policies (where legal)
- Recovery escalation process

# Performance Management

## Overview

Performance management is the continuous process of setting expectations, monitoring progress, providing feedback, and developing employee capabilities to achieve organizational goals. Effective performance management aligns individual contributions with business objectives while supporting employee growth.

For SMB business solutions, a well-designed performance management system drives accountability, identifies top talent, addresses performance issues early, informs compensation decisions, and creates a culture of continuous improvement.

## When to Use

Invoke this skill when:
- Designing or revamping performance review cycles
- Creating goal-setting frameworks (OKRs, KPIs)
- Training managers on performance conversations
- Conducting calibration sessions
- Addressing underperformance issues
- Creating Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)
- Implementing continuous feedback systems
- Linking performance to compensation
- Building development planning processes
- Analyzing performance data and trends
- Preparing for promotion decisions

### 2. Goal Setting Framework

#### OKR Structure

```
OKR FRAMEWORK

OBJECTIVE (What)
- Qualitative and inspirational
- Time-bound (usually quarterly)
- Ambitious but achievable
- 3-5 per person/team

KEY RESULTS (How)
- Quantitative and measurable
- 3-5 per objective
- Outcome-focused (not tasks)
- Binary: achieved or not

EXAMPLE:
Objective: Become the market leader in customer satisfaction

Key Results:
1. Increase NPS from 45 to 65
2. Reduce customer churn from 8% to 5%
3. Achieve 95% first-call resolution rate
4. Grow customer testimonials from 20 to 50

SCORING (0.0 - 1.0)
- 0.7-1.0: Delivered
- 0.4-0.6: Made progress
- 0.0-0.3: Failed to make progress

Target: 0.7 average (stretch goals)
```

#### SMART Goals Template

```
SMART GOAL WORKSHEET

GOAL STATEMENT:
_________________________________________________

S - SPECIFIC
What exactly will be accomplished?
Who is involved?
Where will this happen?

M - MEASURABLE
How will you measure success?
What metrics/KPIs apply?
What is the target number?

A - ACHIEVABLE
Do you have the resources needed?
Is this realistic given constraints?
What support is required?

R - RELEVANT
Why does this matter?
How does it align to team/company goals?
What is the business impact?

T - TIME-BOUND
What is the deadline?
What are the milestones?
How will you track progress?

FINAL GOAL:
By [DATE], [WHO] will [ACTION] [WHAT]
as measured by [METRIC] from [BASELINE] to [TARGET].
```

#### Goal Cascade Model

```
GOAL ALIGNMENT CASCADE

COMPANY LEVEL
Mission/Vision/Strategy
         |
         v
DEPARTMENT LEVEL
Team Objectives (support company goals)
         |
         v
INDIVIDUAL LEVEL
Personal Goals (support team objectives)

ALIGNMENT CHECK
For each individual goal, answer:
1. Which team objective does this support?
2. Which company priority does this connect to?
3. How will success be measured?
4. What is the expected business impact?

If no clear connection, reconsider the goal.
```

### 3. Performance Review Process

#### Review Components

```
PERFORMANCE REVIEW STRUCTURE

SECTION 1: GOAL ACHIEVEMENT (40%)
- Review of established goals
- Rating per goal (1-5 scale)
- Evidence and results
- Weighted average score

SECTION 2: COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT (30%)
- Core competencies (company values)
- Role-specific competencies
- Leadership competencies (if applicable)
- Rating per competency

SECTION 3: OVERALL CONTRIBUTION (20%)
- Impact beyond formal goals
- Initiative and ownership
- Collaboration effectiveness
- Problem-solving examples

SECTION 4: DEVELOPMENT (10%)
- Skills development progress
- Learning achievements
- Career growth
- Areas for improvement

OVERALL RATING
Weighted calculation or calibrated rating
```

#### Rating Scale Definition

```
5-POINT RATING SCALE

5 - EXCEPTIONAL
- Consistently exceeds all expectations
- Role model for others
- Exceptional impact on results
- Top 5% of performers
- Ready for promotion

4 - EXCEEDS EXPECTATIONS
- Frequently exceeds expectations
- Strong contributor
- Positive impact on team
- Top 20% of performers
- High potential

3 - MEETS EXPECTATIONS
- Consistently meets all expectations
- Solid, reliable performer
- Delivers quality work
- Core of the organization
- Performing at level

2 - NEEDS IMPROVEMENT
- Inconsistently meets expectations
- Performance gaps identified
- Development required
- Action plan needed
- At risk

1 - UNSATISFACTORY
- Does not meet expectations
- Significant performance issues
- Immediate intervention required
- PIP or separation likely
- Bottom 5%

DISTRIBUTION GUIDANCE
5: 5-10%
4: 20-25%
3: 50-60%
2: 10-15%
1: 0-5%
```

### 4. Calibration Process

#### Calibration Meeting Structure

```
CALIBRATION SESSION GUIDE

PURPOSE
- Ensure rating consistency across managers
- Identify high potentials and at-risk employees
- Validate promotion readiness
- Inform compensation decisions

PREPARATION
Managers bring:
- Proposed ratings for all direct reports
- Performance evidence for each
- Calibration grid placement
- Promotion/development recommendations

SESSION STRUCTURE (2-3 hours per 20-30 employees)

1. GROUND RULES (10 min)
   - Confidentiality
   - Evidence-based discussion
   - Rating definitions review

2. INITIAL PLACEMENT (20 min)
   - Place all employees on grid
   - Manager presents each briefly

3. CALIBRATION DISCUSSION (90 min)
   - Review each rating band
   - Challenge and discuss outliers
   - Adjust based on evidence
   - Ensure distribution alignment

4. HIGH PERFORMER DISCUSSION (20 min)
   - Validate top ratings
   - Discuss retention risk
   - Identify promotion candidates

5. NEEDS IMPROVEMENT DISCUSSION (20 min)
   - Review all 2s and 1s
   - Confirm PIP candidates
   - Discuss support needed

6. FINALIZE RATINGS (15 min)
   - Lock in final ratings
   - Document decisions
   - Assign follow-up actions
```

#### 9-Box Grid Assessment

```
9-BOX TALENT GRID

Performance (X-axis) vs Potential (Y-axis)

        | Low Potential | Medium Potential | High Potential
--------|---------------|------------------|----------------

--------|---------------|------------------|----------------

Perf    | (Coach)      | Contributor      | Potential
--------|---------------|------------------|----------------
Low     | Talent       | Growth           | Inconsistent

BOX DEFINITIONS & ACTIONS

CORE CONTRIBUTOR (Med/Med): Maintain, steady development
DILEMMA (Med/Low): Assess fit, consider move
INCONSISTENT (Low/High): Coach intensively, unlock potential
GROWTH EMPLOYEE (Low/Med): Develop skills, set expectations
TALENT RISK (Low/Low): Performance plan or exit
```

### 5. Performance Conversations

#### Manager Conversation Guide

```
PERFORMANCE REVIEW MEETING GUIDE

PREPARATION (Before meeting)
- Review self-assessment
- Gather performance data
- Prepare specific examples
- Draft feedback points
- Plan development discussion

MEETING STRUCTURE (60 minutes)

OPENING (5 min)
- Set collaborative tone
- Review agenda
- Employee shares perspective first

ACHIEVEMENT REVIEW (20 min)
- Walk through each goal
- Share evidence and examples
- Discuss ratings
- Acknowledge successes
- Address gaps

COMPETENCY DISCUSSION (15 min)
- Review competency ratings
- Provide specific feedback
- Discuss behavioral examples
- Connect to effectiveness

DEVELOPMENT PLANNING (15 min)
- Career aspirations
- Skills to develop
- Training/experiences needed
- Create action plan

CLOSING (5 min)
- Summarize key points
- Confirm next steps
- Express appreciation
- Schedule follow-up

AFTER MEETING
- Document discussion
- Update development plan
- Send follow-up summary
- Schedule regular check-ins
```

#### Feedback Models

```
SBI FEEDBACK MODEL

SITUATION
Where and when did it happen?
"In yesterday's team meeting..."

BEHAVIOR
What did the person do? (Observable)
"You interrupted several colleagues..."

IMPACT
What was the effect?
"Which made them hesitant to share ideas."

[Optional] DESIRED BEHAVIOR
What should happen instead?
"Next time, wait for them to finish."

---

COIN FEEDBACK MODEL

CONNECTION
Build rapport first
"I appreciate your dedication to the project."

OBSERVATION
State what you observed
"I noticed the deadline was missed."

IMPACT
Explain the effect
"This delayed the client deliverable."

NEXT STEPS
Agree on action
"Let's discuss how to prevent this."
```

#### Underperformance Timeline

```
PERFORMANCE ISSUE ESCALATION

STAGE 1: VERBAL COACHING (Weeks 1-4)
- Document concerns
- Have direct conversation
- Set clear expectations
- Provide support and feedback
- Check in weekly

If not improved after 30 days...

STAGE 2: WRITTEN WARNING (Weeks 5-8)
- Formal written documentation
- Specific improvement required
- Timeline for improvement
- Consequences stated
- HR notified

If not improved after 30 days...

STAGE 3: FORMAL PIP (Weeks 9-16)
- Structured improvement plan
- Measurable objectives
- Regular check-ins
- HR involved throughout
- 30-90 day duration

If PIP objectives not met...

STAGE 4: TERMINATION
- Document all steps taken
- HR and legal review
- Separation meeting
- Exit paperwork

DOCUMENTATION AT EACH STAGE
- Date of conversation
- Specific issues discussed
- Expected improvement
- Support provided
- Employee response
- Next steps agreed
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Process Failures

1. **Recency bias**: Only remembering recent events
2. **Halo effect**: One trait influences overall rating
3. **Central tendency**: Rating everyone as "meets expectations"
4. **Leniency/strictness**: Consistently too high or low
5. **No documentation**: Cannot support ratings

### Goal Setting Errors

1. **Too many goals**: Dilutes focus
2. **Vague goals**: Cannot measure success
3. **No alignment**: Goals disconnected from strategy
4. **Set and forget**: No progress tracking
5. **Sandbagging**: Goals too easy to achieve

### Conversation Gaps

1. **Surprising feedback**: No ongoing communication
2. **Avoiding tough conversations**: Issues fester
3. **One-way discussion**: Employee not heard
4. **No development focus**: Only backward-looking
5. **Rating fixation**: Number over substance

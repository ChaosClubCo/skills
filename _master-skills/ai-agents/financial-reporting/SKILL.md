# Financial Reporting

## Overview

Financial reporting transforms raw accounting data into meaningful insights that drive business decisions. This skill covers the preparation and analysis of key financial statements, management reports, budget comparisons, forecasts, and board-level presentations. Accurate, timely financial reporting enables SMBs to understand performance, identify trends, make informed decisions, and communicate effectively with stakeholders.

For SMB business solutions, streamlined financial reporting provides the visibility needed to manage cash flow, control costs, evaluate investments, and demonstrate business health to investors, lenders, and other stakeholders.

## When to Use

Invoke this skill when:
- Preparing monthly, quarterly, or annual financial statements
- Creating management reports and dashboards
- Analyzing budget vs actual performance
- Conducting variance analysis to explain differences
- Building financial forecasts and projections
- Preparing board presentations and investor updates
- Calculating and presenting financial KPIs
- Performing cash flow analysis and projections
- Creating financial models for decision-making
- Consolidating multi-entity financials
- Responding to audit or due diligence requests

### 4. Variance Analysis

#### Variance Analysis Framework

```
VARIANCE ANALYSIS METHODOLOGY

STEP 1: Identify Material Variances
- Set materiality threshold (e.g., >$5K or >5%)
- Flag all items exceeding threshold
- Prioritize by dollar impact

STEP 2: Categorize Variance Types
FAVORABLE VARIANCES (+)
- Higher revenue than planned
- Lower expenses than planned

UNFAVORABLE VARIANCES (-)
- Lower revenue than planned
- Higher expenses than planned

STEP 3: Analyze Root Causes

VOLUME VARIANCE
- More/fewer units sold
- More/fewer customers
- Higher/lower activity levels

PRICE/RATE VARIANCE
- Different selling prices
- Different cost rates
- Market conditions

TIMING VARIANCE
- Early/late revenue recognition
- Expense timing differences
- Accrual adjustments

MIX VARIANCE
- Product mix shifts
- Customer mix changes
- Channel mix differences

EFFICIENCY VARIANCE
- Productivity differences
- Process improvements
- Resource utilization
```

### KPI Reference Guide

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | 40-70% by industry |
| Operating Margin | Operating Income / Revenue | 10-25% |
| Net Profit Margin | Net Income / Revenue | 5-15% |
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | >1.5 |
| Quick Ratio | (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities | >1.0 |
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Debt / Total Equity | <2.0 |
| Days Sales Outstanding | (AR / Revenue) x 365 | <45 days |
| Days Payable Outstanding | (AP / COGS) x 365 | 30-60 days |
| Inventory Turnover | COGS / Average Inventory | >6x |
| Revenue per Employee | Revenue / FTE Count | Industry varies |
| Rule of 40 | Revenue Growth % + Profit Margin % | >40% |

## Common Pitfalls

### Reporting Errors

1. **Inconsistent formatting**: Different reports look different
2. **Missing context**: Numbers without explanation
3. **Too much detail**: Board gets lost in minutiae
4. **Stale data**: Reports based on outdated information
5. **Manual errors**: Spreadsheet formula mistakes

### Analysis Mistakes

1. **Ignoring trends**: Focus on point-in-time only
2. **Missing seasonality**: Not accounting for cyclical patterns
3. **Correlation vs causation**: Misinterpreting relationships
4. **Survivorship bias**: Only looking at successes
5. **Anchoring**: Over-relying on initial forecasts

### Communication Failures

1. **Jargon overload**: Non-finance stakeholders don't understand
2. **Burying bad news**: Not highlighting problems
3. **No action items**: Reports without recommendations
4. **Inconsistent metrics**: Definitions change over time
5. **Delayed distribution**: Reports arrive too late

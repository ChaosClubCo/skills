# Environmental Design

## Overview

Environmental design applies brand identity to physical spaces, creating immersive brand experiences that engage visitors, employees, and customers. This skill encompasses office branding, retail environments, trade show presence, wayfinding systems, and experiential installations.

Environmental design operates at the intersection of graphic design, architecture, and industrial design, requiring understanding of materials, fabrication methods, and spatial relationships while maintaining brand integrity at scale.

### Core Competencies

- Spatial branding and graphics
- Wayfinding and signage systems
- Trade show and exhibition design
- Retail environment design
- Material and fabrication knowledge
- 3D visualization and rendering
- Installation coordination
- Scale and proportion management

## When to Use

### Primary Applications

1. **Office Branding**
   - Reception and lobby design
   - Meeting room graphics
   - Workspace environmental graphics
   - Amenity space branding

2. **Trade Show Presence**
   - Booth design and fabrication
   - Pop-up displays
   - Conference presence
   - Exhibition installations

3. **Retail Environments**
   - Store graphics
   - Point-of-sale displays
   - Window displays
   - Promotional installations

4. **Wayfinding Systems**
   - Directional signage
   - Identification signs
   - Information displays
   - Regulatory signage

### Trigger Conditions

- New office space or renovation
- Trade show participation
- Retail expansion or refresh
- Brand launch or refresh
- Space needs navigation improvement
- Customer experience enhancement needed

## Common Pitfalls

### Design Mistakes

| Pitfall | Problem | Prevention |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Ignoring scale | Elements look wrong in space | Mock up at scale |
| Poor lighting plan | Graphics not visible | Lighting integration |
| Wrong material | Premature wear or failure | Test in context |
| Cluttered space | Overwhelming experience | Edit and prioritize |

### Technical Issues

| Pitfall | Problem | Prevention |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Surface assumptions | Installation failure | Survey thoroughly |
| Code non-compliance | Removal required | Code review early |
| Power oversight | Features don't work | Electrical coordination |
| Weight miscalculation | Structural issues | Engineer review |

### Production Problems

| Pitfall | Problem | Prevention |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Vendor capability | Quality shortfall | Vet thoroughly |
| Timeline optimism | Rush charges, stress | Buffer adequately |
| Poor coordination | Trade conflicts | Clear communication |
| Access issues | Delays, overtime | Plan access carefully |

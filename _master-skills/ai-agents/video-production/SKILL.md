# Video Production & Strategy

## Overview

Video has become essential for B2B marketing, with buyers preferring video content throughout their journey. For SMBs, video builds trust, explains complex solutions, and scales human connection. This skill covers the video production lifecycle from strategy through distribution, focusing on practical approaches that don't require big budgets.

Effective business video isn't about production quality alone - it's about clear messaging, authentic delivery, and strategic distribution. A well-planned smartphone video can outperform an expensive production if the content resonates.

## When to Use

**Invoke this skill when:**
- Planning video content strategy
- Scripting demo or explainer videos
- Preparing customer testimonial capture
- Creating video content briefs
- Optimizing videos for search and engagement
- Planning video distribution across channels
- Setting up video analytics and tracking
- Improving existing video performance

### 2. Scriptwriting

#### Script Frameworks

```markdown
## Video Script Frameworks

### Problem-Solution-Benefit (PSB)
Best for: Explainer videos, ads

**Structure:**
1. Hook (0-10 sec): Attention-grabbing statement or question
2. Problem (10-30 sec): Pain point your audience relates to
3. Solution (30-60 sec): How you solve the problem
4. Benefits (60-80 sec): What they gain
5. CTA (80-90 sec): Clear next step

### Demo Script Structure
Best for: Product demonstrations

**Structure:**
1. Context (0-15 sec): What you'll show and why it matters
2. Setup (15-30 sec): Brief context on the scenario
3. Walkthrough (30-240 sec): Step-by-step demonstration
4. Outcome (240-270 sec): Result and value demonstrated
5. CTA (270-300 sec): Next step for viewer

### Story-Based Structure
Best for: Testimonials, case studies

**Structure:**
1. Introduction (0-15 sec): Who they are
2. Challenge (15-45 sec): What problem they faced
3. Solution (45-90 sec): How they found and implemented solution
4. Results (90-150 sec): Specific outcomes achieved
5. Recommendation (150-180 sec): Advice and endorsement

### Educational Structure
Best for: How-to, tutorial videos

**Structure:**
1. Hook (0-15 sec): What you'll learn and why
2. Overview (15-30 sec): Quick preview of steps
3. Steps (30-X sec): Detailed walkthrough
4. Recap (X-X+15 sec): Summary of key points
5. CTA (Final 15 sec): Related resources, next steps
```

```markdown
### Example Script (Demo Video)

| Timestamp | Visual | Audio | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------|
| 0:00-0:10 | Logo, product name | "Let me show you how [Product] helps you [outcome] in just 5 minutes." | Energetic, friendly |
| 0:10-0:25 | Dashboard overview | "Here's your main dashboard. This is where you'll [primary action]." | Highlight key areas |
| 0:25-0:50 | Feature 1 walkthrough | "To [do X], simply click here. Notice how [benefit of feature]." | Show cursor path |
| 0:50-1:30 | Feature 2 deep dive | "The real magic is in [Feature]. Watch what happens when I [action]." | Pause on result |
| 1:30-2:00 | Results/output | "And there you have it - [outcome achieved]. What used to take [time] now takes [less time]." | Show before/after |
| 2:00-2:15 | CTA screen | "Ready to try it yourself? Click below to start your free trial." | Clear CTA on screen |

### Script Writing Tips
1. Write for speaking, not reading (conversational)
2. One idea per sentence
3. Use "you" and "your" liberally
4. Include pauses for visual emphasis
5. Read aloud to check flow
6. Time yourself reading at natural pace
```

### 3. Testimonial Capture

#### Customer Interview Framework

```markdown
## Testimonial Interview Guide

### Pre-Interview Preparation

**Customer Selection Criteria:**
- Achieved measurable results
- Articulate and comfortable on camera
- Represents target customer profile
- Approved by their company for participation
- Enthusiastic about your product/service

**Logistics Checklist:**
- [ ] Approval from customer marketing/PR
- [ ] Schedule 45-60 minutes
- [ ] Share questions in advance
- [ ] Confirm recording permissions
- [ ] Technical setup tested (if remote)

### Interview Questions

**Opening (Rapport)**
- Tell me about yourself and your role.
- What does your company do?

**The Challenge**
- What challenge were you facing before [Product]?
- How was that impacting your work/business?
- What had you tried before?

**The Discovery**
- How did you find out about [Product]?
- What made you decide to try it?
- What were you hoping to achieve?

**The Experience**
- What was the implementation/onboarding like?
- How does your team use [Product] day-to-day?
- What features do you rely on most?

**The Results**
- What results have you seen since implementing [Product]?
- Can you share any specific numbers or metrics?
- How has this impacted your team/business?

**The Recommendation**
- What would you say to someone considering [Product]?
- How would you describe [Company] as a partner?
- Anything else you'd like to share?

### Interview Tips
- Let them talk; don't interrupt
- Ask follow-up questions on good answers
- Prompt for specifics ("Can you give an example?")
- Get multiple takes of key sound bites
- Capture B-roll of them working/using product
```

#### Remote Testimonial Capture

```markdown
## Remote Video Testimonial Guide

### Technical Requirements for Customer
**Minimum:**
- Stable internet connection
- Laptop with webcam (or phone)
- Quiet location with good lighting
- Plain or professional background

**Ideal:**
- External webcam or DSLR
- External microphone
- Ring light or window light
- Neutral/branded background

### Remote Recording Setup

**Option 1: Video Call Recording**
Platform: Zoom, Teams, Google Meet
Quality: 720p-1080p
Pros: Familiar, easy for customer
Cons: Lower quality, platform-dependent

**Option 2: Dedicated Recording Platform**
Platforms: Riverside.fm, Squadcast, Zencastr
Quality: Up to 4K local recording
Pros: Higher quality, individual tracks
Cons: Customer needs to install app

**Option 3: Self-Recording**
Method: Customer records themselves
Quality: Variable (depends on equipment)
Pros: No scheduling, flexible
Cons: Quality control, guidance needed

### Self-Recording Guide for Customers
Send this to customers who will self-record:

---
**Quick Tips for a Great Video:**

**Location:**
- Find a quiet spot with no background noise
- Face a window for natural lighting (or use a lamp)
- Plain background (wall, bookshelf) is best

**Setup:**
- Phone works great - use back camera if possible
- Horizontal orientation (landscape mode)
- Eye level - stack books under phone/laptop
- Clean your camera lens

**Recording:**
- Look at the camera, not the screen
- Smile and speak naturally
- It's okay to restart - give us 2-3 takes
- Leave pauses between answers

**What to cover:**
1. Introduce yourself (name, company, role)
2. What challenge you faced before [Product]
3. How [Product] helped solve it
4. Results you've seen
5. Would you recommend [Product]?
---
```

### 4. Production Guidelines

#### DIY Production Setup

```markdown
## Budget-Friendly Video Production

### Essential Equipment (Under $500)
| Item | Options | Price Range |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Camera | iPhone/smartphone, webcam upgrade | $0-200 |
| Microphone | Blue Yeti, Rode NT-USB Mini | $50-150 |
| Lighting | Ring light, desk lamp, window | $30-100 |
| Tripod | Phone tripod, desk stand | $20-50 |
| Background | Wall, collapsible backdrop, virtual | $0-100 |

### Screen Recording Setup
**Software:**
- Loom (free tier available)
- ScreenFlow (Mac)
- Camtasia (Windows/Mac)
- OBS Studio (free)

**Best Practices:**
- 1080p resolution minimum
- Clean desktop, close notifications
- Use zoom/highlight to guide attention
- Add cursor highlight effect
- Record in a quiet environment

### Talking Head Setup
**Framing:**
- Rule of thirds (eyes at top third)
- Headroom (not too much)
- Eye-level camera angle
- Room for lower third graphics

**Lighting:**
- Key light at 45 degrees
- Fill light opposite or reflector
- Avoid backlighting from windows

**Audio:**
- External mic always (not laptop mic)
- Reduce room echo (soft furnishings help)
- Test levels before recording

### Production Quality Checklist
- [ ] Video: 1080p or higher
- [ ] Audio: Clear, no echo or background noise
- [ ] Lighting: Face visible, no harsh shadows
- [ ] Framing: Proper headroom and composition
- [ ] Background: Clean, professional, not distracting
- [ ] Duration: Appropriate for content type
```

```markdown
**Intro (5-10 seconds):**
- Logo animation or branded intro
- Music/sound cue

- Name and title of speaker
- Brand colors and fonts

**Text Overlays:**
- Key points emphasized
- Data/statistics callout
- CTA reinforcement

**Outro (10-15 seconds):**
- Summary or key takeaway
- Clear CTA
- Contact/website
- Logo
- Subscribe/follow prompt

- [ ] Transcript available
- [ ] Audio description (if needed)
```

```markdown
- Primary keyword variations

- Ask viewers to comment (specific question)
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Overproduction
**Problem**: Spending too much time/money on production
**Solution**: Start simple; focus on content quality over production value

### Pitfall 2: No Clear CTA
**Problem**: Great video but no next step
**Solution**: Every video needs a clear, single CTA

### Pitfall 3: Too Long
**Problem**: Losing viewers before key message
**Solution**: Shorter is usually better; front-load value

### Pitfall 4: Poor Audio
**Problem**: Bad audio ruins otherwise good video
**Solution**: Always use external mic; test before recording

### Pitfall 5: No Distribution Plan
**Problem**: Video published but not promoted
**Solution**: Plan distribution before production

### Pitfall 6: One-and-Done
**Problem**: Creating video then never using it again
**Solution**: Repurpose, update, and reshare evergreen content

# Investor Relations

> Strategic fundraising execution, compelling pitch deck creation, and thorough due diligence management for successful capital raises.

## Metadata

- **Skill ID**: investor-relations
- **Category**: Back of House - Executive Operations
- **Complexity Level**: Expert
- **Prerequisites**:
  - Financial modeling expertise
  - Storytelling and presentation skills
  - Market dynamics understanding
  - Negotiation fundamentals

## Overview

Investor Relations encompasses the strategic activities required to attract, engage, and maintain relationships with investors. This skill covers the full fundraising lifecycle from preparation through close, including pitch deck development, due diligence management, and ongoing investor communication.

### 2. Pitch Deck Development

```markdown
## Core Pitch Deck Outline (15-20 slides)

### 1. Title Slide
- Company name and tagline
- Presenter information
- Meeting date
- Confidentiality notice

### 2. The Problem (1-2 slides)
- Pain point description
- Market evidence
- Current solutions shortcomings
- Urgency and magnitude

### 3. The Solution (1-2 slides)
- Your product/service
- Key differentiators
- Value proposition
- Customer benefit

### 4. Market Opportunity (1-2 slides)

- Market trends
- Growth drivers
- Timing rationale

### 5. Business Model (1 slide)
- Revenue model
- Pricing strategy
- Unit economics
- Scalability path

### 6. Traction (2-3 slides)
- Key metrics and growth
- Customer logos/testimonials
- Milestones achieved
- Momentum indicators

### 7. Product (1-2 slides)
- Product overview
- Key features
- Technology differentiation
- Roadmap highlights

### 8. Go-to-Market (1-2 slides)
- Customer acquisition strategy
- Sales process
- Partnership strategy
- Growth levers

### 9. Competition (1 slide)
- Competitive landscape
- Differentiation matrix
- Sustainable advantages
- Market positioning

### 10. Team (1 slide)
- Founder backgrounds
- Key team members
- Relevant experience
- Hiring plans

### 11. Financials (1-2 slides)
- Historical performance
- Projections (3-5 years)
- Key assumptions
- Path to profitability

### 12. The Ask (1 slide)
- Raise amount
- Use of funds
- Key milestones
- Valuation context

### 13. Appendix
- Detailed financials
- Additional metrics
- Technical details
- References
```

### Term Sheet Negotiation

**Key Terms Analysis**
```markdown
## Term Sheet Components

### Economic Terms
| Term | Description | Considerations |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Valuation | Pre/post-money value | Market comparables, dilution |
| Option Pool | Employee equity reserve | Pre vs. post money, size |
| Liquidation Preference | Payout priority | Participating vs. non-participating |
| Anti-dilution | Price protection | Weighted average vs. full ratchet |
| Dividends | Preferred returns | Cumulative vs. non-cumulative |

### Control Terms
| Term | Description | Considerations |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Board Seats | Governance composition | Balance of control |
| Voting Rights | Decision authority | Protective provisions scope |
| Information Rights | Reporting requirements | Operational burden |
| Drag-Along | Forced sale rights | Threshold and terms |
| Protective Provisions | Veto rights | Scope and flexibility |

### Other Terms
| Term | Description | Considerations |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Vesting | Founder equity schedule | Acceleration provisions |
| Right of First Refusal | Share transfer rights | Secondary sale impact |
| Pay-to-Play | Participation requirements | Investor dynamics |
| No-Shop | Exclusivity period | Duration and scope |
| Closing Conditions | Prerequisites to funding | Timing implications |
```

# Social Media Content

> Build platform-native content systems that turn scrollers into engaged communities.

## Description

This skill encompasses social media content strategy, creation, and optimization across major platforms. It provides frameworks for developing platform-specific content that respects each channel's unique composition, tone, and algorithmic preferences. The skill covers content calendars, engagement tactics, trend adaptation, copywriting for constrained formats, and visual content planning. It addresses the full content lifecycle from ideation through performance analysis, enabling consistent brand voice across diverse social ecosystems.

## Instructions

### Core Workflow

**Step 1: Audience and Platform Analysis**
- Define the target audience: demographics, psychographics, platform behavior
- Map which platforms the audience actively engages on (not just where they exist)
- Audit competitor content: identify what resonates and what falls flat
- Establish content pillars — 3-5 thematic categories that define the brand's territory
- Determine the brand's social voice: vocabulary, tone, humor boundaries, POV

**Step 2: Content Strategy Architecture**
- Build a content mix ratio (e.g., 40% educational, 30% entertaining, 20% promotional, 10% community)
- Define content formats per platform: carousels, reels, threads, stories, long-form, polls
- Establish posting cadence aligned with audience activity windows
- Plan content series and recurring formats that build anticipation
- Create a hashtag taxonomy: branded, community, discovery, trending

**Step 3: Content Creation**
- Write platform-native copy that respects character limits and reading patterns
- Design visual compositions that stop the scroll — contrast, faces, text overlay hierarchy
- Craft hooks in the first line or first 3 seconds that create curiosity gaps
- Build narrative arcs even in micro-content — setup, tension, payoff
- Adapt a single content idea across platforms without copy-pasting

**Step 4: Scheduling and Publishing**
- Batch create content in weekly or bi-weekly production sprints
- Schedule posts using optimal timing data per platform and audience
- Queue evergreen content alongside timely, trend-responsive pieces
- Coordinate cross-platform publishing so each channel feels intentional, not duplicated
- Leave room in the calendar for real-time engagement with trends and cultural moments

**Step 5: Analysis and Iteration**
- Track platform-specific metrics: reach, engagement rate, saves, shares, profile visits
- Identify top-performing content and extract repeatable patterns
- A/B test hooks, formats, posting times, and CTAs
- Retire underperforming content pillars and formats
- Report insights monthly with clear connections between content decisions and outcomes

### Platform Composition Framework

Each platform has its own visual grammar, temporal rhythm, and social contract with users. Content must be composed natively for each.

**Instagram:**
The visual-first platform where aesthetic consistency and scroll-stopping imagery dominate. Instagram rewards composition — the careful arrangement of visual elements within a frame that communicates before any caption is read.

- Feed posts: curated, high-quality imagery or graphics with strong color contrast. Carousel posts consistently outperform single images for engagement and saves.
- Reels: 15-60 second vertical video. Hook in the first 1.5 seconds. Use text overlays for sound-off viewing. Native editing tools and trending audio boost algorithmic distribution.
- Stories: ephemeral, casual, interactive. Use polls, questions, and sliders to drive engagement. Stories build intimacy; feed posts build authority.
- Caption structure: lead with a hook line, use line breaks for readability, end with a clear CTA or question. Hashtags in comments or at the end of the caption.

**LinkedIn:**
The professional narrative platform where personal stories intersect with industry insight. LinkedIn rewards vulnerability wrapped in professional relevance.

- Text posts: 1200-1500 characters optimal. Open with a bold statement or counterintuitive claim. Use single-sentence paragraphs and generous white space. The "read more" fold appears at approximately 210 characters — the hook must land before that.
- Document posts (carousels): PDF-based slide decks that teach frameworks. 8-12 slides, one idea per slide, large text, strong visual hierarchy.
- Video: native video under 2 minutes. Professional but not corporate — authenticity outperforms production value here.
- Engagement: comment meaningfully on others' posts before and after publishing your own. The algorithm rewards active participants.

**X (Twitter):**
The conversational platform built on brevity, wit, and intellectual provocation. Twitter rewards sharp thinking compressed into minimal words.

- Single tweets: 280 characters maximum. Aim for under 200 for maximum engagement. Every word must earn its place.
- Threads: long-form storytelling in tweet-sized chunks. Number each tweet. The first tweet must function as a standalone hook. End each tweet with tension that pulls the reader to the next.
- Quote tweets: add commentary that reframes, challenges, or extends the original. Never just agree — add value.
- Timing: tweets have the shortest half-life of any social format. Post frequency matters more here than anywhere else.

**TikTok:**
The attention economy's most demanding arena. TikTok rewards pattern interrupts, authentic energy, and content that teaches or entertains within seconds.

- Hook in the first 1 second. Literally. Use movement, a provocative statement, or visual surprise.
- Native aesthetic: overproduced content underperforms. Phone-shot, real environments, genuine reactions.
- Trending sounds and formats: ride trends early but add a unique twist that connects to your niche.
- Watch time is the primary metric. Structure content to maintain attention through the entire duration.

### Content Repurposing Framework

Transform a single core idea into platform-native content across the entire social ecosystem without redundancy.

**The Content Cascade:**
Start with one "pillar" piece of content — a long-form blog post, podcast episode, video, or presentation. Then cascade it through descending levels of depth and increasing levels of brevity.

**Level 1: Pillar Content (long-form)**
The original, comprehensive piece. A 2000-word article, 30-minute podcast episode, or 15-minute video. This is the source of all derivative content.

**Level 2: Core Excerpts (medium-form)**
Extract 3-5 key insights or segments from the pillar piece. Each becomes a LinkedIn post, a Twitter thread, or an Instagram carousel. These stand alone while pointing back to the pillar.

**Level 3: Micro-Content (short-form)**
Compress each core excerpt into platform-native micro-content. A single compelling quote becomes an Instagram graphic. A key statistic becomes a tweet. A 30-second clip becomes a Reel or TikTok. A surprising claim becomes a LinkedIn text post.

**Level 4: Engagement Catalysts (conversational)**
Transform insights into questions, polls, and discussion prompts. "What's your take on [insight from pillar content]?" These generate community engagement and surface audience perspectives that inform future pillar content.

**Level 5: Evergreen Recycling**
High-performing derivative content re-enters the calendar 60-90 days later with refreshed framing. Insights that resonated once will resonate again with new audience members.

**Adaptation Rules:**
- Never copy-paste across platforms — rewrite for each channel's native voice
- Adjust visual aspect ratios: 1:1 for Instagram feed, 9:16 for Reels/TikTok/Stories, 16:9 for YouTube/LinkedIn video
- Match the energy level to the platform: professional insight on LinkedIn, punchy opinion on Twitter, visual storytelling on Instagram
- Credit the pillar source naturally — "I wrote about this in depth [link]" not "Check out my blog post"

### Best Practices

- Write the hook first — if the first line does not stop the scroll, nothing else matters
- Use contrast in visual content: light against dark, large text against minimal backgrounds, faces against flat colors
- Post consistently rather than frequently — three strong posts beat seven mediocre ones
- Engage authentically in comments within the first 60 minutes of posting to boost algorithmic distribution
- Never use all available hashtags — curate 5-15 highly relevant tags over 30 generic ones
- Design for sound-off by default — 85% of social video is watched without audio initially
- Build content series with recognizable naming patterns so followers anticipate and seek out installments
- Use native platform features (polls, stickers, collabs) to signal to algorithms that you are an active creator
- Maintain a swipe file of high-performing content across platforms for pattern analysis and inspiration
- Schedule creation time and engagement time separately — they require different mental modes
- Respond to comments with questions to extend conversation threads and boost post visibility
- Audit and prune your content pillars quarterly — retire what is not working, double down on what is
- Test posting times for 30 days before committing to a fixed schedule
- Use carousel and thread formats when a single post cannot contain the idea — depth drives saves and shares
- Always provide value before asking for anything — the reciprocity principle drives social growth

### Common Patterns

**Pattern: Product Launch Across Platforms**
A SaaS company launches a new feature. The content team creates a 90-second product demo video as the pillar piece. From this, they extract: a LinkedIn post from the CEO explaining the problem the feature solves (personal narrative format), a Twitter thread walking through the feature in 7 tweets with GIF screenshots, an Instagram carousel showing before-and-after workflows, a TikTok showing the feature in action with a trending sound, and Instagram Stories with a poll asking which use case excites followers most. Each piece is composed natively for its platform, linked by a consistent visual treatment and branded hashtag. Launch-day engagement is coordinated so the team responds to every comment within 60 minutes.

**Pattern: Thought Leadership Content Engine**
A consultant publishes a weekly LinkedIn article as their pillar content. Each article yields: 3 LinkedIn text posts (one hot take, one framework, one personal story), a Twitter thread summarizing the core argument, 2 Instagram carousels teaching the frameworks visually, and 5 Instagram Stories with polls and questions related to the topic. Over 12 weeks, this system produces 120+ pieces of content from 12 articles, building a consistent narrative that establishes the consultant as the go-to voice in their niche.

**Pattern: Community-Driven Content Cycle**
A fitness brand uses audience questions to fuel content creation. Every Friday, they post an Instagram Story with a question sticker: "What's your biggest fitness question this week?" The top 5 questions become the following week's content: Monday carousel answering question 1, Tuesday Reel demonstrating the answer to question 2, Wednesday Twitter thread on question 3, Thursday LinkedIn post connecting question 4 to broader wellness trends, Friday TikTok addressing question 5 with humor. This cycle ensures content is always relevant to the audience's actual needs and creates a feedback loop that deepens community engagement.

### Output Formats

**Content Calendar:**
A structured weekly or monthly publishing schedule organized by platform, format, content pillar, and publishing time. Includes hooks, body copy, hashtags, visual direction notes, and cross-linking strategy. Delivered as a table or structured document ready for scheduling tools.

**Platform-Specific Content Package:**
A batch of ready-to-publish posts for a single platform, each containing final copy, visual direction or specifications, hashtag sets, alt text, and publishing notes. Formatted for direct entry into scheduling tools or hand-off to a design team.

**Social Media Strategy Brief:**
A comprehensive strategy document defining the brand's social media positioning, content pillars, platform priorities, voice guidelines, posting cadence, engagement protocols, and KPI targets. Includes competitive analysis, audience insights, and a 90-day content roadmap.

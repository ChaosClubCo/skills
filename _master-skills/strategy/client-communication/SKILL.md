# Client Communication

## Overview

Client communication is the foundation of successful customer relationships. Every interaction shapes perception, builds trust, and influences outcomes. This skill covers the principles, processes, and templates for effective communication across all client touchpoints.

Excellent communication is proactive, clear, and appropriate for the audience. It anticipates questions, provides context, and maintains professionalism while building genuine relationships.

### Communication Principles

1. **Clarity First** - Remove ambiguity, be specific and direct
2. **Audience Appropriate** - Tailor message to recipient's needs and level
3. **Proactive Over Reactive** - Inform before being asked
4. **Consistent Cadence** - Establish and maintain regular rhythms
5. **Document Everything** - Create records for continuity and reference

### Communication Channels

| Channel | Best For | Response Time |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Email | Formal updates, documentation, complex information | 24 hours |
| Phone/Video | Sensitive topics, complex discussions, relationship building | Scheduled |
| Chat/Slack | Quick questions, informal updates, urgent matters | 4 hours |
| In-Person | Strategic discussions, executive engagement, workshops | Scheduled |
| Portal | Status tracking, self-service, document sharing | Async |

| Stakeholder | Frequency | Format | Content Focus |
|-------------|-----------|--------|---------------|

| Project Sponsor | Weekly | Email, Bi-weekly call | Progress, decisions |
| Day-to-Day Contact | As needed | Email, Chat | Operational details |

## When to Use

### Communication Triggers

**Scheduled Communications:**
- Weekly status updates
- Monthly executive summaries
- Quarterly Business Reviews
- Annual planning sessions
- Renewal discussions

**Event-Driven Communications:**
- Project milestones achieved
- Issues or incidents
- Product updates
- Organizational changes
- Success celebrations

**Proactive Communications:**
- Risk identification
- Opportunity discovery
- Best practice sharing
- Industry insights
- Upcoming changes

### Audience Analysis

Before communicating, consider:
- Who is the primary audience?
- What do they care about most?
- What is their technical level?
- What action do you want them to take?
- What questions will they have?
- What is the best channel?

[2-3 sentence overview of the week]

• [Completed item 2]
• [Completed item 3]

IN PROGRESS
• [Active item 1] - [% complete or status]
• [Active item 2] - [% complete or status]

PLANNED FOR NEXT WEEK
• [Upcoming item 1]
• [Upcoming item 2]

BLOCKERS/RISKS
• [Issue] - [Impact] - [Mitigation]

DECISIONS NEEDED
• [Decision required] - [Due date]

KEY METRICS
• [Metric 1]: [Value]
• [Metric 2]: [Value]

UPCOMING MILESTONES
• [Milestone] - [Date]
```

**Status Color Definitions:**

| Status | Definition | Communication Implication |
|--------|------------|---------------------------|
| Green | On track, no concerns | Standard update |
| Yellow | Minor issues, mitigation in place | Highlight issues and plan |
| Red | Significant issues, needs attention | Escalate, detailed explanation |

**Status Report Best Practices:**
- Send at consistent day/time each week
- Keep it scannable with bullets and sections
- Lead with most important information
- Be honest about challenges
- Include clear next steps
- Proofread before sending

### 2. Executive Communication

**Executive Summary Principles:**
- Lead with business impact, not activities
- Quantify value whenever possible
- Keep it brief (one page maximum)
- Focus on outcomes, not process
- Highlight strategic alignment
- Use their language, not internal jargon

**Monthly Executive Update Template:**

```
MONTHLY EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Client: [Name]
Period: [Month Year]
To: [Executive Name]
From: [CSM Name]

VALUE DELIVERED THIS MONTH
• [Outcome 1] resulting in [business impact]
• [Outcome 2] resulting in [business impact]

KEY METRICS
[Metric] | Last Month | This Month | Trend
[Name]   | [Value]    | [Value]    | [Up/Down/Flat]

STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT
[How current work supports their business objectives]

LOOKING AHEAD
[Key activities and expected outcomes for next month]

DISCUSSION TOPICS
[Items requiring executive input or awareness]
```

**Executive Meeting Preparation:**

```
PRE-MEETING CHECKLIST
□ Research recent company news
□ Review account history and metrics
□ Prepare concise agenda
□ Anticipate questions and prepare answers
□ Have specific asks ready
□ Bring relevant data/visuals
□ Confirm logistics and attendees

MEETING STRUCTURE
1. Relationship building (5 min)
2. Value summary (10 min)
3. Strategic discussion (15 min)
4. Forward planning (10 min)
5. Action items and close (5 min)

POST-MEETING ACTIONS
□ Send thank you and summary within 24 hours
□ Document key insights in CRM
□ Create action items with owners
□ Schedule follow-up if needed
```

• Involve their colleagues
• Provide clear value proposition
• Offer flexible meeting options
• Escalate through relationship chain

• Listen to concerns without defense
• Acknowledge valid points
• Provide data and proof points
• Offer pilot or proof of concept
• Find quick wins to demonstrate value

HOSTILE STAKEHOLDER
• Remain calm and professional
• Document interactions
• Focus on facts, not emotions
• Involve management appropriately
• Seek to understand root cause
```

### 4. Communication Protocols

**Email Best Practices:**

```
STRUCTURE
• Clear, specific subject line
• Greeting and context
• Main message (most important first)
• Clear call to action
• Professional signature

FORMATTING
• Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
• Bullet points for lists
• Bold for emphasis (sparingly)
• Links for long documents
• Mobile-friendly length

TONE
• Professional but warm
• Active voice
• Positive framing
• No jargon or acronyms
• Appropriate formality for audience
```

**Response Time Standards:**

| Priority | Definition | Target Response |
|----------|------------|-----------------|
| Urgent | Critical issue, executive request | 2 hours |
| High | Time-sensitive, blocking progress | 4 hours |
| Normal | Standard business inquiry | 24 hours |
| Low | FYI, no action required | 48 hours |

**Out-of-Office Protocol:**

```
When unavailable:
1. Set appropriate auto-reply
2. Identify backup contact
3. Brief backup on active issues
4. Provide backup contact to key clients
5. Check email daily for urgent items

AUTO-REPLY TEMPLATE:
Thank you for your email. I am currently out of the office
with limited email access through [date].

For urgent matters, please contact [Backup Name] at
[email/phone].

I will respond to your message upon my return.
```

### 5. Difficult Communications

**Delivering Bad News:**

```
FRAMEWORK (SARAH)
S - Situation: State the facts clearly
A - Acknowledge: Recognize the impact
R - Responsibility: Take ownership where appropriate
A - Action: Explain what is being done
H - Help: Offer support and next steps

EXAMPLE:
"I need to share some disappointing news about the project
timeline. [Situation] Due to unexpected technical complexity,
we will not meet the original go-live date.

[Acknowledge] I understand this impacts your team's planning
and creates challenges for your Q2 initiatives.

[Responsibility] We should have identified this risk earlier,
and I take responsibility for that oversight.

[Action] We have already [specific actions taken] and the
new target date is [date].

[Help] I would like to schedule a call to discuss how we
can minimize the impact and support your team through this
change."
```

**Managing Expectations:**

```
WHEN EXPECTATIONS ARE UNREALISTIC
1. Listen fully to understand the expectation
2. Acknowledge the desired outcome
3. Explain constraints factually
4. Offer alternative approaches
5. Document the agreed path forward

PHRASES TO USE:
• "I want to make sure we set you up for success..."
• "To achieve that outcome, here's what would be needed..."
• "Let me share what's realistically possible..."
• "I want to be transparent about..."
• "Here's an alternative that could work..."
```

**Handling Complaints:**

```
RESPONSE FRAMEWORK (LEAP)
L - Listen: Let them express fully without interruption
E - Empathize: Acknowledge their feelings and frustration
A - Apologize: Take responsibility appropriately
P - Problem-solve: Move to resolution

EXAMPLE:
"Thank you for sharing this with me directly. [Listen - let
them finish]

[Empathize] I can see how frustrating this has been, and
your concerns are completely valid.

[Apologize] I'm sorry you've had this experience. This is
not the level of service we aim to provide.

[Problem-solve] Here's what I'd like to do to make this
right... What would be most helpful for you?"
```

### 6. Communication During Incidents

**Incident Communication Timeline:**

| Timeframe | Communication | Audience |
|-----------|---------------|----------|
| 0-15 min | Initial acknowledgment | Affected contacts |
| 30 min | Status update | All stakeholders |
| Every hour | Progress update | All stakeholders |
| Resolution | Confirmation | All stakeholders |
| 24-48 hours | Post-incident summary | All stakeholders |

**Incident Communication Templates:**

**Initial Acknowledgment:**
```
Subject: [INCIDENT] [Brief Description] - Acknowledged

We are aware of an issue affecting [describe impact].

Current Status: Investigating
Impact: [Who/what is affected]
Next Update: Within [timeframe]

Our team is actively working on resolution. We will
provide updates as we have more information.

For urgent needs, contact [backup contact method].
```

**Progress Update:**
```
Subject: [INCIDENT] [Brief Description] - Update #[X]

Current Status: [Investigating/Identified/Mitigating/Resolved]
Impact: [Current impact description]

Update:
[What has been done since last update]
[What is being done now]

Next Update: [Timeframe]
Estimated Resolution: [If known]
```

**Resolution Notice:**
```
Subject: [RESOLVED] [Brief Description]

The issue affecting [description] has been resolved.

Resolution Summary:
• Issue: [What happened]
• Cause: [Root cause]
• Resolution: [What fixed it]
• Duration: [Start time to resolution time]

We apologize for any inconvenience this caused. A full
post-incident report will be provided within 48 hours.

Please contact us if you experience any related issues.
```

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Over-Communication

**Problem:** Flooding clients with too many messages.

**Prevention:**
- Consolidate updates
- Respect communication preferences
- Quality over quantity
- Unsubscribe options for optional content

### 2. Under-Communication

**Problem:** Clients feel uninformed or ignored.

**Prevention:**
- Scheduled update cadence
- Proactive outreach
- Response time standards
- Coverage during absences

### 3. Wrong Channel

**Problem:** Using inappropriate channel for the message.

**Prevention:**
- Channel selection guidelines
- Client preference tracking
- Message urgency matching
- Formality appropriateness

### 4. Jargon and Assumptions

**Problem:** Using internal language clients do not understand.

**Prevention:**
- Plain language policy
- Acronym avoidance
- Context provision
- Audience-appropriate content

### 5. Inconsistent Messaging

**Problem:** Different team members give conflicting information.

**Prevention:**
- Single source of truth
- Communication templates
- Team coordination
- CRM documentation

### 6. Ignoring Bad News

**Problem:** Avoiding difficult conversations.

**Prevention:**
- Proactive disclosure
- Bad news frameworks
- Management support
- Client trust building

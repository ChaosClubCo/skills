# Product Launches

## Overview

Product launches orchestrate the coordinated release of new products or features to market, bringing together product, engineering, marketing, sales, and customer success teams. A successful launch maximizes market impact while minimizing operational risk, ensuring customers can adopt and derive value from new capabilities.

Launch planning extends far beyond the release date itself, encompassing pre-launch preparation, phased rollouts, market activation, and post-launch optimization. The discipline requires balancing speed-to-market pressure with quality assurance, coordinating across time zones and functions, and adapting to real-time feedback.

Effective launches create momentum that compounds over time: early adopters become advocates, positive reviews drive organic discovery, and sales teams gain confidence from successful customer outcomes.

### Why This Matters

Launch execution directly impacts product adoption, revenue realization, and market perception. A botched launch erodes customer trust, creates support burdens, and may never recover mindshare lost to competitors. Conversely, excellent launches accelerate growth trajectories and establish market leadership positions that persist for years.

## When to Use

### Primary Triggers

- New product entering market
- Major feature release or version upgrade
- Market expansion to new geography or segment
- Pricing or packaging changes
- Brand or product repositioning
- Platform migration requiring customer action
- Partnership or integration announcements

### Specific Use Cases

| Launch Type | Scope | Timeline | Key Considerations |
|-------------|-------|----------|-------------------|
| New Product | Full GTM | 3-6 months prep | Market education, competitive positioning |
| Major Feature | Existing customers + prospects | 6-12 weeks prep | Adoption enablement, upsell motion |
| Minor Feature | Existing customers | 2-4 weeks prep | In-app discovery, documentation |
| Beta/Early Access | Select customers | 4-8 weeks | Feedback loops, iteration capacity |
| Geographic Expansion | New market | 3-6 months | Localization, compliance, support |
| Enterprise Launch | Large accounts | 8-16 weeks | White-glove onboarding, customization |

## Core Processes

### Phase 1: Launch Strategy Development

#### Launch Tier Framework

```markdown
## Launch Tier Definitions

### Tier 1: Major Launch
- New product or transformative capability
- Significant revenue or strategic impact
- Full cross-functional mobilization
- Executive visibility and involvement
- External PR and analyst relations
- Lead time: 12-16 weeks minimum

### Tier 2: Moderate Launch
- Meaningful feature addition
- Addresses key customer needs
- Marketing campaign support
- Sales enablement required
- Lead time: 6-10 weeks

### Tier 3: Minor Launch
- Incremental improvement
- Quality of life enhancement
- Documentation and changelog
- In-app notification
- Lead time: 2-4 weeks

### Tier 4: Silent Launch
- Bug fix or minor update
- No customer action required
- Internal documentation only
- Lead time: 1 week
```

### Phase 4: Rollout Execution

#### Rollout Strategies

```markdown
## Rollout Strategy Options

### Big Bang Launch
- All customers simultaneously
- Best for: Simple features, competitive timing
- Risk: High exposure if issues occur
- Mitigation: Extensive pre-launch testing

### Phased Rollout
- Percentage-based expansion
- Day 1: 1% → Day 3: 10% → Day 7: 50% → Day 14: 100%
- Best for: Infrastructure changes, complex features
- Risk: Extended timeline, version fragmentation
- Mitigation: Clear rollout schedule, monitoring

### Segment-Based Rollout
- By customer tier, region, or cohort
- Wave 1: Early adopters
- Wave 2: Strategic accounts
- Wave 3: General availability
- Best for: Enterprise features, localization
- Risk: Communication complexity
- Mitigation: Segment-specific messaging

### Opt-In Launch
- Customer chooses to activate
- Best for: Breaking changes, major UX shifts
- Risk: Low adoption, support burden
- Mitigation: Incentives, sunset timeline

### Dark Launch
- Deploy without announcement
- Monitor performance and usage
- Announce after validation
- Best for: Infrastructure, A/B testing
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Launch Failures to Avoid

1. **Premature Announcement**
   - Problem: Marketing before engineering ready
   - *Mitigation*: Hard gates before external communication

2. **Scope Creep at Launch**
   - Problem: Adding features during launch prep
   - *Mitigation*: Freeze scope 4 weeks before launch

3. **Insufficient Testing**
   - Problem: Critical bugs discovered post-launch
   - *Mitigation*: Mandatory QA gates, beta periods

4. **Support Blindsided**
   - Problem: Support team unprepared for questions
   - *Mitigation*: 2-week minimum support enablement

5. **Sales Unready**
   - Problem: Sales can't demo or discuss
   - *Mitigation*: Demo certification before launch

6. **No Rollback Plan**
   - Problem: Can't recover from launch issues
   - *Mitigation*: Required rollback procedure documented

7. **Single Point of Failure**
   - Problem: Launch depends on one person
   - *Mitigation*: Backup owners for all critical tasks

# Account Management

## Overview

Account management is the systematic practice of retaining, growing, and deepening relationships with a company's existing customer base. It replaces ad-hoc relationship management with structured processes for segmentation, planning, stakeholder engagement, expansion, renewal, and health monitoring. The objective is to turn every account into a long-term, expanding partnership rather than a one-time transaction.

### Why This Matters

The economics of account management are well established and consistently measurable:

- **Acquisition cost vs. retention cost**: Acquiring a new customer costs 5-7x more than retaining an existing one.
- **Retention and profit**: A 5% improvement in customer retention increases profits by 25-95%.
- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**: Companies sustaining NRR above 120% trade at 2-3x the valuation multiples of peers below 100%.
- **Expansion revenue**: Best-in-class B2B organizations generate 30-40% of new bookings from existing accounts.
- **Relationship breadth**: Accounts with 3+ engaged stakeholders renew at rates 15-20 percentage points higher than single-threaded accounts.

When NRR falls below 100%, the installed base is contracting and no volume of new-logo acquisition will compensate over time. Account management is the operational discipline that prevents that outcome.

## When to Use

### Primary Triggers

- Customer portfolio has grown beyond what intuition alone can manage effectively.
- Leadership requires a defensible forecast of expansion revenue or churn risk.
- The organization is shifting from a land-only model to land-and-expand.
- Retention or NRR has declined for two or more consecutive quarters.
- A new account management or customer success function is being stood up.

### Specific Use Cases

- **Annual or quarterly account planning** for top-tier customers.
- **Executive business review (EBR) preparation** with data-driven value narratives.
- **Churn risk mitigation** triggered by health score degradation.
- **Expansion pipeline development** from usage data and whitespace analysis.
- **Stakeholder mapping** for complex, multi-division enterprise accounts.
- **Portfolio rebalancing** after team changes, M&A activity, or segment migration.
- **Post-acquisition customer integration** to protect acquired revenue.

## Core Workflow

### 1. Account Segmentation & Tiering

Not every account warrants the same investment. Tiering uses two dimensions -- current ARR and expansion potential -- to allocate resources where they generate the highest return.

| Tier | Current ARR | Expansion Potential | Touch Model | Plan Cadence |
|------|-------------|---------------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Strategic** | > $500K | High | Named AM + exec sponsor | Monthly review |
| **Growth** | $100K-$500K | Medium-High | Named AM | Quarterly review |
| **Core** | $25K-$100K | Low-Medium | Pooled / tech-touch | Semi-annual review |
| **Foundation** | < $25K | Low | Fully digital, self-serve | Automated health checks |

Re-evaluate tiers at least twice per year. Accounts showing accelerating usage, executive engagement, or new budget signals should be promoted and reassigned.

### 2. Strategic Account Planning

An account plan is a living document, not a shelf artifact. Keep it concise, data-backed, and reviewed on a fixed cadence.

**Recommended plan structure:**

1. **Account snapshot** -- Industry, ARR, contract end date, tier, health score, internal and customer executive sponsors.
2. **Relationship map** -- Stakeholders with roles, sentiment, and influence (see Section 3).
3. **Value delivered** -- Outcomes achieved, quantified in the customer's language (revenue impact, cost savings, risk reduction).
4. **Whitespace analysis** -- Products or modules not yet adopted, mapped to identified needs.
5. **Risk register** -- Known risks with owner, severity, mitigation plan, and status.
6. **Expansion roadmap** -- Sequenced opportunities with estimated ARR, probability, and target quarter.
7. **Action plan** -- Quarterly goals broken into tasks with owners and due dates.

**Benchmark**: Strategic-tier plans should be reviewed monthly; growth-tier plans, quarterly. Plans that go unreviewed for more than one cadence cycle are effectively dead.

### 3. Relationship Mapping & Stakeholder Management

Enterprise buying decisions involve 6-10 stakeholders on average. Losing a single champion to attrition or reorganization can destabilize the entire relationship overnight.

For each key stakeholder, document:

- **Name, title, and reporting line**
- **Buying role**: Decision maker, budget holder, technical evaluator, end user, champion, or blocker.
- **Sentiment**: Advocate, supporter, neutral, skeptic, or detractor.
- **Relationship strength**: Strong (regular contact, mutual trust), moderate (periodic contact), or weak (limited engagement).
- **Personal objectives**: Professional goals your solution can support.

**Target**: Maintain at least 3 strong relationships per strategic account across different organizational levels. Track multi-threading coverage as a portfolio-level KPI with an 80%+ target.

### 4. Expansion & Upsell Strategy

Expansion revenue is the highest-margin growth lever available. It requires a structured approach, not opportunistic deal-finding.

**Expansion identification process:**

1. **Usage analysis** -- Identify features or modules approaching capacity limits or showing high adoption velocity.
2. **Whitespace mapping** -- Compare the customer's current footprint against the full product catalog.
3. **Stakeholder signals** -- Capture expansion intent from QBR discussions, support conversations, and champion feedback.
4. **Trigger events** -- Monitor for budget approvals, new initiatives, leadership changes, or M&A activity.

**Expansion pipeline hygiene:**

| Stage | Definition | Conversion Benchmark |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| Identified | Opportunity recognized from data or signal | -- |
| Qualified | Customer need confirmed, budget pathway exists | 40-50% to close |
| Proposed | Formal proposal or SOW delivered | 60-70% to close |
| Committed | Verbal or written agreement, pending signature | 85-95% to close |

**Benchmark**: Expansion revenue should represent at least 30% of total new bookings. Organizations below 20% are under-investing in their installed base.

### 5. Renewal Management

Renewals are not administrative events. They are the culmination of every interaction since the last contract signature.

**Renewal timeline:**

| Milestone | Strategic Tier | Growth Tier | Core Tier |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Health assessment | 180 days out | 120 days out | 90 days out |
| Stakeholder alignment | 150 days out | 90 days out | 60 days out |
| Commercial proposal | 120 days out | 60 days out | 45 days out |
| Negotiation window | 90-60 days out | 45-30 days out | 30-15 days out |
| Signature target | 30 days out | 15 days out | 7 days out |

**Targets**: Gross revenue retention (GRR) above 90%. Logo retention above 90%. Renewals completed before the contract expiration date at least 85% of the time.

### 6. Account Health Scoring

A health score condenses multiple signals into one composite indicator that predicts renewal likelihood and expansion readiness.

| Signal Category | Weight | Inputs | Scoring (0-100) |
|----------------|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **Product usage** | 30% | DAU/MAU ratio, feature breadth, 30/60/90-day trend | Above benchmark = 80-100; At benchmark = 50-79; Below = 0-49 |
| **Relationship depth** | 20% | Stakeholders engaged, exec sponsor activity, NPS/CSAT | Multi-threaded + high NPS = 80-100; Single-threaded = 50-79; None = 0-49 |
| **Contract signals** | 20% | Time to renewal, payment history, contract term | Multi-year + on-time = 80-100; Annual = 50-79; Month-to-month or late = 0-49 |
| **Support health** | 15% | Open tickets, resolution time, escalation frequency | No escalations = 80-100; Moderate = 50-79; Frequent escalations = 0-49 |
| **Expansion activity** | 15% | Active pipeline, recent upsell, cross-sell conversations | Active pipeline = 80-100; Exploratory = 50-79; None = 0-49 |

**Composite** = (Usage x 0.30) + (Relationship x 0.20) + (Contract x 0.20) + (Support x 0.15) + (Expansion x 0.15)

**Interpretation bands:**

- **80-100 (Healthy)**: Low churn risk. Maintain cadence and pursue expansion.
- **60-79 (Stable)**: Monitor trends. Address declining signals within 30 days.
- **40-59 (At Risk)**: Initiate a structured save plan with executive involvement.
- **0-39 (Critical)**: Immediate intervention. Escalate to leadership and schedule an on-site review.

**Target distribution**: More than 60% of accounts Healthy, fewer than 10% Critical.

## Common Pitfalls

| Problem | Impact | Prevention |
|---------|--------|------------|
| **Treating all accounts equally** | Strategic accounts are underserved; low-value accounts consume disproportionate resources. | Enforce the tiering model. Audit time allocation quarterly. Automate foundation-tier engagement. |
| **Single-threaded relationships** | One champion departure destabilizes the account overnight. | Track multi-threading as a KPI. Require 3+ contacts at different levels for every strategic account. Involve exec sponsors regularly. |
| **Reactive-only engagement** | By the time churn signals are visible, the intervention window has closed. | Establish proactive cadences: monthly for strategic, quarterly for growth. Use health scores to trigger early workflows. |
| **Account plans that gather dust** | Plans written once become stale opinion documents disconnected from reality. | Mandate data-backed plans with health scores, usage metrics, and pipeline data pulled from integrated systems. Review on the defined cadence without exception. |

## Integration Points

- **CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Dynamics)** -- System of record for account data, pipeline, and contacts. Sync health scores to account records, log EBR outcomes as activities, and trigger expansion opportunities from whitespace analysis.
- **Support platforms (Zendesk, Intercom, ServiceNow)** -- Feed ticket volume, resolution time, escalation frequency, and CSAT into the health score. Alert account managers within 24 hours when strategic accounts file severity-one tickets.
- **Product analytics (Pendo, Amplitude, Mixpanel)** -- Supply usage metrics for health scoring and expansion signal detection. Track feature adoption breadth and depth at the account level.
- **Client onboarding** -- Define a formal handoff protocol that transfers the relationship map, documented success criteria, and open risks from the onboarding team to the account manager. Jointly own time-to-value until the first milestone is confirmed.
- **Sales and pipeline management** -- Route qualified expansion opportunities from account managers into the pipeline. Share win/loss data from existing-account deals alongside retention metrics for a complete trajectory view.
- **Product and engineering** -- Aggregate and prioritize feature requests from strategic accounts, tagged with estimated revenue impact. Include account management in beta programs so strategic customers receive early access.

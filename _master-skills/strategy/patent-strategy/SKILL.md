# Patent Strategy

## Overview

Patent strategy transforms innovation into protected intellectual property assets that create competitive moats, generate licensing revenue, and prevent competitor imitation. Effective patent programs balance the costs of prosecution with the strategic value of protection, focusing resources on inventions that matter most to business success.

Beyond defensive protection, strategic patenting can shape industry standards, create negotiating leverage, and signal technology leadership to investors and partners. The discipline requires close collaboration between technical inventors, product strategists, and IP legal professionals.

A well-managed patent portfolio is both a shield against infringement claims and a sword for competitive positioning. Organizations that master patent strategy can extract disproportionate value from their innovation investments.

### Why This Matters

Without patent protection, innovations can be freely copied by competitors, eroding first-mover advantages. Strategic patents create barriers to entry, enable cross-licensing negotiations, and may represent significant enterprise value in M&A transactions. The decision of what to patent, when, and where has long-term strategic implications.

## When to Use

### Primary Triggers

- Developing novel technical solutions
- Planning new product launches
- Entering new markets or geographies
- Preparing for funding rounds or M&A
- Analyzing competitive IP landscape
- Responding to infringement concerns
- Building or rationalizing patent portfolio

#### Filing Decision Framework

```markdown
## Filing Strategy Matrix

### Geographic Coverage Decision
| Factor | US Only | US + Major | Global |
|--------|---------|------------|--------|
| Market revenue | <$10M | $10-50M | >$50M |
| Manufacturing location | US only | Multi-region | Global |

### Filing Vehicle Options
| Vehicle | Cost | Speed | Best For |
|---------|------|-------|----------|
| Provisional | Low | Fast | Early filing, buy time |
| Non-provisional | Medium | Standard | Direct US protection |
| PCT | High | Slow | International coverage |
| Direct foreign | High | Variable | Specific country need |

### Timeline Strategy
| Event | Deadline | Action Required |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| Conception | - | Document invention |
| First disclosure | - | File provisional |
| Provisional + 12mo | Hard | File non-provisional or PCT |
| PCT + 30mo | Hard | Enter national phase |
| Publication | 18mo from priority | Automatic |

| Phase | Cost Range | Notes |
|-------|------------|-------|

| Non-provisional | $10-20K | Full application |
| PCT | $5-10K | International option |

| Grant/issue | $1-3K | Government fees |
```

#### Claims Strategy

```markdown
## Claims Drafting Guidelines

### Claim Types

#### Independent Claims
- Stand alone, broadest protection
- Minimum elements needed
- Multiple independent claims for fallback
- Different claim categories (method, system, product)

#### Dependent Claims
- Add limitations to independent claims
- Create claim tree for negotiation flexibility
- Cover specific implementations
- Anticipate competitor design-arounds

### Claim Breadth Strategy
| Approach | Breadth | Risk | Use When |
|----------|---------|------|----------|
| Pioneer | Very broad | Rejection risk | True breakthrough |
| Improvement | Focused | Lower risk | Incremental innovation |
| Design-around | Specific | Easy allowance | Block competitors |

### Claim Drafting Checklist
- [ ] Independent claim covers core innovation
- [ ] Dependent claims create defensible positions
- [ ] Multiple claim formats (method, system, etc.)
- [ ] Technical terms consistently defined
- [ ] Scope appropriate for prior art landscape
- [ ] Claims detectable in competitor products
```

#### Design-Around Strategies

```markdown
## Design-Around Options

### When to Design Around
- Patent clearly valid and enforceable
- Licensing not available or too expensive
- Technical alternative feasible
- Time to implement before launch

### Design-Around Process
1. Identify specific claim elements to avoid
2. Brainstorm alternative technical approaches
3. Evaluate alternatives for feasibility
4. Confirm design-around avoids all claims
5. Document design-around rationale
6. Implement and verify

### Documentation Requirements
- Clear record of design-around intent
- Technical rationale for alternative approach
- Comparison to patent claims
- Independent development evidence

### Caution: Willful Infringement
- Document good-faith design-around efforts
- Obtain opinion of counsel if concerned
- Avoid copying after notice
- Implement alternatives where possible
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Patent Strategy Mistakes

1. **Filing Too Late**
   - Problem: Public disclosure before filing
   - Impact: Loss of patent rights (many countries)
   - *Mitigation*: Clear disclosure policies, provisional filing

2. **Overly Narrow Claims**
   - Problem: Easy to design around
   - Impact: Limited competitive protection
   - *Mitigation*: Experienced patent counsel, claim strategy review

3. **Overly Broad Claims**
   - Problem: Invalid over prior art
   - Impact: Wasted prosecution effort
   - *Mitigation*: Thorough prior art search

4. **Geographic Mismatch**
   - Problem: Patents where competitors aren't
   - Impact: Unenforceable protection
   - *Mitigation*: Align filing geography to business

5. **Abandoned Maintenance**
   - Problem: Valuable patents lapse
   - Impact: Lost protection
   - *Mitigation*: Maintenance fee tracking, annual review

6. **Invention Hoarding**
   - Problem: Employees don't disclose
   - Impact: Missing IP opportunities
   - *Mitigation*: Inventor incentives, culture building

7. **Ignoring Prior Art**
   - Problem: Filing obvious inventions
   - Impact: Wasted resources, weak patents
   - *Mitigation*: Mandatory prior art search before filing

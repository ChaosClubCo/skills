# Crisis Management

> Crisis response planning, communication protocols, and organizational recovery strategies that protect reputation and enable rapid recovery.

## Metadata

- **Skill ID**: crisis-management
- **Category**: Back of House - Executive Operations
- **Complexity Level**: Expert
- **Prerequisites**:
  - Executive communication skills
  - Risk assessment experience
  - Decision-making under pressure
  - Stakeholder management capability

## Overview

Crisis Management encompasses the preparation, response, and recovery processes for organizational crises. This skill covers crisis identification, response protocols, communication strategies, and recovery planning that protect organizational reputation, maintain stakeholder trust, and enable rapid return to normal operations.

### 2. Crisis Response

**Crisis Response Framework**
```markdown
## Crisis Response Protocol

### First Hour (Golden Hour)
**Minutes 0-15: Alert and Assess**
- Receive and verify alert
- Initial impact assessment
- Activate crisis commander
- Secure relevant evidence/data

**Minutes 15-30: Mobilize**
- Notify crisis team
- Establish communication channels
- Begin detailed assessment
- Prepare initial holding statement

**Minutes 30-60: Stabilize**
- Execute immediate containment
- Draft stakeholder communications
- Brief leadership team
- Establish regular update cadence

### First Day
**Hours 1-4: Contain**
- Full crisis team assembled
- Comprehensive assessment
- Containment actions underway
- Key stakeholder notifications
- Media holding statement ready

**Hours 4-12: Manage**
- Root cause investigation
- Resolution planning
- Ongoing stakeholder communication
- Media management
- Employee communication

**Hours 12-24: Resolve**
- Resolution implementation
- Progress communication
- Recovery planning initiated
- Lessons learned capture begins

### First Week
- Crisis resolution completion
- Stakeholder relationship repair
- Process improvement identification
- After-action review
- Recovery plan execution
```

### 3. Crisis Communication

**Communication Strategy Framework**
```markdown
## Crisis Communication Plan

### Communication Principles
1. **Speed**: Communicate early, even with incomplete info
2. **Transparency**: Be honest about what you know and don't know
3. **Empathy**: Acknowledge impact on stakeholders
4. **Accountability**: Take responsibility appropriately
5. **Consistency**: Unified message across all channels

### Stakeholder Communication Matrix
| Stakeholder | Priority | Channel | Timing | Owner |
|-------------|----------|---------|--------|-------|
| Employees | High | Email/Meeting | Immediate | HR/CEO |
| Customers | High | Email/Support | Within hours | Comms |
| Investors | High | Call/Letter | Same day | CFO |
| Media | Medium | Statement/Brief | As needed | Comms |
| Regulators | High | Required channels | Per requirement | Legal |
| Partners | Medium | Direct outreach | Same day | Relevant exec |
| Public | Variable | Website/Social | As appropriate | Comms |

### Message Development
**Initial Statement Template**
```
[Company] is aware of [situation description].

We are [immediate actions being taken].

Our priority is [key focus, e.g., customer safety, resolution].

We will provide updates as we learn more.

[Contact information for questions]
```

**Key Message Components**
- What happened (facts only)
- What we're doing about it
- How we're protecting stakeholders
- What happens next
- How to get more information
```

- Long-term relationship focus

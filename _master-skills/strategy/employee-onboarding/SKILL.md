# Employee Onboarding

## Overview

Employee onboarding is the structured process of integrating new hires into an organization, from offer acceptance through their first year. Effective onboarding accelerates productivity, builds engagement, reinforces culture, and significantly improves retention rates.

For SMB business solutions, a well-designed onboarding program reduces time-to-productivity, decreases early turnover, ensures compliance with employment requirements, and creates consistent experiences that scale with company growth.

## When to Use

Invoke this skill when:
- Designing or improving onboarding programs
- Creating pre-boarding communication sequences
- Building first-day and first-week checklists
- Setting up new hire paperwork and compliance
- Planning orientation schedules and content
- Creating role-specific training plans
- Developing 30-60-90 day frameworks
- Measuring onboarding effectiveness
- Training managers on onboarding responsibilities
- Integrating onboarding with HRIS systems
- Building onboarding for remote employees

## Core Processes

### 1. Pre-Boarding (Offer to Day 1)

#### Pre-Boarding Timeline

```
PRE-BOARDING CHECKLIST

IMMEDIATELY AFTER OFFER ACCEPTANCE
[ ] Send welcome email with next steps
[ ] Initiate background check
[ ] Send new hire paperwork digitally
[ ] Add to HRIS as pending employee
[ ] Notify IT for equipment setup
[ ] Notify facilities for workspace

1 WEEK BEFORE START
[ ] Confirm start date and logistics
[ ] Send Day 1 schedule
[ ] Provide parking/building access info

[ ] Ensure workspace ready
```

### 2. Day 1 Experience

#### First Day Schedule Template

```
DAY 1 AGENDA

8:30 AM - ARRIVAL & WELCOME
- Greeter meets at entrance
- Badge/access setup

- Welcome gift/swag

9:00 AM - HR ORIENTATION (90 min)
- Company overview and history
- Mission, vision, values

- Coffee with buddy/mentor

11:00 AM - IT SETUP (60 min)
- Equipment walkthrough
- System access verification
- Security training
- Communication tools setup

12:00 PM - TEAM LUNCH
- Manager and team members
- Informal introductions
- Culture immersion

1:30 PM - MANAGER 1:1 (60 min)
- Role expectations
- 30-60-90 day plan review
- Communication preferences
- Questions and concerns

2:30 PM - DEPARTMENT ORIENTATION
- Team structure and roles
- Key projects overview
- Tools and processes
- Meeting cadence

4:00 PM - SELF-PACED SETUP
- Complete remaining paperwork
- Explore systems
- Review training materials

4:30 PM - END OF DAY CHECK-IN
- Questions answered
- Tomorrow preview
- Buddy contact info
```

### 4. 30-60-90 Day Plan

#### 30-60-90 Framework Template

```
30-60-90 DAY PLAN

FIRST 30 DAYS: LEARN
Theme: Absorb and understand

Goals:
- Complete all onboarding training
- Understand team dynamics and culture
- Learn key tools and processes
- Build relationships with teammates
- Understand role expectations fully

Key Activities:
- Shadow team members
- Read documentation
- Attend all meetings as observer
- Complete compliance training
- Weekly manager check-ins

Success Metrics:
- Training completion 100%
- Meet all key stakeholders
- Demonstrate basic tool proficiency
- Pass compliance assessments

DAYS 31-60: CONTRIBUTE
Theme: Apply and engage

Goals:
- Take ownership of initial projects
- Contribute meaningfully to team
- Identify improvement opportunities
- Deepen stakeholder relationships
- Develop role-specific skills

Key Activities:
- Own assigned projects
- Participate actively in meetings
- Seek feedback proactively
- Begin independent work
- Bi-weekly manager check-ins

Success Metrics:
- First project delivered
- Positive peer feedback
- Self-sufficient on daily tasks
- Identified 2-3 improvements

DAYS 61-90: OWN
Theme: Lead and improve

Goals:
- Full ownership of responsibilities
- Drive results independently
- Implement improvements
- Mentor newer team members
- Plan for next quarter

Key Activities:
- Lead projects or workstreams
- Present to stakeholders
- Propose process improvements
- Contribute to team planning
- Monthly manager check-ins

Success Metrics:
- Performance goals on track
- Key stakeholder satisfaction
- Independence demonstrated
- Clear development plan
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Process Failures

1. **Information overload Day 1**: Retention drops significantly
2. **No pre-boarding**: New hires feel forgotten before starting
3. **Manager uninvolved**: No ownership of new hire success
4. **Generic program**: Not tailored to role or level
5. **Ends after Week 1**: No continued support

### Administrative Errors

1. **Paperwork delays**: Compliance risks and payroll issues
2. **IT not ready**: Productivity loss, frustration
3. **No workspace**: Feeling unwelcome
4. **Benefits confusion**: Missed enrollment deadlines
5. **Missing I-9**: Legal liability

### Cultural Misses

1. **No social integration**: Isolation and disengagement
2. **Unclear expectations**: Performance anxiety
3. **No feedback loop**: Problems not identified
4. **Buddy mismatch**: Poor support system
5. **Values not demonstrated**: Cultural disconnect

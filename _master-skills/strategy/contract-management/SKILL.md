# Contract Management

## Overview

Contract management is fundamental to protecting business interests, managing risk, and ensuring operational continuity. For SMBs, contracts govern relationships with customers, vendors, partners, and employees. Effective contract management reduces legal exposure, prevents missed renewals, and ensures parties fulfill their obligations.

This skill provides comprehensive frameworks for the entire contract lifecycle - from initial drafting through execution, management, and renewal or termination. The goal is to establish systematic processes that scale with business growth while maintaining legal rigor appropriate for SMB operations.

## When to Use

Invoke this skill when:
- Drafting new contracts (MSA, SOW, NDA, etc.)
- Reviewing contracts from other parties
- Establishing contract approval workflows
- Setting up renewal tracking systems
- Managing contract obligations
- Negotiating contract terms
- Creating redlining protocols
- Conducting contract audits
- Terminating or renewing agreements
- Building a contract template library

## Core Processes

### 1. Contract Types and Templates

#### Common SMB Contract Types

| Contract Type | Purpose | Typical Duration | Key Terms |
|---------------|---------|------------------|-----------|
| MSA (Master Service Agreement) | Ongoing service relationship | 1-3 years | Terms, liability, IP, termination |
| SOW (Statement of Work) | Specific project under MSA | Project-based | Scope, deliverables, timeline, payment |
| NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) | Protect confidential information | 1-5 years | Definition, obligations, exclusions |
| SaaS Agreement | Software subscription | Annual | License, SLA, data, pricing |
| Employment Agreement | Employee relationship | Indefinite | Compensation, duties, termination |
| Vendor Agreement | Supplier relationship | 1-3 years | Products/services, pricing, terms |
| Partnership Agreement | Business partnership | Varies | Roles, profit sharing, governance |

#### Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) Template

```markdown
# MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**Effective Date:** [Date]

**Between:**
[Company A] ("Party A")
[Address]

**And:**
[Company B] ("Party B")
[Address]

## 1. PURPOSE
The parties wish to explore [describe business purpose]
(the "Purpose") and may need to disclose Confidential
Information to each other.

## 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
"Confidential Information" means any non-public information
disclosed by either party, including but not limited to:
- Technical data, trade secrets, know-how
- Business information, financial data, strategies
- Customer and supplier information
- Product plans and marketing strategies
- Any information marked "Confidential"

## 3. EXCLUSIONS
Confidential Information does not include information that:
(a) Is or becomes publicly available without breach
(b) Was known prior to disclosure
(c) Is received from a third party without restriction
(d) Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
(e) Is required to be disclosed by law (with notice)

## 4. OBLIGATIONS
Each party agrees to:
(a) Use Confidential Information only for the Purpose
(b) Protect Confidential Information using reasonable care
(c) Limit disclosure to employees/contractors with need to know
(d) Not disclose to third parties without written consent
(e) Return or destroy upon request or termination

## 5. TERM
This Agreement shall remain in effect for [2] years from the
Effective Date. Confidentiality obligations survive for [3]
years after termination.

## 6. NO LICENSE
No license or rights are granted except the limited right
to use Confidential Information for the Purpose.

## 7. NO WARRANTY
Confidential Information is provided "AS IS" without warranty.

## 8. REMEDIES
Each party acknowledges that breach may cause irreparable
harm and agrees that the other party may seek injunctive
relief in addition to other remedies.

## 9. GENERAL
- Governing Law: [State]
- Entire Agreement
- Amendment requires written agreement
- Neither party may assign without consent

## SIGNATURES

PARTY A:
Signature: _______________________
Name: _______________________
Title: _______________________
Date: _______________________

PARTY B:
Signature: _______________________
Name: _______________________
Title: _______________________
Date: _______________________
```

#### Approval Workflow

```
CONTRACT APPROVAL PROCESS
=========================

1. SUBMISSION
   Requester submits contract with:
   - Contract summary
   - Business justification
   - Risk assessment
   - Recommended approvers

2. LEGAL REVIEW (if required)
   - Review against standards
   - Identify legal risks
   - Recommend changes
   - Provide approval recommendation

3. FINANCE REVIEW (if required)
   - Verify budget availability
   - Review payment terms
   - Assess financial risk
   - Provide approval recommendation

4. BUSINESS APPROVAL
   - Review business terms
   - Confirm alignment with strategy
   - Approve or request changes

5. FINAL APPROVAL
   - Based on approval matrix
   - May require multiple approvers
   - Document approval in system

6. EXECUTION
   - Obtain authorized signatures
   - Counterparty execution
   - Document storage
```

### 4. Redlining Protocols

#### Redlining Best Practices

```
REDLINING GUIDELINES
===================

GENERAL PRINCIPLES
- Use track changes for all edits
- Never delete without replacement unless truly removing
- Maintain document integrity
- Be clear and specific in changes
- Consider counterparty's perspective

FORMATTING STANDARDS
- Strikethrough for deletions
- Underline for additions
- Comments for explanations
- Use consistent color coding:
  - Blue: Our initial draft
  - Red: Counterparty changes
  - Green: Agreed revisions

COMMENTING GUIDELINES
- Explain rationale for significant changes
- Offer alternatives when rejecting
- Flag items for discussion
- Mark non-negotiable items clearly

VERSION CONTROL
- Use clear naming convention:
  Contract_Name_v1_Date_Party
- Track all versions
- Maintain clean comparison copies
- Archive superseded versions

NEGOTIATION TIPS
- Prioritize your must-haves
- Identify nice-to-haves
- Know your walk-away points
- Document agreed items as you go
```

#### Common Redline Positions

```
STANDARD NEGOTIATION POSITIONS
==============================

LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
Our Position: Cap at fees paid in prior 12 months
Acceptable: Cap at fees paid during agreement term
Unacceptable: Unlimited liability

INDEMNIFICATION
Our Position: Mutual indemnification for own actions
Acceptable: Reasonable breadth with carve-outs
Unacceptable: One-sided broad indemnification

TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE
Our Position: Either party with 30 days notice
Acceptable: 60-90 days notice
Unacceptable: Only counterparty can terminate

AUTO-RENEWAL
Our Position: No auto-renewal or with opt-out notice
Acceptable: Auto-renew with 60+ day opt-out
Unacceptable: Auto-renew with short opt-out window

IP OWNERSHIP
Our Position: We own work product
Acceptable: Joint ownership with license
Unacceptable: Counterparty owns all work product

GOVERNING LAW
Our Position: Our state jurisdiction
Acceptable: Neutral jurisdiction
Unacceptable: Foreign jurisdiction with unfavorable laws
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Drafting Pitfalls
- **Vague scope:** Be specific about what's included and excluded
- **Missing termination rights:** Always include termination provisions
- **Unlimited liability:** Cap liability appropriately
- **Auto-renewal traps:** Include reasonable opt-out periods

### Process Pitfalls
- **Missing deadlines:** Track all key dates
- **Lost contracts:** Use central repository
- **Orphaned contracts:** Assign clear ownership
- **Skipped reviews:** Follow review process consistently

### Negotiation Pitfalls
- **Accepting first draft:** Always review and negotiate
- **Ignoring red flags:** Address concerns before signing
- **Scope creep:** Document changes formally
- **Verbal agreements:** Everything in writing

# Office Operations

## Overview

Office operations encompasses the management of physical and virtual workspaces, equipment, supplies, and the policies that govern how work environments function. For SMBs, effective office operations creates an environment that supports productivity, attracts talent, and optimizes costs.

This skill provides frameworks for managing all aspects of office operations - from physical facilities to remote work infrastructure. The focus is on creating efficient, cost-effective operations that scale with business growth.

### Why Office Operations Matters for SMBs

- **Productivity**: Well-designed workspaces improve employee effectiveness
- **Cost Control**: Efficient operations reduce overhead expenses
- **Talent Attraction**: Modern work environments attract quality candidates
- **Business Continuity**: Proper infrastructure ensures uninterrupted operations
- **Compliance**: Workplace safety and accessibility requirements
- **Culture**: Physical environment reflects and shapes company culture

## When to Use

### Primary Triggers
- Setting up new office spaces or relocating
- Procuring office equipment and technology
- Establishing or updating remote work policies
- Managing facilities maintenance and vendors
- Planning office budgets and cost optimization

### Specific Use Cases
- "We're moving to a new office space"
- "What equipment do we need for new employees?"
- "Help me create a remote work policy"
- "How do we manage office supplies efficiently?"
- "We need to set up a facilities maintenance schedule"
- "What should our office budget include?"
- "How do we design our workspace layout?"

| Item | Par Level | Reorder Point | Order Qty |
|------|-----------|---------------|-----------|

| Notebooks | 15 | 5 | 12 |
| Coffee (bag) | 4 | 1 | 3 |
| Paper towels | 10 | 4 | 8 |

### Ordering Process
1. Weekly inventory check (designated day)
2. Compare to par levels
3. Create order list for items at/below reorder point
4. Submit order to approved vendor
5. Receive and verify delivery
6. Restock and update inventory

### Cost Control Strategies
- Consolidate vendors for volume discounts
- Set up automatic reorder for staples
- Limit high-cost items to request basis
- Monitor usage trends and adjust pars
- Review vendor pricing quarterly

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Underestimating Space Needs
**Problem**: Outgrowing space faster than expected
**Prevention**:
- Plan for 20-30% growth buffer
- Consider flexible/coworking options
- Review space needs quarterly

### 2. Reactive Maintenance
**Problem**: Only fixing things when they break
**Prevention**:
- Implement preventive maintenance schedule
- Track maintenance history
- Budget for ongoing maintenance

### 3. Poor Budget Planning
**Problem**: Unexpected costs strain finances
**Prevention**:
- Include contingency (10-15%)
- Track all expenses diligently
- Review and adjust quarterly

### 4. Remote Work Friction
**Problem**: Remote workers feel disconnected or unsupported
**Prevention**:
- Clear policies and expectations
- Adequate equipment and stipends
- Regular check-ins and inclusion

### 5. Vendor Over-Reliance
**Problem**: Single vendor dependency creates risk
**Prevention**:
- Maintain backup vendor relationships
- Review vendor contracts annually
- Document processes for vendor transitions

### 6. Neglecting Employee Input
**Problem**: Workspace doesn't meet actual needs
**Prevention**:
- Survey employees on workspace needs
- Involve teams in design decisions
- Collect ongoing feedback

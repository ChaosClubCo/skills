# Social Media Marketing

## Overview

Social media marketing is the strategic use of social platforms to build brand awareness, engage audiences, generate leads, and drive business outcomes. For SMBs, social media offers a cost-effective channel to reach target audiences, build relationships, and compete with larger players.

This skill covers essential social media competencies:
- **Strategy**: Aligning social presence with business goals
- **Content Planning**: Creating and scheduling engaging content
- **Platform Management**: Optimizing presence across channels
- **Community Engagement**: Building relationships with followers
- **Analytics**: Measuring and improving performance
- **Integration**: Connecting social with other marketing efforts

Effective social media requires consistency, authenticity, and audience focus. Success comes from providing value to your audience, not just promoting your offerings.

## When to Use

Apply this skill when:

- **Building Social Presence**: Establishing or expanding social channels
- **Launching Brand Awareness Campaigns**: Increasing visibility and recognition
- **Generating Leads**: Using social to drive pipeline
- **Building Community**: Creating engaged audience around your brand
- **Customer Engagement**: Responding to and building relationships
- **Content Distribution**: Amplifying content reach
- **Competitive Positioning**: Establishing thought leadership
- **Crisis Management**: Responding to reputation issues

**Indicators you need better social media marketing:**
- Follower growth stagnant or negative
- Low engagement rates (likes, comments, shares)
- Posting inconsistently or sporadically
- No clear social media strategy or goals
- Unable to attribute business results to social efforts
- Competitors significantly outperforming you socially
- Customer complaints going unanswered

## Core Processes

### Process 1: Social Media Strategy Development

**Platform Selection Framework**

```
Platform Assessment Matrix:

LinkedIn:
├── Best For: B2B, professional services, recruiting
├── Content Types: Articles, professional insights, company news
├── Posting Frequency: 1-2 posts/day, business hours
├── Key Metrics: Engagement rate, follower growth, leads
└── Time Investment: Medium-High

Facebook:
├── Best For: Local business, B2C, community building
├── Content Types: Mixed media, stories, groups, events
├── Posting Frequency: 1-2 posts/day
├── Key Metrics: Engagement, reach, group activity
└── Time Investment: Medium

Instagram:
├── Best For: Visual brands, lifestyle, younger demographics
├── Content Types: Photos, Reels, Stories, carousels
├── Posting Frequency: 1-3 posts/day + Stories
├── Key Metrics: Engagement rate, saves, shares, reach
└── Time Investment: High (visual content creation)

X (Twitter):
├── Best For: News, real-time engagement, B2B, thought leadership
├── Content Types: Short text, threads, links, images
├── Posting Frequency: 3-5+ posts/day
├── Key Metrics: Engagement, retweets, mentions, impressions
└── Time Investment: High (frequent posting required)

YouTube:
├── Best For: Educational content, product demos, brand building
├── Content Types: Long-form video, Shorts
├── Posting Frequency: 1-4 videos/week
├── Key Metrics: Views, watch time, subscribers, engagement
└── Time Investment: Very High (production required)

TikTok:
├── Best For: Younger audience, creative brands, entertainment
├── Content Types: Short-form video, trends, challenges
├── Posting Frequency: 1-4 videos/day
├── Key Metrics: Views, engagement, follows, shares
└── Time Investment: High (consistent video creation)
```

**Platform Selection Decision Tree**

```
1. Who is your primary audience?
   ├── B2B professionals → LinkedIn (primary)
   ├── Consumers 25-54 → Facebook + Instagram
   ├── Consumers 18-34 → Instagram + TikTok
   └── Mixed/All ages → Multi-platform strategy

2. What content can you create consistently?
   ├── Video capable → YouTube, TikTok, Reels
   ├── Strong visuals → Instagram, Pinterest
   ├── Written thought leadership → LinkedIn, X
   └── News/updates → X, LinkedIn

3. How much time/resources available?
   ├── Limited: Focus on 1-2 platforms max
   ├── Moderate: 2-3 platforms
   └── Robust: Multi-platform presence

4. What are your primary goals?
   ├── Lead generation → LinkedIn, Facebook (with ads)
   ├── Brand awareness → Instagram, TikTok, YouTube
   ├── Customer support → X, Facebook
   └── Community building → Facebook Groups, LinkedIn
```

### Process 3: Platform-Specific Optimization

**LinkedIn Best Practices**

```
Profile Optimization (Company Page):
├── Complete all sections (100% profile)
├── Keyword-rich description
├── Custom button CTA
├── Featured content section
├── Regular posting schedule
└── Employee advocacy program

Content Strategy:
├── Optimal Length: 1,200-1,500 characters
├── Include 3-5 relevant hashtags
├── Tag relevant people/companies
├── Use native documents (carousels)
├── Video: 30-90 seconds optimal
└── Best Times: Tues-Thurs, 8-10am, 12pm

Post Types That Perform:
├── Personal stories and lessons
├── Industry insights with opinion
├── Data and statistics
├── Carousel documents (how-tos)
├── Native video content
├── Polls and questions
└── Employee spotlights

Algorithm Tips:
├── First hour engagement matters most
├── Comment depth > likes
├── Dwell time counts (longer posts)
├── External links reduce reach
├── Edit within 10 min, not after
└── Engage before and after posting
```

**Instagram Best Practices**

```
Profile Optimization:
├── Clear profile photo (logo or face)
├── Compelling bio with keywords
├── Link in bio (use link-in-bio tool)
├── Story highlights (organized)
├── Contact buttons enabled
└── Business/Creator account

Content Strategy:
├── Feed Posts: High-quality, cohesive aesthetic
├── Stories: Daily, behind-the-scenes, interactive
├── Reels: 15-60 seconds, trending audio
├── Carousels: Educational, save-worthy
├── Live: Q&As, launches, interviews
└── Best Times: 11am-1pm, 7-9pm

Content Types That Perform:
├── Reels (highest reach)
├── Carousel posts (high saves)
├── User-generated content
├── Behind-the-scenes
├── Interactive Stories
└── Educational content

Algorithm Tips:
├── Reels prioritized for reach
├── Engagement in first hour crucial
├── Saves and shares > likes
├── Respond to all comments
├── Use all features regularly
└── Hashtags: 3-5 relevant, specific
```

**X (Twitter) Best Practices**

```
Profile Optimization:
├── Clear profile photo
├── Compelling header image
├── Bio with keywords and personality
├── Pinned tweet (best content)
├── Location and website
└── Professional account features

Content Strategy:
├── Tweet Length: <280 chars optimal
├── Threads for longer content
├── Include images/videos when possible
├── Engage in conversations
├── Quote tweet with commentary
└── Best Times: 8-10am, 12-1pm weekdays

Post Types That Perform:
├── Threads (educational, stories)
├── Hot takes and opinions
├── Lists and frameworks
├── Questions and engagement
├── Visual content
├── Real-time commentary
└── Memes (brand-appropriate)

Algorithm Tips:
├── Replies and conversation boost reach
├── Posting frequency matters
├── Early engagement amplifies
├── Don't overuse hashtags (1-2 max)
├── Native content > links
└── Quote tweets > retweets
```

### Process 4: Community Engagement

**Engagement Framework**

```
Reactive Engagement (Responding):

Response Time Standards:
├── Questions: Within 1 hour
├── Complaints: Within 30 minutes
├── Praise: Within 2-4 hours
├── Tags/Mentions: Same day
└── DMs: Within 24 hours

Response Templates:

Positive Comment:
"Thank you so much, [Name]! We're thrilled to hear
[specific reference to their comment]. [Follow-up
question or invitation to continue engagement]"

Question:
"Great question, [Name]! [Direct answer]. [Additional
context if helpful]. Let us know if you have any other
questions!"

Complaint (Public):
"Hi [Name], we're sorry to hear about this experience.
We'd like to make this right. Please DM us with your
details so we can help resolve this quickly."

Negative Feedback:
"Thanks for sharing your feedback, [Name]. We hear you
and appreciate you taking the time to let us know.
[Acknowledge specific concern]. [What you're doing about
it or invitation to discuss further]."
```

**Proactive Engagement**

```
Daily Engagement Routine (30-60 min):

First 15 minutes:
├── Check and respond to all notifications
├── Reply to comments on recent posts
├── Answer any DMs
└── Address any urgent mentions

Next 15 minutes:
├── Engage with followers' content
├── Comment on relevant industry posts
├── Support customer/partner content
└── Share relevant UGC

Next 15 minutes:
├── Search and engage with hashtags
├── Participate in relevant conversations
├── Connect with potential customers
└── Engage with competitors' audiences (appropriately)

Final 15 minutes (if time):
├── Engage with industry influencers
├── Join relevant groups/discussions
├── Curate content to share
└── Identify trends and opportunities
```

### Process 5: Social Media Analytics

**Key Metrics by Goal**

```
Brand Awareness Metrics:
├── Reach: Total unique viewers
├── Impressions: Total views
├── Follower Growth: Net new followers
├── Share of Voice: Brand mentions vs. competitors
├── Brand Mentions: Tagged and untagged
└── Video Views: Total and unique

Engagement Metrics:
├── Engagement Rate: (Engagements / Reach) x 100
├── Likes/Reactions: Total and by type
├── Comments: Total and sentiment
├── Shares/Retweets: Amplification
├── Saves: Content value indicator
├── Click-Through Rate: Clicks / Impressions
└── Video Completion Rate: % watching to end

Conversion Metrics:
├── Click-Throughs: Visits to website
├── Leads Generated: Form fills from social
├── Conversions: Sales/signups from social
├── Cost Per Click (paid): Spend / Clicks
├── Cost Per Conversion: Spend / Conversions
└── Return on Ad Spend: Revenue / Ad Spend

Community Metrics:
├── Response Time: Average time to respond
├── Response Rate: % of comments addressed
├── Sentiment: Positive/negative ratio
├── Community Growth: Group/community size
└── Active Members: Engaged participants
```

### Process 6: Paid Social Integration

**Paid Social Strategy Framework**

```
When to Use Paid Social:

Content Amplification:
├── Boost high-performing organic posts
├── Reach beyond existing followers
└── Test content before large investment

Lead Generation:
├── Lead form ads (LinkedIn, Facebook)
├── Traffic to landing pages
├── Webinar/event promotion
└── Content downloads

Brand Awareness:
├── Reach campaigns
├── Video views
├── New audience targeting
└── Competitor audience targeting

Retargeting:
├── Website visitors
├── Engaged social users
├── Email list matching
└── Lookalike audiences
```

**Budget Allocation Guidelines**

```
Starter Budget ($500-2,000/month):
├── Focus on 1-2 platforms
├── Primarily retargeting
├── Boost top organic content
└── Test audiences before scaling

Growth Budget ($2,000-10,000/month):
├── Full-funnel campaigns
├── Multiple platforms
├── Regular testing and optimization
└── Attribution tracking

Scale Budget ($10,000+/month):
├── Sophisticated targeting
├── Creative testing at scale
├── Multi-touch attribution
└── Agency or dedicated resource
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Spreading Too Thin
**Problem**: Trying to maintain presence on every platform.
**Solution**: Focus on 2-3 platforms where your audience is most active. Do fewer platforms better.

### Pitfall 2: Inconsistent Posting
**Problem**: Sporadic posting kills momentum and algorithm favor.
**Solution**: Use content calendar and scheduling tools. Batch create content.

### Pitfall 3: Too Promotional
**Problem**: Only posting about your products/services.
**Solution**: Follow 80/20 rule. Provide value first, promote second.

### Pitfall 4: Ignoring Engagement
**Problem**: Posting but not responding or engaging.
**Solution**: Dedicate time daily for engagement. Respond to all comments and mentions.

### Pitfall 5: No Strategy
**Problem**: Posting without clear goals or plan.
**Solution**: Document strategy with goals, audience, content pillars. Review quarterly.

### Pitfall 6: Chasing Vanity Metrics
**Problem**: Focusing on followers instead of business results.
**Solution**: Track metrics that connect to business goals. Quality over quantity.

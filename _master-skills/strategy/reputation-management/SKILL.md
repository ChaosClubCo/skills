# Reputation Management

## Overview

Online reputation directly impacts customer acquisition and retention. For SMBs, a few negative reviews or a mishandled crisis can significantly damage business, while strong reputation management creates competitive advantage and builds trust. This skill covers proactive reputation building, monitoring, review management, and crisis response.

Reputation management is both defensive (protecting against damage) and offensive (building positive presence). The goal is creating a robust online presence where your company's story is told accurately and positive experiences are visible.

## When to Use

**Invoke this skill when:**
- Setting up review monitoring systems
- Responding to customer reviews (positive or negative)
- Handling negative publicity or crisis situations
- Building proactive reputation strategies
- Analyzing sentiment and brand perception
- Optimizing profiles on review platforms
- Creating crisis communication plans
- Tracking competitor reputation

**Trigger phrases:**
- "Monitor reviews"
- "Respond to negative review"
- "Crisis communication"
- "Reputation strategy"
- "Social listening"
- "Sentiment analysis"

#### Review Solicitation Strategy

```markdown
## Review Generation Framework

### When to Ask for Reviews
**Best Timing (High Success):**
- Immediately after successful implementation
- Following a support ticket with positive resolution
- After customer achieves measurable success
- During renewal when satisfaction confirmed
- Post-positive NPS or CSAT response

**Timing to Avoid:**
- During active support issues
- Right after pricing discussions
- When customer is behind on payment
- During known dissatisfaction

### Review Request Templates

**Email Request - Post Success**
Subject: Quick favor? Share your experience with [Product]

Hi [Name],

Congrats on [specific achievement with product]!

Would you mind taking 2 minutes to share your experience on [Platform]?
Reviews from customers like you help others discover [Company]
and help us improve.

[Direct link to review page]

No pressure at all - either way, we're thrilled you're seeing results!

Best,
[Name]

**In-App Prompt**
[After positive interaction]
"Enjoying [Product]? Help others discover us with a quick review!"
[Leave a Review] [Maybe Later] [Don't Ask Again]

**Post-Support Request**
[After ticket closed with positive rating]
"Thanks for the feedback! Would you share your experience on [Platform]?"
[Yes, I'll Review] [Not Right Now]

### Review Platform Guidance
When directing customers to review:
- Provide direct link to review page
- Don't incentivize reviews (violates most platform policies)
- Don't tell them what to write
- Do remind them of specific positive experiences
- Do make it easy with one-click access
```

### 2. Review Response Strategy

#### Response Framework

```markdown
## Review Response Guidelines

### Response Timing
| Review Type | Response Time | Priority |
|-------------|---------------|----------|
| Negative (1-2 stars) | Within 4 hours | Critical |
| Mixed (3 stars) | Within 24 hours | High |
| Positive (4-5 stars) | Within 48 hours | Standard |

### Negative Review Response Framework

**HEART Method:**
1. **H**ear - Acknowledge their experience
2. **E**mpathize - Show understanding of frustration
3. **A**pologize - Take responsibility appropriately
4. **R**esolve - Offer specific solution
5. **T**ake offline - Move detailed discussion private

### Response Templates

**Negative Review - Service Issue**
Hi [Name],

Thank you for sharing your feedback. I'm sorry to hear about
[specific issue mentioned]. That's not the experience we want
for our customers.

I'd like to understand more about what happened and make this
right. Please reach out to me directly at [email] so we can
resolve this for you.

[Your name]
[Title]

**Negative Review - Product Issue**
Hi [Name],

I appreciate you taking the time to share your experience.
I'm sorry [product] didn't meet your expectations regarding
[specific issue].

We take this feedback seriously. I'd like to learn more about
your situation and see how we can help. Please contact me at
[email] or [phone].

Thank you,
[Your name]

**Negative Review - Already Resolved**
Hi [Name],

Thank you for this feedback. I see that [team/person] worked
with you to resolve [issue] on [date].

If there's anything else we can do, please don't hesitate to
reach out to me directly at [email].

[Your name]

**Positive Review Response**
Thank you for the kind words, [Name]! We're thrilled that
[specific element they mentioned] is making a difference for
[their company/use case].

We appreciate you taking the time to share your experience!

[Your name]

**Mixed Review Response**
Hi [Name],

Thank you for sharing both what's working and where we can improve.
We're glad [positive aspect] is delivering value.

Regarding [concern mentioned], I'd love to learn more.
[Brief explanation if appropriate] Please reach out to me at
[email] so we can discuss further.

[Your name]
```

#### Escalation and Special Cases

```markdown
## Review Escalation Protocol

### Immediate Escalation (Notify leadership)
- Legal threats or mentions
- Discrimination or harassment allegations
- Data security or privacy concerns
- Regulatory compliance issues
- Media involvement mentioned
- Viral potential (high engagement)

### Standard Escalation (Department notification)
- Product defect claims → Product team
- Billing disputes → Finance team
- Employee behavior → HR
- Sales misrepresentation → Sales leadership
- Feature requests → Product feedback

### Response Authority Levels
| Scenario | Authority Level | Approval Required |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Standard negative | CS team | No |
| Legal mention | Leadership | Yes |
| Offer compensation | Manager | Yes |
| Factual correction | Legal review | Yes |
| Policy explanation | CS team | No |

### Reviews to Not Respond To
- Competitor trolling (flag/report instead)
- Spam or fake reviews (report to platform)
- Reviews violating platform policy (flag)
- Personal attacks on employees (report, escalate)
- Ongoing legal matters (consult legal first)
```

#### Sentiment Analysis

```markdown
## Sentiment Tracking Framework

### Manual Sentiment Scoring
For each mention, categorize:

**Positive (+1)**
- Praise, recommendation, success story
- Thank you messages
- Feature highlights
- Positive comparison to competitors

**Neutral (0)**
- Questions about product
- Factual mentions (news, etc.)
- Comparison without clear preference
- Feature discussions

**Negative (-1)**
- Complaints, frustration
- Bug reports with negative tone
- Unfavorable comparisons
- Churn mentions

### Sentiment Dashboard Template

| Period | Total Mentions | Positive | Neutral | Negative | Net Sentiment |
|--------|----------------|----------|---------|----------|---------------|
| This Week | [X] | [X] | [X] | [X] | [Score] |
| Last Week | [X] | [X] | [X] | [X] | [Score] |
| This Month | [X] | [X] | [X] | [X] | [Score] |

### Net Sentiment Score
(Positive - Negative) / Total = Net Sentiment
- >0.5: Strong positive
- 0.2-0.5: Positive
- -0.2-0.2: Neutral
- -0.5-(-0.2): Negative
- <-0.5: Strong negative

### Trend Analysis
Track week-over-week and month-over-month:
- Mention volume changes
- Sentiment shifts
- Topic emergence
- Platform distribution
- Competitor comparison
```

### 4. Crisis Response

#### Crisis Preparation

```markdown
## Crisis Response Playbook

### Crisis Definition
A crisis is any event that could significantly damage reputation:
- Data breach or security incident
- Product failure affecting multiple customers
- Negative press coverage
- Social media viral moment (negative)
- Employee misconduct made public
- Legal action or regulatory issue
- Customer harm or safety issue

### Crisis Response Team
| Role | Responsibility | Primary | Backup |
|------|----------------|---------|--------|
| Crisis Lead | Decision-making, coordination | [Name] | [Name] |
| Communications | Messaging, media | [Name] | [Name] |
| Legal | Liability, compliance | [Name] | [Name] |
| Technical | Product/system issues | [Name] | [Name] |
| Customer | Customer communication | [Name] | [Name] |
| Executive | Approval, spokesperson | [Name] | [Name] |

### Crisis Severity Levels

**Level 1: Minor**
- Isolated incident, limited visibility
- No media involvement
- Contained to single customer/case
- Response: Standard escalation, monitoring

**Level 2: Moderate**
- Multiple customers affected
- Some social media attention
- Potential for spread
- Response: Team assembled, proactive communication

**Level 3: Major**
- Widespread impact
- Media coverage or inquiry
- Viral social media
- Response: Full crisis protocol activated

**Level 4: Critical**
- Existential threat to business
- Major media coverage
- Regulatory or legal involvement
- Response: All hands, executive leadership
```

#### Crisis Response Protocol

```markdown
## Crisis Response Checklist

### First 60 Minutes (Golden Hour)
- [ ] Assess: Gather facts about what happened
- [ ] Assemble: Alert crisis response team
- [ ] Acknowledge: Post holding statement if public
- [ ] Assign: Designate single spokesperson
- [ ] Action: Begin technical response if needed

### Holding Statement Template
"We're aware of [general description of issue] and are actively
investigating. We take this seriously and will provide updates
as we learn more. [If applicable: Customer data/safety is our
priority.]"

### Hours 2-4
- [ ] Develop full understanding of situation
- [ ] Draft detailed response/statement
- [ ] Legal review of all communications
- [ ] Prepare FAQ for customer-facing teams
- [ ] Set up monitoring for escalation
- [ ] Executive briefing and approval

### Response Statement Template
"On [date], [what happened - factual, brief]. We [action taken].
[Number] of [customers/users] were affected.

We have [steps taken to resolve]. We are [ongoing actions].

We apologize for [impact on customers]. [Commitment to do better].

If you have questions or concerns, please contact [support channel].

[Update schedule: We will provide updates every X hours until resolved.]"

### Ongoing Management
- [ ] Regular update schedule (every 2-4 hours initially)
- [ ] Monitor social media and news
- [ ] Track customer inquiries and common questions
- [ ] Document all actions and decisions
- [ ] Prepare for media inquiries

### Post-Crisis
- [ ] Final resolution announcement
- [ ] Internal debrief and documentation
- [ ] Root cause analysis
- [ ] Process improvements identified
- [ ] Follow-up with affected customers
- [ ] Monitor for residual reputation impact
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Ignoring Negative Reviews
**Problem**: Unanswered negative reviews signal you don't care
**Solution**: Respond to every review; negative reviews are opportunities

### Pitfall 2: Defensive Responses
**Problem**: Arguing with reviewers damages reputation further
**Solution**: Lead with empathy; take discussion offline; never argue publicly

### Pitfall 3: Fake Reviews
**Problem**: Buying or incentivizing fake reviews
**Solution**: Violates platform policies, destroys trust when discovered; focus on genuine reviews

### Pitfall 4: No Crisis Plan
**Problem**: Making decisions in the heat of a crisis
**Solution**: Document protocols before you need them; practice scenarios

### Pitfall 5: Reactive Only
**Problem**: Only managing reputation when problems arise
**Solution**: Proactive reputation building; consistent positive content

### Pitfall 6: Slow Response
**Problem**: Taking days to respond to reputation threats
**Solution**: Real-time monitoring; clear escalation paths; empowered responders

# Customer Segmentation

## Overview

Customer segmentation is the strategic practice of dividing markets into distinct groups of customers with similar characteristics, needs, and behaviors. This skill enables organizations to focus resources on the most valuable opportunities and tailor their approach for maximum effectiveness.

Effective segmentation transforms broad market opportunities into targeted, actionable strategies that improve conversion rates, increase customer lifetime value, and optimize go-to-market investments.

### Core Components

1. **Segment Identification**: Discovering meaningful customer groupings
2. **ICP Development**: Defining ideal customer profiles
3. **Persona Creation**: Building buyer archetypes
4. **Segment Prioritization**: Ranking segment attractiveness
5. **Targeting Strategy**: Resource allocation by segment
6. **Segment Evolution**: Continuous refinement

## When to Use

### Primary Scenarios

- **Go-to-Market Planning**: Focusing launch efforts
- **Sales Territory Design**: Allocating sales resources
- **Marketing Campaign Planning**: Targeting optimization
- **Product Development**: Feature prioritization by segment
- **Pricing Strategy**: Value-based pricing by segment
- **Customer Success**: Tailored engagement models

### Trigger Indicators

- Low conversion rates despite lead volume
- One-size-fits-all messaging underperforming
- Sales pursuing unqualified opportunities
- High churn in certain customer types
- Product roadmap lacking customer focus
- Resource allocation debates

## Common Pitfalls

### Segmentation Failures to Avoid

1. **Over-Segmentation**: Too many segments to execute
   - *Mitigation*: Limit to 3-5 actionable segments

2. **Demographic-Only Focus**: Ignoring behavioral factors
   - *Mitigation*: Include needs and behavior variables

3. **Static Segmentation**: Not updating with market changes
   - *Mitigation*: Quarterly validation cycles

4. **Unactionable Segments**: Can't be targeted effectively
   - *Mitigation*: Test reachability before finalizing

5. **Internal Disagreement**: Different teams using different segmentation
   - *Mitigation*: Organization-wide segment definitions

# Event Management

## Overview

Events are high-touch opportunities to build relationships, demonstrate expertise, and generate leads. For SMBs, well-executed events can create outsized impact relative to investment, whether hosting webinars, sponsoring conferences, or exhibiting at trade shows. This skill covers the complete event lifecycle from planning through post-event conversion.

Strategic event management balances brand awareness goals with lead generation objectives, creating memorable experiences that move prospects through the funnel while strengthening relationships with existing customers.

## When to Use

**Invoke this skill when:**
- Planning a webinar or virtual event
- Preparing for trade show or conference participation
- Organizing customer or partner conferences
- Coordinating speaker logistics and content
- Building event promotion strategies
- Managing registration and attendee experience
- Developing post-event follow-up sequences
- Calculating event ROI and optimization

**Trigger phrases:**
- "Plan a webinar"
- "Prepare for trade show"
- "Event logistics checklist"
- "Attendee management"

#### Booth Design & Staffing

```markdown
## Booth Strategy

### Booth Design Principles
1. **Stop traffic**: Eye-catching visual from 20+ feet
2. **Communicate quickly**: Key message visible in 3 seconds
3. **Enable conversation**: Open design, no barriers
4. **Demonstrate product**: Interactive demo station
5. **Capture leads**: Easy scan/entry point

### Booth Size Guidelines
| Booth Size | Best For | Staff Needed |
|------------|----------|--------------|
| 10x10 | Awareness, smaller shows | 2-3 people |
| 10x20 | Lead gen, demos | 3-5 people |
| 20x20 | Major presence, meetings | 5-8 people |
| Island | Flagship, large shows | 8-15 people |

### Staff Roles
**Lead Qualifier**: First contact, quick qualification
- "What brings you to the show?"
- "What challenges are you looking to solve?"

**Demo Specialist**: Deep dives with qualified prospects
- Technical questions, custom scenarios

**Senior Exec**: High-value meetings, partnership discussions
- C-level conversations, closing

### Booth Schedule Template
| Time | Position 1 | Position 2 | Position 3 | Break |
|------|------------|------------|------------|-------|
| 9-10 | [Name] | [Name] | [Name] | - |
| 10-11 | [Name] | [Name] | - | [Name] |
| 11-12 | [Name] | - | [Name] | [Name] |
...

### Engagement Tactics
- Live demo schedule (every 30 min)
- Raffle for badge scans
- Interactive game/quiz
- Charging station
- Refreshments
- Photo opportunity
```

#### Lead Capture Best Practices

```markdown
## Lead Capture System

### Capture Methods
| Method | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| Badge scan | Fast, low friction | Limited data | High volume |
| Paper forms | No tech issues | Manual entry | Backup |
| App + notes | Rich data | Slower | Quality convos |
| Business cards | Familiar | Manual entry | Relationship building |

### Lead Qualification Framework
Rate each lead (use simple A/B/C or numeric):

**A Lead (Hot)**
- Budget: Confirmed
- Authority: Decision maker present
- Need: Active project
- Timeline: <6 months
Action: Sales follow-up within 24 hours

**B Lead (Warm)**
- Interest confirmed
- Some criteria met
- Researching/evaluating
Action: Follow-up within 1 week

**C Lead (Cool)**
- Information seeker
- No clear timeline
- May be competitor
Action: Add to nurture

### Capture Data Points
Required:
- Name, email, company
- Lead rating (A/B/C)
- Key pain point or interest

Optional but valuable:
- Current solution
- Buying timeline
- Follow-up preference
- Specific questions asked

### Staff Notes Template
"[Rating] - [Name] from [Company]. Interested in [topic].
Challenge: [pain point]. Timeline: [when].
Follow-up: [specific action needed]"
```

#### Production Checklist

```markdown
## Virtual Event Technical Setup

### Pre-Event (1 week before)
Equipment:
- [ ] Camera tested (webcam or professional)
- [ ] Microphone tested (USB mic preferred over laptop)
- [ ] Lighting checked (ring light or natural)
- [ ] Background set (clean, branded, or virtual)

Platform:
- [ ] Host and speaker accounts active
- [ ] Registration page live and tested
- [ ] Integration with CRM confirmed
- [ ] Recording settings configured
- [ ] Branding/logos uploaded

### Rehearsal (2-3 days before)
- [ ] Full run-through with all speakers
- [ ] Screen share tested
- [ ] Slides reviewed for virtual display
- [ ] Polls and Q&A tested
- [ ] Backup presenter assigned
- [ ] Timing confirmed

### Day Of Event
**60 min before:**
- [ ] Platform green room open
- [ ] All speakers online
- [ ] Final tech check
- [ ] Slides loaded
- [ ] Chat/Q&A moderation ready

**15 min before:**
- [ ] Recording started
- [ ] Waiting room open
- [ ] Music/holding slide active

**During event:**
- [ ] Producer monitoring chat
- [ ] Time checks for speakers
- [ ] Q&A collection for live session
- [ ] Technical issues handled

**Post-event:**
- [ ] Recording stopped and saved
- [ ] Thank you message sent
- [ ] Analytics pulled
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Over-Promoting, Under-Delivering
**Problem**: Registration volume but low attendance and engagement
**Solution**: Focus on value promise; deliver on it; quality over quantity

### Pitfall 2: No Clear CTA
**Problem**: Great event, no next step
**Solution**: Plan post-event journey before event; make CTA clear

### Pitfall 3: Tech Failures
**Problem**: Platform issues derail the event
**Solution**: Rehearse, have backups, arrive early, designate tech support

### Pitfall 4: Speaker-Centric (Not Audience-Centric)
**Problem**: Talking AT attendees instead of engaging them
**Solution**: Interactive elements, polls, Q&A, stories over slides

### Pitfall 5: Poor Lead Follow-Up
**Problem**: Leads generated but never worked
**Solution**: Pre-plan follow-up sequences; establish SLAs; automate where possible

### Pitfall 6: Vanity Metrics Focus
**Problem**: Celebrating registrations instead of pipeline
**Solution**: Track full-funnel metrics; attribute revenue

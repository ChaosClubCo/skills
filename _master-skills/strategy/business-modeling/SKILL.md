# Business Modeling

## Overview

Business modeling is the discipline of designing and validating how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value. This skill encompasses the development of revenue models, unit economics analysis, pricing strategies, and financial viability assessments that form the foundation of sustainable business operations.

Effective business modeling transforms product capabilities into profitable business outcomes. It ensures that customer value translates into economic value, that growth is financially sustainable, and that strategic decisions are grounded in sound economic principles.

### Core Components

1. **Business Model Design**: Value creation and capture framework
2. **Revenue Model Architecture**: Monetization strategy selection
3. **Unit Economics Analysis**: Per-customer profitability assessment
4. **Pricing Strategy**: Value-based price optimization
5. **Cost Structure Mapping**: Fixed and variable cost analysis
6. **Financial Viability**: Break-even and profitability modeling

## When to Use

### Primary Scenarios

- **New Venture Launch**: Designing initial business model
- **Product Monetization**: Transitioning from free to paid
- **Pricing Changes**: Updating pricing structure or levels
- **Market Expansion**: Adapting model for new segments
- **Investor Preparation**: Building financial projections
- **Strategic Pivot**: Fundamental model redesign

### Trigger Indicators

- Customer acquisition costs exceeding targets
- Unclear path to profitability
- Pricing objections in sales process
- Competitive pricing pressure
- Margin erosion over time
- Investor questions about unit economics

### Timing Considerations

| Activity | Frequency | Stakeholders |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Full Model Review | Annual | Leadership team |
| Unit Economics Update | Quarterly | Finance + Product |
| Pricing Analysis | Semi-annual | Product + Sales |
| Sensitivity Testing | Major decisions | Finance + Strategy |

### Phase 3: Unit Economics Analysis

#### Core Unit Economics Metrics

```markdown
## Essential Metrics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend / New Customers Acquired

### Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
LTV = ARPU x Gross Margin % x Customer Lifetime

### LTV:CAC Ratio
Target: 3:1 or higher
- <1:1 = Unsustainable, losing money
- 1:1-3:1 = Challenging, need improvement
- 3:1-5:1 = Healthy, efficient growth
- >5:1 = May be under-investing in growth

### CAC Payback Period
Months to Recover CAC = CAC / (Monthly ARPU x Gross Margin %)
Target: <12 months for SMB, <18 months for Enterprise
```

### Phase 4: Pricing Strategy Development

#### Value-Based Pricing Framework

```markdown
## Value Quantification Process

### Step 1: Identify Value Drivers
- Time savings (hours x hourly cost)
- Revenue increase (% lift x baseline)
- Cost reduction (before vs. after)
- Risk mitigation (probability x impact)

### Step 2: Quantify Customer Value
| Value Driver | Calculation | Annual Value |
|--------------|-------------|--------------|
| Time Savings | 10 hrs/wk x $50/hr x 52 | $26,000 |
| Revenue Lift | 5% x $500K baseline | $25,000 |
| Cost Reduction | $2K/mo x 12 | $24,000 |
| Total Quantified Value | | $75,000 |

### Step 3: Set Price Based on Value
Value-Based Price = Total Value x Value Capture %
Typical capture rate: 10-25% of quantified value
Example: $75,000 x 15% = $11,250/year price point
```

#### Pricing Psychology Principles

```markdown
## Behavioral Pricing Tactics

### Anchoring
- Show highest tier first
- Display "most popular" option
- Include enterprise tier for contrast

### Decoy Effect
- Include asymmetrically dominated option
- Make middle tier most attractive
- Guide toward target tier

### Price Framing
- Per day vs. per month vs. per year
- Comparison to alternatives
- ROI payback messaging

### Psychological Price Points
- $99 vs. $100 (charm pricing)
- $997 vs. $1,000 (professional services)
- Round numbers for enterprise deals
```

### Phase 5: Financial Viability Modeling

#### Break-Even Analysis

```markdown
## Break-Even Calculation

### Fixed Costs (Monthly)
- Salaries and benefits: $X
- Rent and facilities: $Y
- Software and tools: $Z
- Total Fixed: $A

### Variable Costs (Per Unit)
- COGS: $B
- Commissions: $C
- Support: $D
- Total Variable: $E per customer

### Break-Even Point
Units = Fixed Costs / (Price - Variable Costs)
Revenue = Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin %
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Modeling Failures to Avoid

1. **Fantasy Economics**: Assumptions disconnected from reality
   - *Mitigation*: Validate with real customer data

2. **Ignoring CAC**: Underestimating acquisition costs
   - *Mitigation*: Full-funnel cost accounting

3. **Vanity Pricing**: Pricing based on competitor copying
   - *Mitigation*: Value-based pricing approach

4. **Fixed Cost Blindness**: Ignoring overhead allocation
   - *Mitigation*: Full cost modeling

5. **One-Size Pricing**: Same price for all segments
   - *Mitigation*: Segment-specific value pricing

6. **Churn Optimism**: Underestimating customer loss
   - *Mitigation*: Conservative retention assumptions

7. **Linear Projections**: Assuming steady growth
   - *Mitigation*: S-curve and scenario modeling

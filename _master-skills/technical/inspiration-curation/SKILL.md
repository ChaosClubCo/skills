# Inspiration Curation Skill

> Master creative reference collection with systematic curation, organization, and sharing of visual and conceptual inspiration to fuel creative excellence and team alignment.

## Overview

Inspiration curation is the systematic collection, organization, and application of creative references to inform and inspire creative work. This skill enables creative teams to build and maintain rich reference libraries, develop compelling moodboards, and ensure creative decisions are informed by relevant precedents and possibilities. It transforms inspiration from individual, ad-hoc activity into a strategic, shared creative resource.

## Why This Matters

### For Creative Teams
- Provides rich reference material for creative development
- Enables shared creative language and vision alignment
- Reduces creative blocks through diverse inspiration
- Builds institutional creative knowledge over time

### For Project Quality
- Elevates creative ambition through benchmark exposure
- Ensures awareness of category and cross-category excellence
- Informs stakeholder conversations with visual reference
- Reduces miscommunication through shared reference points

### For Creative Culture
- Cultivates continuous learning and curiosity
- Democratizes access to creative inspiration
- Encourages cross-disciplinary thinking
- Builds creative community through sharing

## When to Use

### Primary Triggers
- New project or campaign kickoff
- Creative brief development support
- Stakeholder alignment and presentation
- Team creative development sessions
- New team member onboarding
- Capability building initiatives

### Use This Skill When
- Need to establish visual direction
- Building moodboards for stakeholder alignment
- Researching creative precedents for a challenge
- Looking for innovative approaches to brief
- Developing creative rationale and references
- Training or inspiring creative teams

### Do Not Use When
- Conducting competitive analysis (use design-research)
- Tracking cultural trends (use trend-spotting)
- Testing creative concepts (use creative-testing)
- Measuring content performance (use content-performance)

## Common Pitfalls

### Collection Mistakes

```yaml
collection_pitfalls:
  hoarding_without_curation:
    problem: "Saving everything without filtering"
    impact: "Unusable library, needle in haystack"
    solution: "Regular pruning, quality threshold"

  echo_chamber_collection:
    problem: "Only collecting similar aesthetic"
    impact: "Limited inspiration range, predictable output"
    solution: "Intentional diversity, cross-discipline exploration"

  no_organization_system:
    problem: "Random collection without structure"
    impact: "Can't find when needed, lost inspiration"
    solution: "Consistent tagging, folder structure"

  stale_library:
    problem: "Not updating collection over time"
    impact: "Outdated references, missed evolution"
    solution: "Regular curation rhythm, freshness focus"
```

### Application Mistakes

```yaml
application_pitfalls:
  literal_interpretation:
    problem: "Copying references directly"
    impact: "Derivative work, potential legal issues"
    solution: "Extract principles not executions"

  moodboard_overload:
    problem: "Too many images, no focus"
    impact: "Confused stakeholders, unclear direction"
    solution: "Ruthless curation, clear hierarchy"

  reference_mismatch:
    problem: "Inspiration doesn't match project context"
    impact: "Unrealistic expectations, misalignment"
    solution: "Context-appropriate selection"

  no_annotation:
    problem: "Sharing references without context"
    impact: "Misinterpretation, missed intent"
    solution: "Explain why not just what"
```

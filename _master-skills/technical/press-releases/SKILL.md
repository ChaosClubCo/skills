# Press Release & Media Relations

## Overview

Effective media relations amplify your company's reach far beyond paid advertising budgets. For SMBs, a well-crafted press release can generate coverage that builds credibility, drives traffic, and establishes thought leadership. This skill covers the complete media relations lifecycle from announcement strategy through journalist relationship building.

Press releases remain a cornerstone of B2B communications, providing journalists with the structured information they need while giving your company control over the initial narrative. When combined with proactive media outreach and relationship building, they become a powerful growth lever.

## When to Use

**Invoke this skill when:**
- Launching a new product, feature, or service
- Announcing funding, acquisition, or major partnership
- Sharing significant company milestones
- Responding to industry news or trends
- Managing crisis communications
- Building relationships with industry journalists
- Preparing executives for media interviews
- Creating press kits for events or ongoing media needs
- Planning announcement timing and embargo strategies

**Trigger phrases:**
- "Write a press release"
- "Plan media announcement"
- "Create press kit"
- "Prepare for journalist interview"
- "Media outreach strategy"
- "Embargo management"

## Core Processes

### 1. Press Release Writing

#### Standard Press Release Structure

```markdown
# Press Release Template

## Header Section
**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE** (or **EMBARGOED UNTIL [Date, Time, Timezone]**)

**Media Contact:**
[Name]
[Title]
[Email]
[Phone]

## Headline
[Action verb] + [Company] + [Key news] + [Benefit/Impact]
Maximum 10-12 words, title case

## Subheadline (optional)
[Additional context or secondary angle]
Sentence case, expands on headline

## Dateline & Lead Paragraph
[CITY, State] - [Month Day, Year] - [Company name] today announced [news].
[One sentence explaining what, why it matters, and to whom]

## Body Paragraph 1: The Details
[Expand on the news with specific details]
[Include relevant numbers, dates, availability]
[Answer: What exactly is being announced?]

## Body Paragraph 2: The Context
[Why this matters to the industry/customers]
[Market context or trend this addresses]
[Answer: Why should anyone care?]

## Quote 1: Executive Perspective
"[Quote from CEO/founder about vision and significance]," said
[Name], [Title] of [Company]. "[Second sentence adding color]."

## Body Paragraph 3: Supporting Details
[Technical specifications, pricing, availability]
[Partnership details if applicable]
[Timeline or rollout information]

## Quote 2: Customer/Partner Voice (optional)
"[Quote from customer or partner validating the news]," said
[Name], [Title] of [Partner/Customer Company].

## Boilerplate
**About [Company]**
[Company] [what you do] for [who you serve]. Founded in [year] and
headquartered in [location], [Company] [key differentiator or achievement].
For more information, visit [website].

## Closing
###

[Optional: Link to press kit, images, or additional resources]
```

#### Quote Writing Best Practices

```markdown
## Crafting Effective Quotes

### Quote Formula
Sentence 1: [Emotional/visionary statement about significance]
Sentence 2: [Specific detail or forward-looking commitment]

### Good Quote Characteristics
- Sounds like a human actually said it
- Adds perspective not available elsewhere in release
- Demonstrates passion, vision, or expertise
- Is specific rather than generic

### Quote Templates by Role

**CEO/Founder (Vision)**
"[This news] represents [milestone] in our mission to [company vision].
[Specific commitment or what this means for customers]."

**Product Leader (Details)**
"[Product] was built from the ground up to [solve specific problem].
With [key feature], [target audience] can now [specific outcome]."

**Customer (Validation)**
"Since implementing [product/service], we've seen [specific result].
[Company] has been a true partner in [achieving outcome]."

**Partner (Collaboration)**
"Combining [our capability] with [partner capability] creates
[unique value]. Together, we're [industry impact statement]."

### Quotes to Avoid
- "We're excited to announce..." (overused, low value)
- Generic superlatives without specifics
- Jargon-heavy statements
- Anything that sounds like legal wrote it
```

### 2. Media Relations Strategy

#### Journalist Research & Targeting

```markdown
## Media List Building Process

### Step 1: Define Coverage Goals
- Industry/trade publications (highest conversion)
- Business press (broader reach, harder to land)
- Local/regional media (good for location news)
- Podcasts and newsletters (growing influence)
- Analyst/influencer coverage (industry credibility)

### Step 2: Research Relevant Journalists
For each target publication:
- Who covers your industry/beat?
- What have they written recently?
- What topics interest them? (check Twitter/LinkedIn)
- What's their preferred contact method?
- Have they covered competitors?

### Step 3: Build Media Database
| Name | Outlet | Beat | Email | Twitter | Notes | Last Contact |
|------|--------|------|-------|---------|-------|--------------|
| [Name] | [Pub] | [Beat] | [Email] | [@handle] | [Interests] | [Date] |

### Step 4: Tier Your List
Tier 1: Perfect fit, high priority (personalized pitch)
Tier 2: Good fit, medium priority (customized pitch)
Tier 3: General relevance (standard pitch)

### Research Tools
- Publication mastheads and editorial calendars
- Twitter lists of industry journalists
- LinkedIn company pages for publications
- Muck Rack, Cision, or similar PR databases
- Previous coverage of competitors
```

```markdown
Subject: [Specific hook relevant to their beat]

### Follow-Up Template (3-5 days later)
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Hi [First name],

Wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. Happy to share more
details or adjust the angle if helpful.

[One new detail or different hook that might interest them]

[Your name]

### Exclusive Offer Template
Subject: Exclusive: [Compelling hook]

Hi [First name],

I have a story I think you'd want first. [Brief description].

[Why this is exclusive-worthy: major news, surprising data,
industry first].

Would you be interested in an exclusive with [X hours/days]
head start? I can have [CEO/expert] available for an interview
[proposed times].

Let me know by [deadline] so I can plan accordingly.

[Your name]
```

### 3. Press Kit Creation

#### Press Kit Components

```markdown
## Press Kit Structure

### Essential Elements
1. **Company Overview** (1 page)
   - What you do (2-3 sentences)
   - Key facts (founded, HQ, employees, funding)
   - Mission statement
   - Key products/services

2. **Fact Sheet** (1 page)
   - Company vitals in scannable format
   - Key metrics and milestones
   - Leadership team with titles
   - Contact information

3. **Executive Bios** (1 paragraph each)
   - Name, title, role
   - Relevant background
   - Notable achievements
   - Available for interviews on [topics]

4. **High-Resolution Images**
   - Logo (various formats and backgrounds)
   - Executive headshots
   - Product screenshots/photos
   - Office/team photos
   - All at minimum 300 DPI, multiple sizes

5. **Boilerplate** (3 lengths)
   - Short (50 words): For tight spaces
   - Medium (100 words): Standard use
   - Long (200 words): Full context

### Optional Elements
6. **Recent Press Releases** (last 6-12 months)
7. **Media Coverage Highlights** (logos + links)
8. **Customer Testimonials** (with permission)
9. **Product Demos/Videos** (or links)
10. **Industry Awards/Recognition**
11. **Key Statistics/Research** (citable data)
12. **FAQ Document** (common journalist questions)

### Press Kit Delivery
- Hosted press page on website
- Downloadable ZIP file option
- Google Drive/Dropbox folder for easy access
- Physical kit for events (branded folder)
```

#### Executive Bio Framework

```markdown
## Executive Bio Template

### Structure
[Name] is [Title] of [Company], where [he/she/they] [primary
responsibility in one phrase].

[1-2 sentences on relevant background and expertise that
establishes credibility for this role.]

[Optional: Notable achievement, publication, speaking, board role]

[Name] [personal detail: location, education, hobby - humanizing].

### Example
Sarah Chen is CEO and co-founder of TechCorp, where she leads
the company's mission to democratize data analytics for small
businesses.

Prior to TechCorp, Sarah spent eight years at Salesforce, most
recently as VP of Product for their SMB division. She holds an
MBA from Stanford and a BS in Computer Science from MIT.

Sarah has been recognized as one of Forbes' 30 Under 30 in
Enterprise Technology and serves on the board of Girls Who Code.

She lives in San Francisco with her family and unreasonably
large vinyl record collection.

### Bio Versions
- **Full** (200 words): Press kits, speaker intros
- **Medium** (75 words): Bylines, panel descriptions
- **Short** (25 words): Quick references, limited space
- **Tweet-length**: "[Name] is [Title] at [Company]. [One key fact]."
```

### 4. Embargo Management

#### Embargo Best Practices

```markdown
## Embargo Protocol

### Setting an Embargo
1. Clearly state embargo terms in writing
2. Get explicit acknowledgment from journalist
3. Provide enough lead time (typically 48-72 hours minimum)
4. Include timezone in all communications
5. Have assets ready before embargo outreach begins

### Embargo Communication Template
Subject: EMBARGOED: [Brief description of news]

Hi [Name],

The following information is embargoed until [Day], [Date] at
[Time] [Timezone].

By reading beyond this point, you agree to honor the embargo.

[Press release or news summary]

Assets available:
- [List of available materials]

Interview availability:
- [CEO/Spokesperson] is available [times] for briefings

Please confirm receipt and acceptance of embargo terms.

[Your name]

### Embargo Tracking
| Outlet | Contact | Sent | Confirmed | Briefing Scheduled | Story Status |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| [Pub] | [Name] | [Date] | [Y/N] | [Date/time] | [Planning/Writing/No] |

### Embargo Break Protocol
If embargo is broken:
1. Assess: Intentional or accident?
2. Contact the outlet immediately
3. Decide: Lift embargo early for all or continue?
4. Communicate decision to all embargoed journalists
5. Document incident for future reference
```

### 5. Media Interview Preparation

#### Executive Media Training

```markdown
## Interview Preparation Framework

### Before the Interview
1. **Understand the outlet and journalist**
   - Recent articles they've written
   - Publication's audience and tone
   - Potential angles they might pursue

2. **Define your objectives**
   - 3 key messages to communicate
   - Specific proof points to include
   - Desired headline outcome

3. **Prepare for tough questions**
   - Competitive questions
   - Financial/performance questions
   - Controversy or criticism
   - "What if" scenarios

### Key Message Development
Message 1: [Main point - stated in memorable way]
- Proof point A
- Proof point B
- Bridge phrase: "The key thing is..."

Message 2: [Secondary point]
- Proof point A
- Proof point B
- Bridge phrase: "What's most important..."

Message 3: [Third point or call to action]
- Proof point A
- Proof point B
- Bridge phrase: "What I want people to understand..."

### Bridging Techniques
When asked an off-topic or difficult question:
- "That's a common question, and what's most relevant is..."
- "I understand why you'd ask that. Let me put it in context..."
- "The real story here is..."
- "What our customers tell us is..."
- "I'd frame it differently..."

### Interview Don'ts
- Never say "no comment" (explain why you can't discuss)
- Don't repeat negative framing in your answer
- Avoid "off the record" (assume everything is on record)
- Don't speculate about competitors or future events
- Never lie or mislead
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Announcement Fatigue
**Problem**: Every minor update becomes a press release
**Solution**: Reserve releases for genuinely newsworthy events; use blog posts for smaller updates

### Pitfall 2: Generic Mass Pitching
**Problem**: Same pitch to every journalist
**Solution**: Tier your list, personalize pitches, focus on relevance to their beat

### Pitfall 3: Buried Lead
**Problem**: Key news isn't clear in first paragraph
**Solution**: Assume they only read the first sentence; make it count

### Pitfall 4: Quote Stuffing
**Problem**: Too many quotes that add no value
**Solution**: 1-2 quotes maximum, each adding unique perspective

### Pitfall 5: Ignoring Newsworthiness
**Problem**: Self-promotional content with no news value
**Solution**: Ask "Why would anyone outside our company care about this today?"

### Pitfall 6: Poor Timing
**Problem**: Announcing during major news events or bad days
**Solution**: Monitor news cycle, avoid Fridays and holidays, watch for conflicts

### Pitfall 7: No Follow-Up Plan
**Problem**: Release distributed, then nothing
**Solution**: Plan post-distribution outreach, interview availability, social amplification

- Internal announcement
- Website news update

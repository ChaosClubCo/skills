# Partnership Development

## Overview

Strategic partnerships allow SMBs to expand reach, access new markets, and add value without building everything in-house. Well-structured partnerships create mutual benefit through shared audiences, complementary capabilities, and combined resources. This skill covers the partnership lifecycle from opportunity identification through program scaling.

For SMBs, partnerships can be a force multiplier - accessing enterprise accounts through larger partners, reaching new audiences through co-marketing, or adding capabilities through integrations. The key is creating clear value exchange and operational excellence in execution.

## When to Use

**Invoke this skill when:**
- Identifying potential partnership opportunities
- Structuring partnership agreements
- Creating co-marketing campaigns
- Building referral programs
- Enabling and training partners
- Developing joint value propositions
- Managing partner relationships
- Scaling partner channels

**Trigger phrases:**

| Annual Revenue | Discount | Support Level |

### 4. Referral Program Design

#### Referral Program Structure

```markdown
## Referral Program Blueprint

### Program Tiers

**Tier 1: Casual Referrer**
Commitment: Ad-hoc referrals, no minimum
Benefits:
- [X]% commission on closed deals
- Partner directory listing
- Referral tracking portal

**Tier 2: Active Partner**
Commitment: 2+ referrals per quarter
Benefits:
- [X]% commission (higher than Tier 1)
- Co-marketing opportunities
- Quarterly partner call
- Early access to features

**Tier 3: Strategic Partner**
Commitment: 5+ referrals per quarter
Benefits:
- [X]% commission (highest tier)
- Dedicated partner manager
- Joint marketing budget
- Product roadmap input
- Customer success priority

### Referral Process
1. Partner submits referral via portal/form
2. Referral acknowledged within 24 hours
3. Sales team follows up within 48 hours
4. Partner notified of status changes
5. Commission paid within 30 days of closed deal

### Referral Qualification
For referral to be accepted:
- [ ] New prospect (not in system)
- [ ] Contact information complete
- [ ] Basic qualification shared
- [ ] Partner introduction made
- [ ] Prospect expecting contact

### Commission Structure
| Deal Size | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|-----------|--------|--------|--------|
| <$10K ACV | 10% | 15% | 20% |
| $10-50K ACV | 10% | 15% | 20% |
| $50K+ ACV | 10% | 15% | 20% |

**Payment Terms:**
- Paid after customer's first payment received
- Net 30 payment via [method]
- Monthly reporting on referral status
- Annual 1099 provided (US partners)
```

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: One-Sided Value
**Problem**: Partnership only benefits one party
**Solution**: Clearly define and balance value exchange; regular check-ins on mutual benefit

### Pitfall 2: Launch and Abandon
**Problem**: Great launch, no ongoing investment
**Solution**: Build partnership management into ongoing roles; schedule regular touchpoints

### Pitfall 3: Over-Promising, Under-Delivering
**Problem**: Commitments made that can't be kept
**Solution**: Start small, prove value, scale gradually; under-promise, over-deliver

### Pitfall 4: Unclear Attribution
**Problem**: Disputes over lead/revenue credit
**Solution**: Define attribution rules clearly upfront; use tracking technology

### Pitfall 5: Partner Cannibalization
**Problem**: Partners competing with each other or your direct sales
**Solution**: Clear rules of engagement; territory or account definitions

### Pitfall 6: Neglecting Partner Experience
**Problem**: Partners frustrated by poor support or process
**Solution**: Treat partners like customers; invest in partner success

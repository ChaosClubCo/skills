# Vendor Management

> Extract maximum value and minimum risk from every supplier relationship

## Description

Vendor management encompasses the full lifecycle of supplier relationships from initial qualification through ongoing performance management to renewal or exit. This skill covers vendor selection methodologies, contract negotiation frameworks, SLA definition, performance scorecarding, risk assessment, and relationship governance structures. It applies strategic sourcing principles to segment vendors by criticality and value, enabling differentiated management approaches. Practitioners use this skill to build a vendor ecosystem that delivers consistent quality, competitive pricing, contractual compliance, and innovation while mitigating supply disruption and concentration risks.

## Instructions

### Core Workflow

**Step 1: Vendor Landscape Assessment**
- Inventory all active vendors: category, spend, contract status, business owner
- Classify vendors using Kraljic matrix: strategic, leverage, bottleneck, non-critical
- Identify redundancies, gaps, and concentration risks in current vendor portfolio
- Collect current contract terms: duration, SLAs, pricing model, termination clauses
- Assess current vendor management maturity: ad-hoc, reactive, managed, strategic

**Step 2: Strategy and Segmentation**
- Define vendor segmentation criteria: annual spend, criticality, substitutability, risk
- Assign management tier: Tier 1 (strategic partnership), Tier 2 (managed), Tier 3 (transactional)
- Develop category strategies: consolidate, diversify, insource, or maintain status quo
- Set governance model per tier: review frequency, escalation paths, executive sponsorship
- Establish vendor diversity targets aligned with corporate social responsibility goals

**Step 3: Selection and Onboarding**
- Define requirements through cross-functional stakeholder input
- Issue RFP/RFQ with standardized evaluation criteria and weighting
- Conduct technical, commercial, and reference evaluations
- Negotiate contract terms including SLAs, pricing, liability, IP, and exit provisions
- Execute onboarding: system access, training, integration testing, knowledge transfer

**Step 4: Performance Management**
- Deploy vendor scorecards measuring quality, delivery, cost, and responsiveness
- Conduct reviews per governance cadence: monthly operational, quarterly business
- Track SLA compliance and enforce contractual remedies when thresholds are breached
- Manage issue resolution through structured escalation process
- Facilitate continuous improvement initiatives with strategic vendors

**Step 5: Lifecycle Governance**
- Conduct annual vendor risk reassessments including financial health and compliance
- Review contracts 6 months before expiry to plan renewal, renegotiation, or transition
- Maintain exit plans for all Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors with transition runbooks
- Update vendor portfolio strategy based on business changes and market dynamics
- Report vendor management program value to executive stakeholders quarterly

### Vendor Performance Framework

**Scorecard Metrics by Tier**

| Metric | Tier 1 (Strategic) | Tier 2 (Managed) | Tier 3 (Transactional) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quality / Defect Rate | < 1% defect rate | < 3% defect rate | < 5% defect rate |
| On-Time Delivery (OTD) | > 98% | > 95% | > 90% |
| SLA Compliance | > 99% | > 95% | > 90% |
| Responsiveness (Ack time) | < 1 hour | < 4 hours | < 1 business day |
| Cost Competitiveness | Within 5% of market | Within 10% of market | Best available price |
| Innovation Contribution | Joint roadmap, co-development | Proactive suggestions | Not required |
| Reporting Accuracy | 100% on time, complete | 95%+ on time | As needed |
| Review Cadence | Monthly ops + Quarterly strategic | Quarterly | Annual |

**Vendor Risk Assessment Matrix**

| Risk Category | Assessment Criteria | Weight | Scoring (1-5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Financial Stability | Revenue trend, profitability, credit rating, D&B score | 20% | Annual financial review |
| Operational Risk | Capacity, key person dependency, process maturity | 20% | Site audit or assessment |
| Concentration Risk | % of our spend with vendor; vendor's dependency on us | 15% | Spend analysis |
| Compliance/Regulatory | Certifications, audit results, regulatory standing | 15% | Certificate verification |
| Cybersecurity | SOC 2 status, penetration test results, data handling | 15% | Security questionnaire |
| Geographic/Political | Location risks, trade restrictions, currency exposure | 10% | Geopolitical assessment |
| Business Continuity | DR/BCP plans, tested recovery capabilities, redundancy | 5% | BCP documentation review |

**SLA Design Principles**

- Define metrics that are measurable, within vendor control, and aligned to business outcomes
- Set thresholds at three levels: target (expected), minimum (acceptable), breach (unacceptable)
- Include measurement methodology, data source, and reporting frequency
- Define remedies: service credits for minimum breach, termination rights for persistent breach
- Establish exclusions clearly: force majeure, customer-caused delays, agreed maintenance windows
- Review and adjust SLAs annually to reflect changing business needs and market capabilities

### Contract Governance Framework

**Contract Lifecycle Checklist**

- [ ] Business requirements documented and approved by stakeholders
- [ ] RFP issued with clear scope, evaluation criteria, and timeline
- [ ] Minimum 3 qualified responses received and evaluated
- [ ] Technical evaluation completed with scoring matrix
- [ ] Commercial evaluation completed with TCO comparison
- [ ] Reference checks conducted (minimum 2 per finalist)
- [ ] Legal review of contract terms completed
- [ ] Security/privacy assessment completed for data-handling vendors
- [ ] SLAs defined with measurable targets and remedies
- [ ] Insurance requirements verified and certificates on file
- [ ] Contract signed by authorized signatories
- [ ] Onboarding plan executed with knowledge transfer
- [ ] Contract loaded into vendor management system with key dates

**Escalation Matrix**

| Level | Trigger | Vendor Contact | Our Contact | Response Time | Resolution Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 - Operational | Single SLA miss, minor quality issue | Account manager | Contract manager | 4 hours | 5 business days |
| 2 - Management | Repeat SLA miss, pattern of underperformance | Director/VP | Category manager | 2 hours | 10 business days |
| 3 - Executive | Systemic failure, material breach, risk event | C-level / SVP | VP Procurement | 1 hour | 30 days remediation plan |
| 4 - Termination | Unresolved Level 3, irreparable breach | Legal/C-level | Legal + CPO | Immediate | Per contract exit clause |

### Best Practices

- Segment vendors by strategic value and manage each tier with appropriate rigor and investment
- Never allow a single vendor to exceed 30% of spend in any critical category without a qualified backup
- Include clear exit provisions in every contract including data portability and transition assistance
- Conduct financial health checks annually for Tier 1 vendors and at renewal for Tier 2
- Automate SLA tracking and reporting; manual SLA measurement introduces bias and delays
- Maintain a qualified vendor bench of 2-3 pre-vetted alternates for each critical category
- Negotiate volume discounts and rebate structures tied to actual spend commitments
- Require SOC 2 Type II or equivalent for any vendor handling sensitive data
- Use total cost of ownership in evaluations, not just unit price or rate card comparison
- Document all verbal agreements in writing within 48 hours as contract amendments
- Build relationships beyond the sales contact: know the delivery team, escalation contacts, and executive sponsor
- Review contract auto-renewal dates and set calendar alerts 6 months in advance
- Track vendor-initiated innovations and give credit in scorecards to incentivize proactive improvement
- Conduct joint risk workshops with strategic vendors to identify and mitigate shared vulnerabilities

### Common Patterns

**Pattern 1: Vendor Consolidation for Cost and Efficiency**

An enterprise uses 45 IT staffing vendors, with the top 5 providing 60% of contractors and the bottom 30 providing just 15%. Action: (1) Analyze vendor performance scores across quality, time-to-fill, attrition rate, and cost, (2) Designate 8 preferred vendors covering all skill categories with volume commitments, (3) Transition active contractors from non-preferred vendors at natural contract end, (4) Negotiate 12% rate reduction in exchange for volume guarantees. Result: Vendor count reduced from 45 to 12, management overhead cut by 60%, average contractor quality score improved from 3.2 to 4.1, annual savings of $1.8M on $15M staffing spend.

**Pattern 2: SLA Restructuring for Outcome Alignment**

A company's outsourced help desk vendor consistently meets SLAs (tickets answered within time) but user satisfaction scores are declining. Analysis reveals SLAs measure response speed but not resolution quality. Action: (1) Redesign SLAs adding first-contact resolution (FCR), customer satisfaction (CSAT), and ticket reopen rate, (2) Weight outcome metrics at 60% and process metrics at 40% in scorecard, (3) Implement service credits tied to CSAT dropping below 4.0/5.0, (4) Establish monthly calibration sessions on ticket quality. Result: FCR improves from 52% to 71%, CSAT rises from 3.4 to 4.2, ticket volume decreases 18% due to better first-contact resolution.

**Pattern 3: Vendor Risk Mitigation After Critical Incident**

A key SaaS vendor experiences a 48-hour outage affecting business operations. Post-incident review reveals no BCP testing requirement in contract and no alternative system available. Action: (1) Negotiate contract amendment requiring annual DR test with documented results, (2) Establish RPO/RTO requirements in SLA with financial penalties, (3) Implement data export to secondary cloud storage nightly, (4) Qualify backup vendor and maintain warm standby capability for critical functions. Result: Recovery capability validated through annual testing, backup vendor can assume operations within 24 hours, data loss exposure reduced from 48 hours to 4 hours maximum.

### Output Formats

**Vendor Portfolio Dashboard**
Visual summary showing vendor count by tier, total spend distribution, aggregate scorecard performance, risk heat map by category, contract renewal timeline, and diversity spend metrics.

**Vendor Performance Report**
Detailed report per vendor covering: scorecard with metric-level detail and trend arrows, SLA compliance summary with breach log, financial summary (spend vs. budget, invoice accuracy), open issues and escalations, and recommended actions with owners.

**Strategic Sourcing Recommendation**
Decision document with: market analysis summary, vendor comparison matrix with weighted scores, TCO analysis across evaluation period, risk assessment comparison, recommended vendor with rationale, and implementation plan with timeline and resource requirements.

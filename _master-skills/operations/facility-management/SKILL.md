# Facility Management

> Optimize every square foot, system, and service across your built environment

## Description

Facility management covers the integrated planning, operation, and maintenance of buildings, infrastructure, and workplace environments. This skill addresses space utilization optimization, preventive and corrective maintenance programs, energy and sustainability management, health and safety compliance, and capital project planning. It applies total productive maintenance (TPM) principles and building performance analytics to reduce operating costs while maximizing occupant satisfaction and asset longevity. Practitioners use this skill to transform facilities from cost centers into strategic enablers of workforce productivity and business continuity.

## Instructions

### Core Workflow

**Step 1: Facility Assessment**
- Conduct building condition assessment (BCA) for all managed properties
- Inventory all critical building systems: HVAC, electrical, plumbing, fire/life safety, elevators
- Measure current space utilization using occupancy sensors or badge data
- Audit energy consumption by building, floor, and system
- Review existing maintenance records, work order backlog, and contractor agreements

**Step 2: Performance Benchmarking**
- Calculate cost per square foot: total operating cost / gross square footage
- Benchmark against BOMA or IFMA industry standards for building class
- Assess Facility Condition Index (FCI): deferred maintenance / current replacement value
- Measure energy use intensity (EUI): kBTU per square foot per year
- Survey occupant satisfaction using standardized workplace experience metrics

**Step 3: Strategy Development**
- Define space standards by function: workstation, collaboration, quiet, amenity
- Design preventive maintenance schedules for all critical assets by system type
- Develop energy management plan targeting EUI reduction by specific percentage
- Create 5-year capital plan prioritized by FCI severity and business impact
- Establish service level agreements for janitorial, security, grounds, and reception

**Step 4: Operations Execution**
- Implement CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System) for all work orders
- Deploy building automation system (BAS) controls for HVAC and lighting optimization
- Establish vendor management program with performance-based contracts
- Execute space changes: moves, adds, changes (MAC) with minimal disruption
- Conduct regular safety inspections and maintain compliance documentation

**Step 5: Performance Optimization**
- Monitor building performance dashboards daily for anomalies
- Track work order metrics: response time, completion rate, first-time fix rate
- Conduct quarterly business reviews with outsourced service providers
- Update space plans based on changing headcount and work patterns
- Review and adjust capital plan annually based on condition assessments

**Key Performance Indicators**

| KPI Category | Metric | Benchmark | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cost | Operating cost per SF | $8-15/SF (office, Class A) | Monthly |
| Cost | Energy cost per SF | $2-4/SF | Monthly |

| Condition | Facility Condition Index (FCI) | < 0.10 (good), > 0.30 (poor) | Annually |
| Utilization | Space utilization rate | 60-80% average occupancy | Weekly |
| Utilization | SF per person (allocated) | 150-250 SF/person | Quarterly |
| Maintenance | PM compliance rate | > 95% | Monthly |
| Maintenance | Work order backlog | < 2 weeks | Weekly |
| Maintenance | MTTR for critical systems | < 4 hours | Monthly |
| Energy | Energy Use Intensity (EUI) | Compare to ENERGY STAR | Monthly |
| Satisfaction | Occupant satisfaction score | > 4.0 / 5.0 | Quarterly |

**Building System Criticality Matrix**

| System | Failure Impact | Redundancy Required | PM Frequency | Max Acceptable Downtime |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fire/Life Safety | Life safety, code violation | Full redundancy | Monthly test, annual inspection | 0 hours |
| Electrical (main) | Total building shutdown | Generator backup | Quarterly PM, annual thermography | 1 hour |
| HVAC (primary) | Occupant discomfort, productivity loss | N+1 capacity | Monthly filter, quarterly full PM | 4 hours |
| Elevators | Accessibility, entrapment risk | Multiple cars | Monthly PM, annual load test | 8 hours |
| Plumbing (main) | Water damage, health risk | Isolation valves | Annual inspection | 4 hours |
| Security/Access | Unauthorized access risk | Battery backup, manual override | Monthly test | 1 hour |
| BAS/Controls | Loss of automation efficiency | Manual override capability | Quarterly calibration | 24 hours |

### Space Planning and Optimization Framework

**Space Allocation Standards**

| Space Type | SF Allocation | Target Utilization | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Individual workstation | 48-64 SF | 40-60% (hybrid) | 1:1 or 3:2 sharing |
| Private office | 96-150 SF | 50-70% | Assigned by role |
| Small meeting (2-4) | 80-120 SF | 40-50% | 1 per 15 people |
| Medium meeting (5-8) | 150-250 SF | 35-45% | 1 per 30 people |
| Large conference (9-20) | 300-500 SF | 25-35% | 1 per 60 people |
| Collaboration/huddle | 60-100 SF | 30-50% | 1 per 20 people |
| Quiet/focus room | 40-60 SF | 40-60% | 1 per 25 people |
| Amenity (kitchen, wellness) | Varies | Peak-driven | Per building code + culture |

**Space Optimization Checklist**

- [ ] Collect 90 days of occupancy sensor or badge data
- [ ] Calculate peak, average, and off-peak utilization by zone
- [ ] Identify spaces consistently below 30% utilization for repurposing
- [ ] Identify spaces consistently above 85% utilization for expansion
- [ ] Model desk-sharing ratios based on actual attendance patterns
- [ ] Estimate moves/adds/changes required and associated costs
- [ ] Calculate projected savings: lease cost avoided, energy reduction
- [ ] Develop change management plan for workspace transitions
- [ ] Pilot new layout in one floor/zone before full rollout

### Best Practices

- Maintain FCI below 0.10 through consistent capital reinvestment of 2-4% of CRV annually
- Implement condition-based maintenance using BAS data and IoT sensors for critical equipment
- Standardize work order categories and priority levels to enable meaningful trend analysis
- Keep a minimum 90-day parts inventory for critical building systems
- Conduct quarterly walk-through inspections with standardized checklists by building zone
- Use energy sub-metering to identify consumption by system and floor for targeted reduction
- Maintain updated as-built drawings and equipment records in a digital twin or CMMS
- Negotiate performance-based contracts with FM service providers including SLA penalties
- Plan moves/adds/changes with at least 30 days lead time to coordinate all trades
- Benchmark annually against BOMA Experience Exchange or IFMA benchmarking reports
- Conduct annual emergency preparedness drills covering fire, evacuation, and shelter-in-place
- Document all warranty terms and track expiration dates to claim covered repairs
- Survey occupant satisfaction quarterly and correlate with specific building systems or services
- Keep deferred maintenance log current and tie it directly to risk exposure assessments

### Common Patterns

**Pattern 1: Energy Cost Reduction Program**

A 500,000 SF office campus spends $2.1M annually on energy ($4.20/SF EUI of 95 kBTU/SF). Benchmarking shows peer buildings at $3.20/SF and EUI of 72. Action: (1) Implement BAS scheduling optimization reducing after-hours HVAC runtime by 40%, (2) Install LED lighting with occupancy sensors saving 35% lighting energy, (3) Retro-commission air handling units correcting economizer and damper faults, (4) Add building envelope improvements (window film, door seals). Result: EUI reduced to 68, annual energy spend drops to $1.55M, saving $550K/year with $800K total investment (1.5-year payback).

**Pattern 2: Workplace Consolidation for Hybrid Work**

A company occupying 4 floors (80,000 SF) post-pandemic has 35% average occupancy. Lease cost is $3.2M annually. Action: (1) Analyze badge data confirming peak occupancy never exceeds 55%, (2) Redesign 2 floors with activity-based working at 3:2 sharing ratio, (3) Surrender 2 floors at lease renewal, (4) Invest $400K in collaboration technology and furniture reconfiguration. Result: Footprint reduced to 40,000 SF, annual lease savings of $1.6M, occupant satisfaction improves 12% due to upgraded amenities and varied work settings.

### Output Formats

**Facility Performance Dashboard**
Visual display with building scorecards showing FCI, EUI, utilization, and cost per SF; maintenance KPI gauges (PM compliance, MTTR, backlog); energy consumption trends with weather normalization; and occupant satisfaction heat maps by zone.

**Annual Facility Condition Report**
Comprehensive document with executive summary, property-by-property condition ratings, photographic documentation of deficiencies, prioritized capital needs list with cost estimates, 5-year capital forecast, and FCI trending analysis.

**Monthly Operations Report**
Structured report covering: budget vs. actual by expense category, work order volume and completion metrics, energy consumption vs. baseline, safety incidents and inspection results, active capital projects status, and upcoming planned maintenance events.

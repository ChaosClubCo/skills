# Stakeholder Map — INT AI Enablement PM

## Framing Guide

| Stakeholder | Role | What They Care About | Frame AI As | What Breaks Engagement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dave Linderman | President | Business ROI, competitive position, org risk | Revenue enablement, cost reduction, retention advantage | Jargon, vague outcomes, no ask |
| Jackie Miles | VP Operations | Process efficiency, consistency, staff capability | Hours saved, error reduction, scalable repeatable process | Scope creep, unclear ownership, no baseline |
| Daniel Bell | Service Desk Manager | Ticket resolution speed, KB quality, engineer support load | Faster triage, KB article output, self-service deflection | Unproven tools, extra work for his team |
| Engineering (50–200) | End users | Reduced toil, career leverage, not breaking flow | Time saved on specific repetitive tasks, skill growth | Mandatory adoption, clunky tooling |

## Brief Examples

### Dave Brief (ROI Framing)
```
# AI Enablement Program — Q1 Update
From: Kyle Rosebrook, AI SME | For: Dave Linderman

What we're doing: Deploying a library of reusable AI skills that automate repetitive work
across engineering, service desk, and operations.

Why it matters: Competitors are shipping 2–3x faster with AI tooling. We risk falling behind
on talent retention and delivery velocity. Current: ~8 engineers actively using AI tools.
Target: 40+ by Q2 across all hat-wearers.

What it costs: ~5 hrs/week of Kyle's time for office hours and 1:1 sessions.
No additional tooling budget required — Claude access already provisioned.

What we're asking for: Continued support for the program and 5 minutes at the next all-hands
to showcase a non-engineering win.

What success looks like: 40% of the org with ≥1 AI domain unlocked by end of Q2.
Estimated 200+ hours/month saved across teams.
```

### Daniel Brief (Service Desk Framing)
```
# KB Article Generator — Pilot Proposal
From: Kyle Rosebrook, AI SME | For: Daniel Bell

What we're doing: Deploying the kb-article-generator skill to your team.
It produces formatted KB articles in ~8 minutes vs. the current ~45-minute manual process.

Why it matters to you: Your team creates ~8 KB articles/month — that's ~6 hours that could
go to triage and resolution instead. Article quality and consistency also improves because
the skill enforces the INT template automatically.

What it costs: 15-minute demo + one pilot run per team member. No setup required —
they access it through Claude.ai with no install.

What we're asking for: 15 minutes at your next team standup to demo it, and your
go-ahead to share the skill link with your team.

What success looks like: Article creation time under 10 minutes within 2 weeks of rollout.
```

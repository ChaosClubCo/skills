# Threaded long-form social templates

20 templates. Each fits one of: LinkedIn thread (1000–2000 words, single post or native article), X thread (8–25 tweets), Threads post, IG carousel (6–10 slides). Each template notes which platforms fit best.

**What makes threaded long-form distinct:**
- Beat structure. Each post/tweet/slide must stand alone AND pull the reader to the next.
- Engagement mechanics baked in. Save-bait, share-bait, reply-bait — platform-native.
- No paragraph flow. Threads are episodic. Blog long-form flows; threads chunk.
- Hook is everything. First post/tweet/slide decides if the rest gets read.
- CTA lands at the end, specifically. Never mid-thread.

**Platform cheat sheet (applies to all 20 templates):**

| Platform | Length sweet spot | Line break style | Emoji | Save-bait works? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | 1000–1500 words, single post | White space between lines, each line short | Sparingly | Yes |
| X thread | 8–15 tweets | One idea per tweet, 240 chars each | Almost never | Medium |
| IG carousel | 6–10 slides | Large text per slide, 1-2 sentences each | Platform-expected | Very high |
| Threads | 5–8 posts | Like X but 500 chars | Sparingly | Medium |

## 1. Founder origin thread

**Use:** Tell the story of how FlashFusion / chaosclub / any current project started. Relationship-builder, not sales.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn (strongest), X, Threads.

**Voice:** First person. Concrete details over abstractions. Include at least one moment where you were wrong about something.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook post]
{{one sentence that names the current outcome — "6 months ago I was X.
today I'm Y."}}

[Post 2 — the starting line]
Where I actually was. Concrete: job, bank balance, what I was
working on. No vague "I felt stuck."

[Post 3 — the catalyst]
The specific moment or signal. One scene.

[Post 4–6 — the hard middle]
3 posts, one per real obstacle. Each obstacle:
  - what it was
  - what I tried that didn't work
  - what I learned

[Post 7 — the turn]
The shift in approach. What I stopped doing, what I started doing.

[Post 8 — where I am now]
Concrete again. What exists today. Numbers if they help, humility if not.

[Post 9 — close]
One line the reader can use. No "keep going, you got this." Something
specific to what you've learned.
```

**Acceptance:** At least one moment where the founder was wrong. No hero narrative. Concrete numbers where they exist, honest "I don't know yet" where they don't.

## 2. Counter-intuitive take thread

**Use:** Challenge a widely-held belief in your space with evidence.

**Platforms:** X (strongest), LinkedIn, Threads.

**Voice:** Confident but not combative. You're not against the crowd — you've looked at the data and it says something different.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"Everyone says {{claim}}. The data says the opposite."
OR
"{{claim}} is wrong. Here's what the numbers actually show."

[Post 2 — the received wisdom]
Steelman the common view. Show you understand why people believe it.

[Post 3 — the data]
The evidence. Specific. Cited. Not cherry-picked.

[Post 4 — the mechanism]
Why the common view is wrong. The actual mechanism underneath.

[Post 5 — when the common view IS right]
The bounded case where the conventional wisdom holds. This builds trust.

[Post 6 — implication]
What should you do differently now. One specific action.

[Post 7 — close]
"I might be wrong about this. Here's what would change my mind:
{{specific falsifier}}"
```

**Acceptance:** You steelman before you attack. You name when the conventional view holds. You state what would falsify your own take. If those three things aren't there, you're posting a hot take, not a counter-intuitive one.

## 3. Viral post / company breakdown thread

**Use:** Analyze something that blew up or worked. Teach from it.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn, X.

**Voice:** Analytical. Not fanboy, not hater. The subject is a case study, not a hero or villain.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{thing}} got {{specific metric — 10M views, $5M in 6 months, etc}}.
Here's what's actually going on."

[Post 2 — the surface]
What most people saw and thought was the reason.

[Post 3–5 — the real mechanics]
3 posts, one per actual driver. Each one:
  - the mechanic
  - why it worked here specifically
  - what it wouldn't work for

[Post 6 — what's NOT replicable]
The context-dependent bits. The timing. The person. The luck.

[Post 7 — what IS replicable]
The portable principles. 1–2 max. Over-specifying kills the trust.

[Post 8 — close]
Link to the original (if public). Credit the creator/company cleanly.
```

**Acceptance:** Named what's luck vs skill. Credited the source. Didn't pretend to know more than you can know from the outside.

## 4. "What I got wrong" retrospective thread

**Use:** Public post-mortem on a take, prediction, or decision. Trust-builder.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn (strongest), X, Threads.

**Voice:** No self-flagellation, no "look how humble I am." Just the actual error and what changed.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{n months/years}} ago I said {{claim}}. I was wrong. Here's what I
missed."

[Post 2 — the claim, in full]
Quote or screenshot the original take. Don't soften it.

[Post 3 — why I believed it]
The reasoning at the time. Not self-serving.

[Post 4 — what I missed]
The specific information, reasoning gap, or bias that caused the error.

[Post 5 — how it played out]
What actually happened. Evidence.

[Post 6 — the update]
What I believe now. Stated with the same confidence level the evidence
warrants.

[Post 7 — close]
"I'll probably be wrong about something else. If you see it, tell me."
```

**Acceptance:** Quotes the original claim in full. Names the reasoning error, not just "I was wrong." Doesn't over-correct — the new view is calibrated to the new evidence, not to the opposite of the old view.

## 5. Tool stack reveal thread

**Use:** "What I actually use" thread. High engagement, but only if specific.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn, X, Threads, IG carousel (visual-friendly).

**Voice:** Practitioner. Use-case specific. Include what you dropped and why.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"My stack for {{specific workflow}}. No affiliates, no sponsorships.
Just what actually sits on my machine."

[Post 2–N — one post per tool]
For each tool:
  - Tool name
  - What specifically it does in your workflow (not "automation")
  - Who it's for (and who it's NOT for)
  - One alternative you considered and rejected, and why

[Second-to-last post] DROPPED
What you used to use and stopped using. This is the highest-trust signal.
Name names, give reason.

[Last post] THE TAKE
One sentence on how the stack fits together. What the system is for.
```

**Acceptance:** Specific use-case per tool (not generic category). Names at least one tool you dropped. No affiliate links — or clearly labeled if any.

## 6. Case study thread (with before/after metrics)

**Use:** Show a piece of work you did and what it produced. For client work or internal project.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn (strongest), IG carousel, X.

**Voice:** Analytical. Client-confidential redactions where needed, but the numbers that matter are present.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{specific-metric}} improvement in {{timeframe}}. Here's what we did."

[Post 2 — the before]
Starting state. Numbers. The specific problem.

[Post 3 — the diagnosis]
What we thought was wrong. How we tested it.

[Post 4 — what we actually did]
The intervention. Specific enough to be useful, general enough to not
break confidentiality.

[Post 5 — the after]
Numbers. Same metrics as the before. Timeframe.

[Post 6 — what we'd do differently]
The honest part. What we over-indexed on, under-invested in.

[Post 7 — close]
What this applies to. What it doesn't.

CLAIMS: {{metrics claimed — cite source or method}}
GAPS: {{confounders, small-sample warnings, context-dependencies}}
```

**Acceptance:** Before and after use the same metrics. One thing you'd do differently. Honest caveats on generalizability.

## 7. Numbered-list thread

**Use:** "N things I learned from X" or "N ways to do Y." The default LinkedIn format — use sparingly because it's saturated.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn (most native), IG carousel.

**Voice:** Each item has to actually earn its number. If item 4 is filler, delete it and make it "3 things."

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{N}} {{specific-things}} from {{specific-source or experience}}."
— N is odd if possible (3, 5, 7). Even numbers feel padded.

[Post 2 — item 1]
Name it. Explain it in 1-2 sentences. Give one example.

[Post 3 — item 2]
Same structure.

...

[Post N+1 — item N]
Save the strongest for last. First and last are read most.

[Close post]
"The thread in one sentence: {{summary}}."
OR
"If you only take one: {{pick one}}."
```

**Acceptance:** Every item is load-bearing. No filler. First and last are the strongest. Summary in one sentence exists.

## 8. Transformation / journey thread

**Use:** "From X to Y in timeframe Z" narrative. Different from founder origin — this is about a specific transformation, not a whole backstory.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn, Threads, IG carousel.

**Voice:** Temporal. Scene by scene. No skipping the hard middle.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"Day 1 I was {{state-A}}. Day {{N}} I'm {{state-B}}. Here's what
happened in between — the part people skip."

[Post 2] DAY 1
Scene. What it actually looked like, felt like.

[Post 3] DAY {{small-n}}
The first signal something was working. Or wasn't.

[Post 4–5] THE MIDDLE
The part that's usually glossed over. The boring, unglamorous grind.
Specific enough to be believable.

[Post 6] THE BREAK
The moment things shifted. Or when you almost quit.

[Post 7] DAY {{N}}
Current state. Numbers where they exist.

[Post 8 — close]
"The middle was the whole thing. That's where most people bail."
Or whatever the real lesson is — but no fortune-cookie wisdom.
```

**Acceptance:** The middle section is longer than the end. You don't skip to the outcome. At least one moment of near-quitting or doubt.

## 9. Process breakdown thread ("how I do X")

**Use:** Walking through your workflow for a specific task.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn, IG carousel (diagrammatic), X.

**Voice:** Practitioner. Exact tools, exact steps. No hand-waving.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"My process for {{specific-task}}. Takes {{time}}. Here's the whole thing."

[Post 2] THE INPUT
What you start with. What you need before you can begin.

[Post 3–N] THE STEPS
One post per step.
  Step name.
  What you do.
  What tool / doc / template you use.
  What you check before moving on (the quality gate).

[Post N+1] THE OUTPUT
What you end with. How you know it's done.

[Close post] WHAT BREAKS IT
The top 2 failure modes. How you recover.
```

**Acceptance:** Quality gate named per step. Failure modes named at the end. Tools named specifically, not vaguely ("I use AI" → "I use Claude with X skill").

## 10. Myth-busting thread

**Use:** Take down a common misconception in your space with evidence.

**Platforms:** X (strongest), LinkedIn, Threads.

**Voice:** Direct. Not snarky. You're correcting the record, not picking a fight.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{common-claim}} is a myth. Here's where it came from and what's
actually true."

[Post 2] THE CLAIM
State it in its strongest form. No strawmanning.

[Post 3] WHERE IT COMES FROM
The origin of the myth. Often there's a kernel of truth — name it.

[Post 4] THE EVIDENCE AGAINST
Specific studies, data, counterexamples. Cited.

[Post 5] WHY IT PERSISTS
The reason the myth keeps spreading despite evidence. Usually it serves
someone — name who and why.

[Post 6] WHAT'S ACTUALLY TRUE
The calibrated version. Not "the opposite" — the nuanced reality.

[Post 7 — close]
"If you've repeated this before — same. We all absorb bad info. The
update is the thing."

CLAIMS: {{specific evidence cited — with source}}
GAPS: {{what the evidence doesn't settle}}
```

**Acceptance:** The claim is stated strongly before you knock it. You name why it persists. You don't overclaim the truth — the nuanced version is calibrated.

## 11. Hot take / opinion thread

**Use:** Strong opinion on a contested topic. Highest risk template in this library.

**Platforms:** X (native), LinkedIn (use caution), Threads.

**Voice:** Confident, not aggressive. First person. Own it as opinion, not as revealed truth.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"Unpopular opinion: {{claim}}"
OR
"I'm going to say the thing nobody wants to say: {{claim}}"

[Post 2] WHY I BELIEVE IT
The reasoning. Not data-dump — the path your mind took.

[Post 3] WHAT I'M NOT SAYING
Pre-empt the worst-faith reading. Name what people might assume and
clarify.

[Post 4] EVIDENCE I FIND COMPELLING
The data or experience that drives the take.

[Post 5] WHEN I'D CHANGE MY MIND
What specific evidence would falsify this for you. Makes the take real.

[Post 6 — close]
"Push back welcome. Bad-faith ratios get muted, not debated."
```

**Acceptance:** Names the falsifier. Pre-empts worst-faith reading. Owns it as opinion. If any of these are missing, it's a rage-bait thread, not a take.

## 12. Socratic question thread

**Use:** Open a question publicly. Lead readers through their own reasoning.

**Platforms:** X, Threads, LinkedIn.

**Voice:** Genuine curiosity. Not rhetorical — actually interested in the answer.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"Question I've been sitting with: {{question}}"
Or: "Here's a question I don't have a clean answer to: {{question}}"

[Post 2] WHY THIS MATTERS
Stakes of the question. Why it's worth thinking about.

[Post 3] THE OBVIOUS ANSWER
What most people jump to. State it fully.

[Post 4] WHY THE OBVIOUS ANSWER IS INCOMPLETE
Not "wrong" — incomplete. The edge cases.

[Post 5] A SECOND ANGLE
Different frame. Different answer. Neither wins, yet.

[Post 6] WHERE I'M LANDING (tentatively)
Your current position. Marked tentative.

[Post 7 — close]
"Genuine question — if you've thought about this, reply. I'm looking
for disagreement more than agreement."
```

**Acceptance:** You genuinely don't have a clean answer. You state your tentative view with appropriate uncertainty. You invite disagreement, and mean it.

## 13. Prediction / future-looking thread

**Use:** Calling what you think will happen in a space, with timelines.

**Platforms:** X, LinkedIn, Threads.

**Voice:** Specific enough to be wrong. Vague predictions aren't predictions.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{N}} predictions for {{domain}} by {{specific-date}}. Scored against
at {{check-in-date}}."

[Post 2–N+1] ONE PREDICTION PER POST
Format each prediction:
  - The prediction (specific enough to grade)
  - Confidence level (50%, 70%, 90%)
  - Why I think this
  - What would falsify it early

[Post N+2] WHAT I'M DELIBERATELY NOT PREDICTING
Topics where you don't have a read. Builds credibility.

[Post N+3 — close]
"I'll come back to this on {{date}} and score each one. Follow or
bookmark if you want the scorecard."

CLAIMS: {{predictions are testable, timebound, confidence-scored}}
```

**Acceptance:** Every prediction has a specific date and a specific falsifier. Confidence levels assigned. You commit to grading yourself on a specific future date.

## 14. Framework / mental model thread

**Use:** Introduce or explain a framework you use or have developed.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn (strongest), IG carousel (diagrammatic), X.

**Voice:** Didactic but not preachy. The framework earns its complexity or it doesn't ship.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"The framework I use for {{specific-decision-or-task}}. {{N}} parts.
Takes 5 minutes to learn."

[Post 2] THE PROBLEM IT SOLVES
The specific confusion the framework clears up. If there isn't one,
the framework is vanity.

[Post 3–N+2] ONE PART PER POST
For each part:
  - Name
  - What it asks you to do / consider
  - One example

[Post N+3] WHEN IT FAILS
Cases where the framework breaks down. Naming this makes the rest
usable.

[Post N+4 — close]
"Steal it, modify it, name it something else. Frameworks are tools,
not trademarks."
```

**Acceptance:** The problem it solves is specific. You name when it fails. Every part has an example. If a part has no example, cut it.

## 15. Day-in-the-life narrative thread

**Use:** Scene-by-scene walkthrough of a real day (or composite). High-intimacy content.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn, Threads, IG carousel.

**Voice:** First person, present tense. Concrete sensory detail. No self-congratulation.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"6:47 AM. Coffee's burnt. Deploying chaosclub.co at 7."
A specific opening scene, timestamped, sensory.

[Post 2–N] SCENES
Each post is one scene. Time-stamped. One specific task or moment.
Include boring moments — they're what make it real.
Include at least one frustration.
Include at least one small delight.

[Last post]
One sentence on what the day added up to. Or didn't.
No "crushing it." No "grateful for the grind."
```

**Acceptance:** Timestamps throughout. At least one frustration, at least one delight, at least one boring moment. No "crushing it" language.

## 16. Mini-essay thread (single developed argument)

**Use:** Develop one idea across 8–12 posts. Longer-form thinking, still episodic.

**Platforms:** X (native territory), LinkedIn, Threads.

**Voice:** Essayistic but threaded. Each post advances the argument — no recap posts.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
One-sentence statement of the thesis, provocative enough to keep reading.

[Post 2] THE CLAIM, IN FULL
Unpack the hook. What exactly are you arguing.

[Post 3] THE SETUP
Why this question matters. What's at stake.

[Post 4–6] THE ARGUMENT
3 posts advancing the case. Each one a different piece of the argument,
not a restatement.

[Post 7] THE COUNTER
The strongest argument against your position. State it fully.

[Post 8] WHY THE COUNTER DOESN'T CLOSE IT
Your response to the strongest counter.

[Post 9] IMPLICATIONS
If your argument is right, what follows.

[Post 10 — close]
One line. Lands the whole thread.
```

**Acceptance:** Thesis is unpacked, not just repeated. Strongest counter is actually the strongest. You land on one line that sums it up without summarizing.

## 17. Analogy thread

**Use:** Explain something complex by mapping it to something familiar.

**Platforms:** X, LinkedIn, Threads, IG carousel.

**Voice:** Teacherly. The analogy has to hold across at least 3 points of correspondence or it's just a metaphor.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{complex-thing}} is like {{familiar-thing}}. Here's why that's
useful — and where the analogy breaks."

[Post 2] THE MAPPING
The core of the analogy. The thing that makes it click.

[Post 3] MAPPING POINT 1
{{element of A}} maps to {{element of B}}. Why.

[Post 4] MAPPING POINT 2
Next correspondence.

[Post 5] MAPPING POINT 3
Next correspondence.

[Post 6] WHERE IT BREAKS
The place the analogy fails. Naming this is what makes analogies useful
vs misleading.

[Post 7 — close]
"Analogies are ladders. Use them to climb, then throw them away when
you don't need them."
Or a more specific close to your topic.
```

**Acceptance:** At least 3 correspondences. At least one place the analogy breaks. You explicitly name the analogy's limits.

## 18. Controversial position thread

**Use:** Stake out a position you know will attract pushback. More careful than the hot take — this is building, not provoking.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn (use caution), X, Threads.

**Voice:** Serious, measured. This isn't a stunt post — you're going to have to defend this in replies for days.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"I'm going to argue something I know will get pushback: {{position}}."

[Post 2] WHO I UNDERSTAND IS HURT BY THIS POSITION
Name the groups or positions that will be upset. Show you understand
why.

[Post 3] THE ARGUMENT
The core case. Serious. Evidenced.

[Post 4] THE PERSONAL STAKES
Why you care enough to post this despite knowing the cost.

[Post 5] THE STEELMAN
Strongest version of the opposing view.

[Post 6] WHY I STILL HOLD MY POSITION
Your response to the steelman.

[Post 7] WHAT I'M NOT SAYING
Pre-empt the bad-faith reading. "This does NOT mean {{thing}}."

[Post 8 — close]
"I'll read thoughtful pushback. I won't engage with strawmen. Block
list active."
```

**Acceptance:** Named who is hurt. Steelman is genuine. Named what you're NOT saying. Personal stakes stated. If you're not willing to defend this in replies for 72 hours, don't post it.

## 19. Public post-mortem thread

**Use:** Something you built or shipped failed. You're analyzing it publicly.

**Platforms:** LinkedIn (strongest), X, Threads.

**Voice:** Blameless. Same rules as the Intinc incident postmortem — systems and decisions, not people.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{thing}} failed. Shipped {{date}}, {{outcome}}. Here's what went
wrong and what I'd do differently."

[Post 2] WHAT IT WAS
The thing you built / did / tried. Specific.

[Post 3] WHAT I EXPECTED
The hypothesis. The bet. Stated without hindsight.

[Post 4] WHAT HAPPENED
The actual outcome. Numbers if you have them.

[Post 5] WHY IT FAILED
Root cause. Systems-level, not people-level. Honest.

[Post 6] WHAT I MISSED
The reasoning gap. The information you didn't seek out. The signal you
ignored.

[Post 7] WHAT I'D DO DIFFERENTLY
Specific changes. Not "trust my gut more" — concrete.

[Post 8] WHAT I WOULDN'T DO DIFFERENTLY
The bets that were right to make even though the outcome was bad.

[Post 9 — close]
"Postmortems only help if they're honest. If you've shipped something
and it didn't work — write yours. I'll read it."
```

**Acceptance:** Blameless. Systems-level root cause. Names what you WOULDN'T change. No performative self-flagellation.

## 20. Build-in-public update thread

**Use:** Regular cadence update on something you're building. Weekly, monthly. Compounds trust over time.

**Platforms:** X (native — the home of build-in-public), LinkedIn, Threads.

**Voice:** Honest. Numbers when you have them, "I don't know yet" when you don't. No hyping.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook]
"{{project}} — {{timeframe}} update. {{N}} {{metric}}. Here's what
shipped, what broke, what's next."

[Post 2] WHAT SHIPPED
What's live that wasn't last update. 2-3 specific things.

[Post 3] NUMBERS
Metrics you're tracking. Same metrics every update, so compounding is
visible. Users, revenue, whatever the right numbers are. No "engagement."

[Post 4] WHAT BROKE
What's not working. What you had to roll back. What's slower than hoped.

[Post 5] WHAT I LEARNED
The most useful update in your own mental model.

[Post 6] WHAT'S NEXT
The specific next thing. Dated if possible.

[Post 7] WHAT I COULD USE HELP WITH
A specific, askable thing. Not "support" — "does anyone have experience
with {{specific-problem}}."

[Post 8 — close]
"Next update {{date}}. Same format."
```

**Acceptance:** Same metrics as previous updates (so compounding is visible). What broke is named. The ask is specific. Commit to the next update date.

## Cross-template notes

**Hook test.** If the first post doesn't work as a standalone post, cut it. Threads start with the hook carrying 80% of the weight.

**Reply-bait vs engagement-bait.** The former invites real conversation ("what have you seen?"), the latter demands it ("agree? 🤔"). Use the former.

**Reposting rules.** A thread that performed well can be re-threaded 6 months later with fresh examples. Don't just re-post verbatim.

**Cross-platform adaptation.** Most threads here work on 2-3 platforms with minor edits. X version will be tighter, LinkedIn version will be slower-cadence, IG carousel will need visual thinking per slide.

**Scheduling.** Threads are time-of-day sensitive. LinkedIn: Tue–Thu mornings US. X: mornings or evenings US, depending on audience. IG: evenings.

## 📊 Stat of the Week

A strategically significant number. Frame with market context and explicit InVelo implication.

**Example:**
> **Stat of the Week:** OpenAI's $110B round valued the company at $730B — roughly equal
> to the entire U.S. airline industry combined. Amazon's $50B chunk makes them the largest
> single investor in AI, dual-betting on both OpenAI and Anthropic.
> **InVelo implication:** Enterprise buyers now face a landscape where their AI vendors'
> investors have competing strategic interests. This is a client conversation waiting to happen.
> *Source: [Bloomberg](URL)*

## 🔁 Last Week We Said...

Strategic callback. Connect to evolving thesis, market prediction, or positioning angle.
Always include InVelo implication update.

**Example:**
> **Last Week We Said...** The supply chain designation would face legal challenge within
> weeks. Update: Anthropic retained WilmerHale. Filing expected this week. Lawfare's
> analysis remains the best public legal assessment — the designation is likely
> unconstitutional. InVelo should prepare a "post-resolution" brief for the two likely outcomes.

## Section 1: Strategic Intel (~400 words)

Top 3–5 developments with full competitive analysis. For each:
- **What happened** (factual, sourced)
- **The strategic read** (what this signals — take a position, don't hedge)
- **Vendor strategy analysis** (why the company made this move)
- **INT positioning impact** (specific, named implications for InVelo, HumanX, client work)

Every story gets an opinionated read. "It remains to be seen" is not allowed here.
"The strategic read: Anthropic made a calculated bet that public trust > government revenue."

**InVelo integration:** Every major story needs an explicit InVelo angle. If a story
doesn't have one, it probably shouldn't be the lead.

## Section 2: Model Landscape (~250 words)

Full frontier model benchmark table with strategic analysis — not just numbers.

| Model | SWE-Bench | ARC-AGI-2 | Context | In $/1M | Out $/1M | Task Horizon | Status |
|-------|-----------|-----------|---------|---------|----------|-------------|--------|
| Opus 4.6 | 80.8% | ~65% | 1Mβ | $15.00 | $75.00 | 14.5h | Stable |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

Include:
- Pricing trend analysis (costs going up or down? margin implications?)
- Context window / task horizon comparison and what it means for InVelo engagements
- Deprecation notes
- Architecture observations (context compaction, extended thinking, etc.)

## 💡 Did You Know?

Deeper/more technical for this audience. Market data, historical context, counterintuitive signal.

**Example:**
> **Did You Know?** The $200M Pentagon contract Anthropic refused is 1.4% of their $14B
> annual revenue. The brand equity and user growth from refusing it has already exceeded
> the contract value by orders of magnitude.

## Section 3: Enterprise & Market Signals (~200 words)

Funding rounds, M&A, adoption data, analyst reports. Hard numbers sourced. Signal vs. noise.

- Funding: amounts, investors, valuations, strategic implications
- Adoption: user counts, enterprise licenses, market penetration
- Analyst takes: paraphrase with source
- M&A: acquisitions, talent moves, strategic hires

**Filter:** Only include signals that affect INT's competitive position, InVelo pricing,
or client conversations. Skip noise.

## Section 4: Agentic AI & Architecture (~150 words)

New agent frameworks, orchestration patterns, MCP ecosystem updates, production deployment
case studies.

- MCP ecosystem updates (new servers, protocol changes, security patches)
- Agent framework developments (task horizons, computer use, tool use patterns)
- Architecture patterns worth adopting or testing
- Production case studies relevant to INT's delivery model

**InVelo lens:** Frame architecture patterns as delivery model implications.

## 💬 Shareable One-Liner

Strategic, quotable, suitable for Kyle's LinkedIn or a client meeting opener.

**Example:**
> **This week in one line:** "Anthropic sacrificed $200M in government revenue and became
> the #1 app in America. 900 competitors' employees signed letters supporting them. The
> safety-as-growth thesis just got its first empirical proof point."

## Section 5: INT Positioning & Actions (~150 words)

How this week's news affects InVelo pricing, HumanX prep, client engagements, and
platform strategy. Named action items with owners.

**InVelo Impact**
- Pricing implications
- New use cases or engagement types created by this week's news
- HumanX talking point updates
- Competitive positioning changes

**Platform & Tools**
- Models/tools to evaluate
- Architecture decisions to make
- Cost optimization opportunities
- Integration priorities

## Section 6: Radar (~50 words)

3–5 bullets. Emerging patterns, early signals, things to watch. Forward-looking only.

> - **[Signal]:** [1–2 sentence description + why it matters]. Watch for: [specific trigger].

## 🎁 Free This Week  ← MANDATORY

A strategic analysis template, framework, prompt, or decision tool tied to this week's
top story. One per issue. Never skip. This is the highest-retention feature in The Prompt.

**Format:** Green "FREE" badge + the deliverable as a copy-paste block.

**Example:**
> 🎁 **Free This Week:** Use this prompt to generate a vendor lock-in risk assessment
> for any AI tool your clients are evaluating. Paste into Claude:
>
> *"You are an enterprise AI risk analyst. Analyze [AI vendor/tool] for vendor lock-in risk
> across these dimensions: (1) data portability, (2) API compatibility with alternatives,
> (3) pricing trajectory, (4) contractual constraints, (5) switching cost estimate.
> Output a risk rating (Low/Med/High) per dimension and a one-paragraph executive summary
> suitable for a client briefing."*
>
> *(Swap in the vendor name — takes 90 seconds, saves 2 hours of research per client.)*

**Rules:**
- Must be tied to this week's lead story
- Must produce something immediately useful in a client engagement or InVelo delivery
- Include an estimate of time saved
- Log in continuity file to maintain variety week to week

# Content Categories — The Prompt Newsletter

Six content categories define the newsletter's coverage universe. Every story must map to
at least one category. Stories that span multiple categories are stronger candidates for
lead positioning.

## Category 1: AI Tools & Product Releases

**What this covers:**
- New model releases (Claude, GPT, Gemini, open-source)
- Model updates, version bumps, deprecations
- New product features (Cowork, Code, plugins, integrations)
- Platform changes (console migrations, pricing changes, API updates)
- Developer tooling (Claude Code, Copilot, Cursor, MCP connectors)
- Consumer features (memory, import tools, mobile apps)

**Primary sources:**
- Anthropic blog/changelog: `anthropic.com/news`, `docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/about-claude/models`
- OpenAI blog: `openai.com/blog`, `platform.openai.com/docs`
- Google AI blog: `blog.google/technology/ai/`, Vertex AI release notes
- Developer community: Hacker News, GitHub release pages

**Tier routing:**
- Non-Tech: Only if it affects tools INT employees use daily. Translate to outcome.
- Tech Dept: All releases with version numbers, benchmarks, migration paths.
- AI Team: Releases + strategic analysis (why the vendor made this move, competitive implications).

**Search queries (rotate weekly):**
- `anthropic claude release [month] [year]`
- `openai gpt release [month] [year]`
- `google gemini update [month] [year]`
- `AI model benchmark [month] [year]`
- `claude code update`, `mcp new server release`

## Category 2: AI Strategy & Business Impact

**What this covers:**
- Enterprise AI adoption metrics (user counts, license numbers, spending data)
- Market sizing and analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
- CEO/exec statements about AI strategy
- AI ROI data and productivity studies
- Business model shifts (SaaS → usage-based, platform plays)
- AI workforce impact studies

**Primary sources:**
- Bloomberg, Reuters, WSJ, FT (business/financial coverage)
- Analyst firms (Gartner, Forrester, IDC, CB Insights)
- Company earnings calls and investor presentations
- McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte AI practice reports

**Tier routing:**
- Non-Tech: "Is AI delivering results?" framing. Plain-English adoption data.
- Tech Dept: Usually skip unless it affects tool selection or budgets.
- AI Team: Full market analysis. Funding signals, M&A, competitive positioning.

**Search queries:**
- `enterprise AI adoption [month] [year]`
- `AI market data funding [quarter] [year]`
- `AI productivity ROI study`
- `AI investment round [week]`

---

## Category 3: Security & AI Risk

**What this covers:**
- AI model vulnerabilities (jailbreaks, prompt injection, data extraction)
- AI-powered attack techniques (deepfakes, phishing, social engineering)
- AI supply chain security (model poisoning, distillation attacks)
- Safety research publications (alignment, red-teaming results)
- AI tool security features (Claude Code Security, vulnerability scanning)
- Responsible scaling policies and safety commitments

**Primary sources:**
- CISA advisories
- Vendor security blogs (Anthropic safety research, OpenAI Preparedness)
- Academic papers (arXiv, conference proceedings)
- Security research firms (Trail of Bits, NCC Group, Wiz)
- Bug bounty disclosures

**Tier routing:**
- Non-Tech: "Are we affected?" framing only. No technical details.
- Tech Dept: Technical advisories with severity, impact, and action items.
- AI Team: Strategic implications — vendor response quality, safety positioning, compliance patterns.

**Search queries:**
- `AI security vulnerability [month] [year]`
- `LLM jailbreak attack [month] [year]`
- `AI model safety research`
- `AI vendor security advisory`
- `anthropic safety research`, `openai preparedness`

## Category 4: Policy & Geopolitical Risk

**What this covers:**
- AI legislation and regulation (EU AI Act, state laws, executive orders)
- Government-AI vendor relationships (Pentagon, intelligence community, federal agencies)
- International AI competition (U.S.-China, export controls, trade policy)
- AI ethics and governance debates
- AI company policy positions (military use, surveillance, safety stances)
- Legal challenges (copyright, liability, employment)
- Sanctions, bans, supply chain designations

**Primary sources:**
- Lawfare, Brookings, RAND (policy analysis)
- Government publications (Federal Register, Congressional hearings)
- Reuters, AP, Politico (political coverage)
- Vendor policy statements and legal filings
- International: EU Commission, OECD, UN AI governance

**Tier routing:**
- Non-Tech: "Should we be worried?" framing. Client talking points essential.
- Tech Dept: Only if it affects tool availability or compliance requirements.
- AI Team: Full geopolitical analysis. Vendor risk implications. InVelo positioning.

**Search queries:**
- `AI regulation policy [month] [year]`
- `AI government [country] [month] [year]`
- `anthropic pentagon`, `openai government`
- `EU AI Act enforcement`
- `AI export controls China`

## Category 6: Future Watch (90-180 Day Signals)

**What this covers:**
- Model pre-announcements and roadmap leaks
- Beta program openings and testing signals
- Conference schedules and expected announcements
- Patent filings that signal product direction
- Leadership hires that signal strategy shifts
- Regulatory timelines (comment periods, enforcement dates)
- Funding rounds that signal future product investment

**Primary sources:**
- Vertex AI model logs (e.g., Claude 5 "Fennec" spotted)
- Beta program announcements
- Conference agendas (HumanX, Google I/O, WWDC, NeurIPS)
- LinkedIn job postings (what companies are hiring for)
- SEC filings (IPO signals, investment disclosures)

**Tier routing:**
- Non-Tech: "Coming Up" section. 1 sentence per item. What to expect.
- Tech Dept: "Watch Next Week/Month" section. Upcoming deprecations, maintenance windows.
- AI Team: "Radar" section. Full signal analysis with "watch for" triggers.

**Search queries:**
- `AI conference upcoming [quarter] [year]`
- `AI model roadmap [vendor] [year]`
- `claude 5 release date`, `gpt-6 announcement`
- `AI IPO filing [year]`

## Cross-Category Stories

The highest-impact stories span multiple categories. Examples:

| Story | Categories | Why it's strong |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Anthropic-Pentagon standoff | 4 (Policy) + 2 (Strategy) + 3 (Security) | Affects vendor trust, market position, and safety positioning simultaneously |
| Claude Code Security launch | 1 (Tools) + 3 (Security) | New tool that directly addresses security category |
| OpenAI $110B round | 2 (Strategy) + 6 (Future Watch) | Current market signal + future product implications |
| MCP standard adoption | 1 (Tools) + 6 (Future Watch) | Current tooling + architecture evolution |

When a story spans 3+ categories, it's almost always the lead for the AI Team edition
and likely appears in all 3 tiers.

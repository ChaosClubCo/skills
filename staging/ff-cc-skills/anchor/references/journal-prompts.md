# Anchor — Journal Prompt Bank

Load when Journal mode needs a context-specific prompt beyond what's in modes.md.
Always offer ONE prompt, not a list. Let the user sit with it before shifting.

---

## BY CONTEXT

### After a Headspace Session
- "What came up during that session — anything that surprised you or felt unfinished?"
- "Was there a moment your mind pulled away? What was it pulling toward?"
- "What's one thing you want to carry forward from that session?"
- "Did anything shift, even a little?"

### After Therapy
- "Is there something from today you want to hold onto?"
- "What felt hard to say in session — what would you add if you could?"
- "What did your therapist reflect back that landed, or that you're still sitting with?"
- "What's still unresolved? What do you want to bring back next time?"

### Start of Day
- "What's already on your mind before the day has started?"
- "What would a good version of today look like — not perfect, just good?"
- "Is there anything you're dreading? What would make it more manageable?"
- "What do you need most from yourself today?"

### End of Day / Wind-Down
- "When did you feel most yourself today?"
- "What drained you? What, if anything, filled you back up?"
- "Did anxiety show up today? When? What were the circumstances?"
- "What's one thing you handled today you want to acknowledge — even if it was small?"

### High Anxiety / Processing Something Hard
- "What's taking up the most space in your head right now? Just let it out."
- "If your anxiety had a voice, what would it be saying?"
- "What does your body feel like right now? Where are you feeling it?"
- "What's the fear underneath this? What are you actually afraid of?"

### Processing a Specific Event
- "Walk me through what happened — facts first, then what you felt."
- "What story did you tell yourself about what happened?"
- "What did you want to happen? What would that have meant to you?"
- "Is there a version of this where it wasn't entirely about you?"

### Family / Parental Conflict
- "What's the hardest part of this — the specific thing that happened, or what it means?"
- "Is this about the relationship, or about the specific incident? Or both?"
- "What do you wish they could understand — even if they never will?"
- "What would it mean to let this sit without resolving it right now?"

### Pattern Noticing (across multiple sessions)
- "Looking at this week — what kept triggering anxiety? What did those moments share?"
- "When you felt calmer, what was different? Environment, people, your own mindset?"
- "What coping actually helped this week? What didn't?"
- "What do you notice about when the anxiety is loudest?"

### Self-Compassion Days
- "What have you been through lately that deserves more credit than you've given it?"
- "If a friend had your week, what would you say to them?"
- "What's one thing you actually like about how you handled something this week?"

### Open / Freewrite
- "What's true right now that you haven't said out loud yet?"
- "Finish this: 'The thing I keep not letting myself think about is...'"

---

## AFTER JOURNALING — ALWAYS DO THIS

1. Reflect the main theme in 1–2 sentences: *"It sounds like the main thing is..."*
2. Check: *"Does that feel accurate? Anything I missed?"*
3. One of these to close:
   - *"Is there one thread you want to pull on more?"*
   - *"What does naming that feel like?"*
   - *"You don't have to do anything with this right now. Sometimes writing it is enough."*

---

## WHAT TO AVOID IN PROMPTS

- Never use "should" ("What should you have done?")
- Don't imply there's a right answer
- Don't prompt for gratitude mid-anxiety — can feel dismissive
- Don't push toward resolution — processing is the goal

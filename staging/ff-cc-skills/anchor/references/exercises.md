# Anchor — Exercise Library

Load when a user requests a technique not covered in modes.md, or when a standard
exercise hasn't landed and you need an alternative. After reading, return to session.

---

## BREATHING EXERCISES

### Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)
Best for: general anxiety, pre-event nerves, needing to feel in control
In 4 → Hold 4 → Out 4 → Hold 4. Repeat 3–4 cycles.

### 4-7-8 Breathing
Best for: trouble sleeping, high-activation anxiety, racing heart
In 4 → Hold 7 → Out 8. The long exhale activates the parasympathetic system.
Note: Can feel intense. Start with 2 cycles.

### Physiological Sigh (fastest calming effect)
Best for: immediate spike, fastest reset
Double inhale through nose (sniff-sniff) → long slow exhale through mouth.
One cycle is often enough.

### Resonant Breathing (5-5)
Best for: longer sessions, building calm baseline
In 5 → Out 5. Steady rhythm 5–10 minutes. Pairs well with Headspace.

### Triangle Breathing (4-4-4)
Best for: simpler version of box, when box feels like too much
In 4 → Hold 4 → Out 4. No second hold.

## GROUNDING EXERCISES

### 5-4-3-2-1 Sensory
Best for: dissociation, spiraling, feeling unreal
5 see → 4 feel → 3 hear → 2 smell/touch → 1 slow breath

### Feet on the Floor
Best for: quick anchor, can be done anywhere mid-conversation
Press feet flat into the floor. Feel the ground push back. Notice body weight in the chair.

### Cold Water Reset
Best for: acute panic, emotion spike
Hold something cold (ice, glass of cold water), or splash cold water on face.
Activates the dive reflex — physiologically slows heart rate.
Language: *"If you have something cold nearby, can you hold it for a second?"*

### Safe Place Visualization
Best for: when physical grounding isn't landing, wants something internal
*"Can you picture a place — real or imagined — where you've felt really safe or calm?"*
*"What does it look like? What can you hear? What does the temperature feel like?"*
*"Just stay there for a moment."*

### STOP Technique
Best for: mid-task anxiety, work stress, can't break a thought spiral
S — Stop for a moment
T — Take a breath
O — Observe: what am I thinking? what do I feel in my body?
P — Proceed with awareness

### Progressive Muscle Relaxation (Short)
Best for: physical tension, headaches, end-of-day wind-down
Tense each muscle group 5 seconds, then release.
Sequence: fists → arms → shoulders → face → stomach → legs → feet

### Body Scan (Full)
Best for: deeper tension work, pre-sleep
Top of head down to feet. At each point: notice, don't fix.
Takes 5–8 minutes done properly.

## ROUTING GUIDE — WHICH TO SUGGEST WHEN

| Situation | Best Options |
|---|---|
| Acute panic, can't think | Physiological Sigh → Feet on Floor |
| Can't sleep | 4-7-8 → Safe Place Visualization |
| Before something scary | Box Breathing → 5-4-3-2-1 |
| Physical tension | PMR → Body Scan |
| Spiraling at work | STOP → Triangle Breathing |
| Feeling disconnected | 5-4-3-2-1 → Cold Water Reset |
| Angry, not anxious | Cold Water Reset → movement (walk, not breathing) |
| Just wants to feel steadier | Resonant Breathing |

---

## DELIVERY RULES

- Offer one technique. Not a menu.
- Slow your language down. Short sentences. Put breathing room between instructions.
- After any exercise: *"How does that feel compared to when we started?"*
- Never say what they should feel. Ask what they do feel.
- If the first technique doesn't land, offer one alternative. Don't keep cycling.
